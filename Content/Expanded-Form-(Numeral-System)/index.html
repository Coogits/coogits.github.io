    Expanded Form (Numeral System)

    In Numeral System, Expanded Form (or Polynomial Form) is a method of representing numbers where it shows the number as the sum of its digits, each multiplied by the appropriate power of the base.

    Symbols

    • $N\quad$ Represent a number in base $b$.
    • $b\quad$ Represent the base of the number.
      • $b=2\quad$ Binary.
      • $b=10\quad$ Decimal.
    • $n\quad$ Represent the most significative digit position starting from the comma ($n\geq0$).
    • $-m\quad$ Represent the least significative digit position starting from the comma ($-m\leq0$).
      • $-m=0\quad$ No fractional part.
      • $-m<0\quad$ Is there a fractional part.
    • $i\quad$ Represent the digit's position.
      • $i\geq0\quad$ Corresponding to the integer part.
      • $i<0\quad$ Corresponding to the fractional part.
      • $i=0\quad$ Corresponding to the first digit after the comma.
    • $a_i\quad$ Represent the digit's value at position $i$ ($0\leq a_i< b$).
    • $b^i\quad$ Represent the positional weight associated with digit $a_i$.
    • $\displaystyle\sum_{i=-m}^na_i\cdot b^i\quad$ The Expanded Form is a Polynomial where
      • $a_i\cdot b^i\quad$ Are the Polynomial terms.

    Definition

    • Definition:
      The Expanded Form is a representation of the same number in a Polynomial form. $$N=\displaystyle\sum_{i=-m}^na_i\cdot b^i\qquad \begin{align} 0\leq a_i<b \\ -m<i<n \end{align}$$
    • Explicit:
      Expanding the summation: $$a_n\ldots a_0,a_{-1}\ldots a_{-m}= \underbrace{a_n\cdot b^{n}}+ \ldots+\underbrace{a_0\cdot b^{0}}+\underbrace{a_{-1}\cdot b^{-1}}+\ldots+ \underbrace{a_{-m}\cdot b^{-m}}$$

    Examples

    • Decimal:
      • $1872,4_{10}=1\cdot10^3+8\cdot10^2+7\cdot10^1+2\cdot10^0+4\cdot10^{-1}$
    • Binary:
      • $110,01_{2}=1\cdot2^{2}+1\cdot2^{1}+0\cdot2^{0}+0\cdot2^{-1}+1\cdot2^{-2}+=6,25$
