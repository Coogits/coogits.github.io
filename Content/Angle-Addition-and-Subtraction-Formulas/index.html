    Angle Addition and Subtraction Formulas

    In trigonometric's Theorems, the Angle Addition and Subtraction Formulas provide a way to express the sine, cosine, and tangent of the sum or difference of two angles in terms of their individual trigonometric functions. These formulas are essential for simplifying expressions, solving trigonometric equations, and deriving other trigonometric identities.

    • Sine Addition and Subtraction Formulas: $$\sin(\alpha\pm\beta)=\sin\alpha\cos\beta\pm\sin\beta\cos\alpha$$
    • Cosine Addition and Subtraction: $$\cos(\alpha\pm\beta)=\cos\alpha\cos\beta\mp\sin\alpha\sin\beta$$
    • Tangent Addition and Subtraction: $$\tan(\alpha\pm\beta)=\cfrac{\tan\alpha\pm\tan\beta}{1\mp\tan\alpha\tan\beta}$$

    Symbols

    • $\alpha\pm\beta\quad$ The sum or difference between two angles.
    • $\sin\quad$ Sine (Trigonometry)
    • $\cos\quad$ Cosine (Trigonometry)
    • $\tan\quad$ Tangent (Trigonometry)
    • $\pm\;\mp\quad$ respectively plus or minus and minus or plus, they are reciprocal in the formulas, so:
      • $\text{if }\pm=+\text{ then }\mp=-$
      • $\text{if }\pm=-\text{ then }\mp=+$

    Applications

    • Used to simplify complex trigonometric expressions.
    • Helps in solving trigonometric equations.
    • Useful in deriving double-angle and half-angle identities.
    • Widely applied in physics, engineering, and signal processing.
