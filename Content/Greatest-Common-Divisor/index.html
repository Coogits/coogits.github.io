    Greatest Common Divisor

    Greatest Common Divisor (GCD): also known as greatest common factor (GCF), in Algebra, is the largest positive integer that divides two or more integers without leaving a remainder. It is the greatest number that is a divisor of each of the given numbers.

    Notation

    • Greatest Common Divisor:
      The is represented as: $$\mbox{GCD}(a, b,\ldots)$$
      • Example
        • $\mbox{GCD}(12, 15)=3$

    Prime Factorization

    Step Prime Factorization Method Example
    $\mbox{GCD}(60,16,100)$
    1 Prime Factorization $\begin{array}{r\|l} 60 & 2 \\ 30 & 2 \\ 15 & 3 \\ 5 & 5 \\ 1 &\\\hline&2^2\cdot3\cdot5 \end{array}\qquad$ $\begin{array}{r\|l} 16 & 2 \\ 8 & 2 \\ 4 & 2 \\ 2 & 2 \\ 1 & \\\hline&2^4\end{array}\qquad$ $\begin{array}{r\|l} 100 & 2 \\ 50 & 2 \\ 25 & 5 \\ 5 & 5 \\ 1 & \\\hline&2^2\cdot5^2\end{array}$
    2 Multiply the common factors
    with the smaller exponent
    		 $2^2=4$
    $\mbox{GCD}(60,16,100)=4$

    Euclidean Algorithm

    The Euclidean Algorithm is a efficient algorithm.

    Step Euclidean algorithm Example 1 step 1 Example 1 step 2
    $\mbox{GCD}(60,16,100)$ $\mbox{GCD}(4,100)$
    1 Start with two integers $a$ and $b$, where
    $$a>b$$    		 $a=60;\;b=16$ $a=100, b=4$
    2 Divide $a$ by $b$ and calculate the remainder $r$ so that
    $$a=b\cdot q+r\quad 0\leq r<b$$    		 $60\div16=3\quad r=12$
    $60=16\cdot3+12$    		 $100\div4=25\quad r=0$
    $100=4\cdot25+0$
    3 Replace $a$ with $b$ and $b$ with $r$
    $$a=b \qquad b=r$$    		 $a=16;\;b=12$
    4 Repeat steps 2 and 3 until the remainder $r=0$. 2. $16\div12=1\quad r=4$
    $\quad16=12\cdot1+4$
    3. $a=12;b=4$
    2. $12\div4=3\quad r=0$
    $\quad12=4\cdot3+0$
    5 When $r=0$, the GCD is the last non-zero remainder. $\mbox{GCD}(60,16)=4$ $\mbox{GCD}(60,16,100)=4$
