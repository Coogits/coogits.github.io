    Mesolithic

    The Mesolithic (or Middle Stone Age) was a transitional period between the Paleolithic (Old Stone Age) and the Neolithic (New Stone Age). It lasted roughly from 10,000 BCE to 8,000 BCE, though the dates vary by region. This period saw significant changes in human lifestyle, technology, and environment.

    • Start: 10,000 BCE
    • End: 8,000 BCE

    Timeline

    Key Features

    • Transition from Hunting to Farming: People still hunted and gathered but also started experimenting with early farming and animal domestication.

    • Microliths (Small Stone Tools): Tools became more advanced and specialized, with small, sharp stone blades (microliths) fitted onto wood or bone handles to create arrows, harpoons, and sickles.

    • Fishing and New Food Sources: More reliance on fish and aquatic resources, with the invention of fishing tools like hooks and nets.

    • Domestication of Dogs: Dogs were one of the first animals to be domesticated, helping with hunting and protection.

    • Semi-Nomadic Lifestyle: Some groups started forming seasonal camps or small settlements, though full-time farming communities werenâ€™t yet common.

    • Early Pottery and Weaving: The first evidence of pottery, baskets, and woven fabrics appeared in some Mesolithic cultures.

    The Mesolithic ended as agriculture became widespread, leading into the Neolithic era, where permanent settlements and complex societies emerged.
