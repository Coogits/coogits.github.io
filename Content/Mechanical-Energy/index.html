    Mechanical Energy

    Mechanical energy is the sum of an object's kinetic energy and potential energy. It represents the energy associated with the motion and position of an object. In an isolated system (where no energy is lost to friction or other dissipative forces), mechanical energy is conserved.

    Symbols

    • $K\quad$ Kinetic Energy
    • $U\quad$ Potential Energy

    Formulas

    • Definition: $$E=K+U$$

    Mechanical Energy Conservation

    The Conservation of Mechanical Energy states that in an isolated system, the total mechanical energy (the sum of kinetic energy and potential energy) remains constant as long as no non-conservative forces (such as friction or air resistance) do work on the system.

    • Formula:
      if the Mechanical energy is constant $\Delta E=\text{constant}$, then: $$E_i=E_f\quad\implies\quad K_i+U_i=K_f+U_f$$
      • $E_i,E_f\quad$ Mechanical Energy initial and final, respectively.
      • $K_i,Kf\quad$ Kinetic Energy initial and final, respectively.
      • $U_i,U_f\quad$ Potential Energy initial and final, respectively.
