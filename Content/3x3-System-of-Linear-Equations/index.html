    3x3 System of Linear Equations

    A 3x3 System of Linear Equations consists in a system of three linear equations with three variables.

    Key Points

    • **Consists of three linear equations with three variables.
    • Only first-degree terms (no exponents, products of variables, or non-linear functions).
    • Exactly three equations and three unknowns, forming a solvable system under most conditions.

    Symbols

    • $x,y\quad$ are variables.
    • $a_1,b_1,c_1\quad$ are coefficient of the first equation
    • $a_2,b_2,c_2\quad$ are coefficient of the second equation
    • $a_3,b_3,c_3\quad$ are coefficient of the third equation
    • $d_1,d_2,d_3\quad$ are the constants

    Notation

    • 3x3 System of Linear Equations:
      It can be written in the general form: $$\begin{cases} a_1x+b_1y+c_1z=d_1 \\ a_2x+b_2y+c_2z=d_2 \\ a_3x+b_3y+c_3z=d_3\end{cases}$$

    Solutions

    Types of Solutions:

    • Unique Solution:
      The system has exactly one solution, where the lines represented by the equations intersect at a single point.
    • Infinitely Many Solutions:
      The two equations are dependent (represent the same line), and every point on the line is a solution.
    • No Solution:
      The two equations are inconsistent (represent parallel lines that never intersect), and there is no point of intersection.

    Solving

    Methods of Solving:

    • Graphically:
      Plot both equations on a graph and find the point of intersection (if any).
    • Substitution:
      Solve one equation for one variable and substitute into the second equation.
    • Elimination:
      Add or subtract the equations to eliminate one variable, then solve for the other.
    • Matrix Method:
      Represent the system as a matrix and solve using Gaussian elimination or matrix inversion.
