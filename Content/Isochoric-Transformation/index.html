    Isochoric Transformation

    An isochoric transformation (also called an isovolumetric or isochoric process) is a thermodynamic process in which volume remains constant while pressure and temperature change. Since the volume does not change, the system does no work ($W=0$), and any heat added or removed from the system directly affects its internal energy.

    Formulas

    • Isochoric Transformation: in the Ideal Gas Law $pV=nRT$ assuming the volume is constant $\Delta V=0$: $$\cfrac{p_0}{T_0}=\cfrac{p_1}{T_1}=\cfrac{Nk_B}{V} \quad\implies\quad \cfrac{p}{T}=\cfrac{Nk_B}{V}\begin{cases} p(T)=T\cfrac{Nk_B}{V} \\ T(p)=p\cfrac{Nk_B}{V}\end{cases}$$
    • Work done: Since the volume is constant, no work is done: $$W=0$$
    • Heat: from the first law $Q=\Delta U+W$, since work is zero the heat rise faster in isochoric transformations: $$Q=\Delta U$$
