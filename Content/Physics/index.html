    Methods

    • Experimentation: Conducting controlled experiments to observe physical phenomena and gather data.
    • Mathematical Modeling: Using mathematics to represent physical systems and derive predictions.
    • Theoretical Analysis: Developing and testing hypotheses and theories to explain physical observations.

    Objectives

    • To describe natural phenomena:
      Physics explains the causes and effects of physical events, from motion to energy transformations.
    • To discover universal laws:
      Physics aims to uncover the fundamental laws of nature that govern all aspects of reality, such as Newton's laws of motion, the laws of thermodynamics, and the principles of quantum mechanics.
    • To predict outcomes:
      By understanding these laws, physics allows us to predict the behavior of objects and systems under various conditions (e.g., predicting the motion of planets, the behavior of gases, or the energy output of a star).
