    Universal Gravitation Force

    The universal gravitational force is the attractive force that acts between any two masses in the universe due to their mass. It is always directed along the line connecting the centers of the two objects and follows an inverse square law, meaning that it decreases with the square of the distance between them.

    Formulas

    • Universal Gravitation Force: $$\vec F_{G}=G\cfrac{m_1m_2}{r^2}\hat{r}$$

    Symbols

    • $\vec F_{G}\quad$ Universal Gravitation Force
    • $G\quad$ is the Gravitational Constant $(6,674\,30\pm0,000\,15)\times10^{-11}\;\left(\cfrac{Nm^2}{kg^2}\right)$
    • $m_1,m_2\quad$ are the gravitational masses of the two objects,
    • $r\quad$ is the distance between their centers,
    • $\hat r\quad$ is the unit vector pointing from one mass to the other.

    Properties of Universal Gravitation Force

    • Conservative Force: The universal gravitational force is a conservative force, meaning the work done by this force in moving an object between two points depends only on the initial and final positions, not on the path taken.
    • Central Force: It is a central force, meaning the gravitational force between two masses acts along the line connecting the centers of the masses and is directed towards or away from each other.
    • Superposition Principle: The total gravitational force acting on an object is the vector sum of the individual gravitational forces from all other masses: $$F_{tot}=\displaystyle\sum_{i=0}F_i$$
