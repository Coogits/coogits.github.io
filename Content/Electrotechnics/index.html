    Electrotechnics

    Electrotechnics is the branch of engineering that deals with the study, design, and application of electrical systems and devices. It encompasses the generation, transmission, distribution, and utilization of electrical energy, as well as the development of electrical components such as circuits, transformers, motors, and control systems. Electrotechnics serves as the foundation for various fields, including power engineering, electronics, and telecommunications.

    Electronics

    Electronics, rooted in the physics of electron behavior, forms the theoretical foundation of electrotechnics. It governs how electrical charge moves through conductors, semiconductors, and components, shaping principles that drive everything from basic circuits to complex power systems.

    Electronic Component

    Electronic Component are physical component used to build Electrical Circuits:

    Passive Component

    Do not require an external power source

    • Resistor
    • Capacitor
    • Inductive Device

    Active Component

    Require an external power source to function

    • Transistor
    • Diode
    • Integrated circuit
