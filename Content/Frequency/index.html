    Frequency

    Frequency is the number of occurrences of a repeating event per unit of time. It is typically measured in hertz (Hz), where 1 Hz equals 1 cycle per second. In the context of waves, frequency refers to the number of complete oscillations (or cycles) a wave undergoes in one second.

    Symbols

    • $f\quad$ Frequency (unit: Hertz (Hz))
    • $T\quad$ Period (unit: seconds s). Represent the time taken for one complete cycle.

    Formulas

    • Frequency:
      The frequency $f$ of a wave is given by: $$f=\cfrac{1}{T}$$

    Contexts

    In electromagnetic waves

    In electromagnetic waves[1]Electromagnetic Wave, the frequency determines the wave's energy. Higher frequencies (e.g., X-rays, ultraviolet) have higher energy, while lower frequencies (e.g., radio waves) have lower energy.

    In telecommunications

    In telecommunications[2]Telecommunications, the frequency of the signal dictates the bandwidth available for communication, influencing the data transfer rate. Higher frequencies are used for high-speed data transmission but may have shorter ranges due to greater susceptibility to obstacles and attenuation.

    In sound waves

    In sound wave, the frequency is perceived as pitch. A higher frequency corresponds to a higher pitch (e.g., a whistle), and a lower frequency corresponds to a lower pitch (e.g., a drum beat).

    In Kinematics

    In Kinematics[3]Kinematics, specifically in Harmonic Motion[4]Simple Harmonic Motion and Circular Motion[5]Circular Motion, frequency refers to how often oscillations or rotations occur. In Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM), it determines the number of oscillations per unit of time, while in Circular Motion, it represents the number of full rotations per unit of time.
