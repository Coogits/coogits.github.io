    Factorization (Polynomial)

    Polynomial Factorization is the process of expressing a Polynomial as a product of simpler polynomials (or Monomials) that, when multiplied together, yield the original polynomial. This decomposition simplifies solving equations, analyzing polynomial behavior, and finding roots.

    Types of Factorizations

    Complete Factoring (Polynomial)

    This involves factoring a polynomial completely into a product of irreducible polynomials over a given number set (e.g., integers, real numbers).

    • Goal: Break the polynomial down as far as possible.
    • Example:
      $x^2-5x+6=x(x-5)+6$
