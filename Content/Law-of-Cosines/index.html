    Law of Cosines

    The Law of Cosines relates the sides and angles of a triangle in a way that generalizes the Pythagorean theorem. It is particularly useful for finding the third side of a triangle when two sides and the included angle are known, or for finding angles when all three sides are known.

    Symbols

    • $a,b,c\quad$ are the lengths of the sides of the triangle.
    • $\alpha,\beta,\gamma\quad$ are the angle opposite side of $a,b,c$, respectively.
    • $\cos\quad$ Cosine (Trigonometry)

    Formulas

    • Law of Cosines: Defined as: $$a^2=b^2+c^2-2bc\cdot\cos\alpha$$$$b^2=a^2+c^2-2ac\cdot\cos\beta$$$$c^2=a^2+b^2-2ab\cdot\cos\gamma$$

    Applications

    • Solving triangles when two sides and the included angle are known (SAS case).
    • Solving triangles when all three sides are known (SSS case).
    • Finding angles when all sides of the triangle are known.
