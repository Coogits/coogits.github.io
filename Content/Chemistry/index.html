    Chemistry

    Chemistry is the branch of science that focuses on the study of matter and the changes it undergoes.
    It explores the composition, structure, properties, and interactions of substances, providing insights into how matter behaves under different conditions.
    Chemistry bridges the gap between physics and biology, making it essential for understanding the natural world and solving practical problems in industry, medicine, and the environment.

    Matter

    Matter is anything that has mass and occupies space. It is the fundamental substance of the universe, existing in various forms that can change through chemical reactions.

    Element

    An element is a pure substance made of only one type of atom, characterized by its atomic number (the number of protons in its nucleus). Each element has unique properties.

    State of Matter

    Compound

    A compound is a substance formed when two or more elements chemically combine in fixed proportions. Examples include water (H₂O) and carbon dioxide (CO₂).

    Molecule

    A molecule is a group of two or more atoms bonded together. Molecules can consist of atoms of the same element (e.g., O₂) or different elements (e.g., H₂O).

    Mixtures and Solutions

    • Mixtures: Physical combinations of two or more substances, where each retains its own properties (e.g., sand and water).
    • Solutions: Homogeneous mixtures where one substance (solute) dissolves in another (solvent) (e.g., salt dissolved in water).

    Mixtures

    A mixture is a combination of two or more substances where each retains its own properties. Mixtures can be either homogeneous or heterogeneous.

    • Homogeneous Mixtures: The composition is uniform throughout (e.g., air, alloys).

    • Heterogeneous Mixtures: The components are not uniformly distributed (e.g., salad, sand and water).

    Solutions

    A solution is a homogeneous mixture in which one substance (the solute) dissolves in another (the solvent). Solutions can be found in different phases (liquid, solid, gas).

    • Solvent and Solute: The solvent is the substance that dissolves the solute, typically in liquid form (e.g., water is the solvent in a saltwater solution).

    • Concentration: The concentration of a solution refers to the amount of solute dissolved in a given amount of solvent.
