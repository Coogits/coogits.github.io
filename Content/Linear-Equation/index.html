    Linear Equation

    Linear Equation is a equation where the unknown variable is raised to the first power (i.e., no exponents higher than one).

    • Key Points
      • Degree: The highest exponent of any variable is 1.
      • Solutions: If the solution is determinate, it is unique (1 solution).

    Symbols

    • $a_1,a_2,\ldots,a_n\quad$ are coefficients.
    • $x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_n\quad$ are variables.
    • $c\quad$ is constant

    Equation

    • Linear Equation:
      A general representation of a generic equation can be written as: $$a_1x_1+a_2x_2+\ldots+a_nx_n=c$$
    • Linear Equation Standard form:
      A linear equation is in standard form when is represented as: $$ax+by=c \qquad a\neq0,b\neq0$$

    Linear Equation as Line

    A linear equation can be represented as a Line (Analytic Geometry) in their forms:

    • Standard Form (Line): $$ax+by+c=0$$
    • Slope-intercept form (Line): $$y=m x+q$$
    • Point-slope form (Line): $$y-y_1=m(x-x_1)$$

    Resolution of a Linear Equation

    The process of solving a linear equation consists of isolating the unknown variable by systematically applying the Properties of Equality to both sides of the equation, ensuring the equality remains valid.

    Key Steps:

    1. Simplify both sides by combining like terms and eliminating parentheses if necessary.
    2. Isolate the variable by applying inverse operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division).
    3. Verify the solution by substituting it back into the original equation.

    Properties:

    • Graph:
      In two dimensions $n=2$, it represents a straight line; in three dimensions ($n=3$), it represents a plane.
    • Solutions:
      A linear equation can have one solution, infinitely many solutions, or no solution depending on the number of equations and their relationships.
