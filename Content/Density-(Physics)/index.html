    Density (Physics)

    Density is a quantifiable physical property of matter that measures the mass per unit volume of a substance.
    Density helps determine how "heavy" a material is for a given volume, and it plays a key role in phenomena like buoyancy and fluid flow.

    Symbols

    • $\rho\quad$ Density (unit: $\cfrac{kg}{m^3}$)
    • $M\quad$ Mass (unit: kilogram ($kg$))
    • $V\quad$ Volume (unit: cubic meter ($m^3$))

    Formulas

    • Density: $$\rho=\cfrac{M}{V}$$

    Density Types

    • Linear Density: $$\cfrac{M}{L}=\cfrac{kg}{m}$$
    • Superficial Density: $$\cfrac{M}{S}=\cfrac{kg}{m^2}$$
    • Volumetric Density: $$\cfrac{M}{V}=\cfrac{kg}{m^3}$$

    Properties

    • Intensive Quantity: does not depend on the size or the amount of matter in the system.
