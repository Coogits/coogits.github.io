    Voltage

    Voltage, also known as electric potential difference, is the amount of energy per unit charge required to move a charge between two points in an electric field.
    It represents the difference in electric potential between those points and determines the ability to drive electric current in a circuit.

    Symbols

    • $\Delta V\quad$ Voltage expressed as the difference of Electric Potential (unit: Volt (V))
    • $V_{AB}\quad$ Voltage expressed as the difference of Electric Potential between two points A,B.
    • $\Delta U\quad$ Difference in Electric Potential Energy (unit: Joule (J))
    • $W\quad$ Work of the Electric Force (unit: Joule (J))
    • $Q\quad$ source Charge (unit: Coulombs (C))
    • $q\quad$ test Charge (unit: Coulombs (C))
    • $\epsilon_0\quad$ vacuum permittivity, a fundamental constant ($\epsilon_0\approx 8,854\cdot10^{-12}\;\frac{C^2}{N\cdot m^2}$)

    Formulas

    • Voltage and Energy Relationship:
      A notation used to express the difference in Electric Potential: $$\Delta{V}=\cfrac{\Delta{U}}{q}=\cfrac{-W}{q}$$
    • Voltage as Potential Difference:
      The potential difference (voltage) between two points in an electric field represents the work needed to move a unit charge from one point to another: $$V_{AB}=V_B-V_A$$
    • Voltage Due to a Point Charge:
      For a charge $Q$ at distances $r_A$ and $r_B$, the potential difference between two points A and B due to a point charge: $$\Delta V=\cfrac{Q}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\left(\cfrac{1}{r_A}-\cfrac{1}{r_B}\right)$$

    Properties

    • Electric Potential vs. Voltage:
      Electric Potential and Voltages are different concepts
      • Electric Potential ($V$): Absolute potential energy per unit charge at a point.
      • Voltage ($\Delta V$): Difference in electric potential between two points.
      • Relationship: $$\Delta V=V_B-V_A$$
