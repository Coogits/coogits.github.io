    Work (Physics)

    Work is done when a force is applied to an object, and the object moves in the direction of the applied force. The work is the energy transferred to the object by the force.

    Symbols

    • $W\quad$ Work (unit: Joule ($J=N\cdot m$)
    • $\vec F\quad$ Force (Physics)
    • $\vec s\quad$ Displacement
    • $F(x)\quad$ one-dimensional force that is a function of position
    • $x_0,x_1\quad$ path from $x_0$ to $x_1$

    Formulas

    • Definition with constant force:
      the work $W$ of a constant force $\vec F$ in a straight displacement $\vec s$ is the Dot Product between the force vector and the displacement vector: $$\begin{align}W&=\vec{F}\cdot\vec{s} \\ &=||\vec{F}||\cdot||\vec{s}||\cdot\cos{\alpha}\end{align}$$
    • Work by Integral:
      The work done by a non-constant force is calculated using Integral Calculus. This formula is used when the force varies along the path (e.g., a spring force or gravitational force), and the displacement is along a straight line. It integrates the force over a path from $x_0,x_1$: $$W=\displaystyle\int_{x_0}^{x_1}F(x)dx$$
    • General Definition:
      with any kind of force and trajectory. It is used when the force can have any direction and the trajectory can be arbitrary (not necessarily straight): $$W=\displaystyle\int_A^B\vec{F}\cdot\vec{s}$$

    Work's Sign

    • Positive Work
      occurs when the force and displacement are in the same direction (e.g., pushing a box across the floor).
    • Negative Work
      occurs when the force and displacement are in opposite directions (e.g., friction opposing motion).

    Work-Energy Theorem

    • Work-Energy Theorem
      The work done on an object is equal to the change in its kinetic energy: $$W=\Delta{K}=\frac{1}{2}mv_f^2-\frac{1}{2}mv_i^2$$
      • Where
        • $\Delta K\quad$ is the Kinetic Energy
