    Gravitational Field

    The gravitational field is a vector field that describes the gravitational force exerted on a mass at any point in space. It represents the influence of gravitational forces in a particular region due to a source mass or masses. The gravitational field at a point in space is defined as the gravitational force experienced by a unit test mass placed at that point.

    Formulas

    • Gravitational Field: Mathematically, the gravitational field at a point is given by: $$\vec{g}=\cfrac{\vec{F_G}}{m}$$
    • Gravitational Field From Gravitational Force (Generic Form): The gravitational field at a point at a distance $r$ from a mass $M$: $$\vec g=-G\cfrac{M}{r^2}\hat{r}$$
    • Gravitational Field From Gravitational Force (Vector Form): Expressed in vector form with position vectors of the test and source masses: $$\vec g=GM\cfrac{\vec{r_m}-\vec{r_M}}{||\vec{r_m}-\vec{r_M}||^3}$$

    Symbols

    • $\overrightarrow{g}\quad$ Campo Gravitazionale
    • $\vec{F_G}\quad$ is the Universal Gravitation Force acting on a test mass $m$: $\vec F_{G}=G\cfrac{mM}{r^2}\hat{r}$
    • $m\quad$ Gravitational Mass of the test body,
    • $M\quad$ Gravitational Mass of the source body,
    • $r,\hat{r},\vec{r}\quad$ distance, unit vector and vector, respectively, from $M$ to $m$.
    • $G\quad$ Gravitational Constant,
    • $\vec{r_M}\quad$ is the position vector of the source mass $M$,
    • $\vec{r_m}\quad$ is the position vector of the test mass $m$,

    Properties

    • Gravitational Field on Earth: At sea level on Earth, the gravitational field is given by:: $$\vec{g}=\cfrac{\vec{F}}{m}=\cfrac{m\vec{g_t}}{m}=\vec{g_t}\simeq9.81\frac{m}{s^2}$$
    • Superposition Principle: the total gravitational field at a point is the vector sum of the gravitational fields created by each mass individually: $$\vec g_{tot}=\displaystyle\sum_{i=0}\vec g_i$$
