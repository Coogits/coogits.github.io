    Equation

    Equation Is a mathematical statement that asserts the equality of two expressions, connected by an equals sign ($=$).

    Symbols and Terminology

    • $A(x),B(y)\quad$ Equal terms: Are expressions that can involve Polynomial, functions, or other mathematical operations;
    • $A(x)\quad$ Left-hand side (LHS) of the equation is how the polynomial at the left is called.
    • $B(x)\quad$ Right-hand side (RHS) of the equation is how the polynomial at the right is called.

    Notation

    • Generic equation:
      An equation can be represented as follow, where $A(x),B(y)$ are generic mathematical expression: $$A(x)=B(y)$$
    • Standard Form (Generic Equation):
      The Standard Form (Algebra) of an equation refers to a conventional way of writing it that follows a structured and widely accepted format: $$F(x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_n)=0$$
      • $F(x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_n)\quad$ is a function of the variables often expressed in an ordered and simplified manner.

    Equation types

    • Polynomial Equation
      Involves terms with variables raised to whole-number exponents.
    • Linear Equation
      A first-degree equation representing a straight line.
    • Quadratic Equation
      A second-degree polynomial equation (ax² + bx + c = 0).
    • Exponential Equation
      Variables appear as exponents (a^x = b).
    • Logarithmic Equation
      Involves logarithms (log_b(x) = y).
    • Rational Equation
      Contains fractions with polynomials in the numerator and denominator.
    • Irrational Equation
      Includes radicals with variables (√x + 3 = 5).
    • Diophantine Equation
      Integer-only solutions, often in number theory.
    • Differential Equations
      Involves derivatives, describing change over time.

    Existence Conditions

    The classification of solutions to an equation based on its domain can be described as follows:

    • Impossible Equation:
      An equation that has no solutions within the set of real numbers: $$\nexists x \in \mathbb{R}$$
    • Determinate Equation:
      An equation with a finite number of specific solutions: $$\exists x \in \mathbb{R}$$
    • Indeterminate Equation: An equation that is true for all real numbers: $$\forall x\in\mathbb{R}$$

    Properties of Equality

    Properties of Equality are the rules that preserve the equality of an equation while transforming.
