    Standard Form (Quadratic Equation)

    The Standard Form of a quadratic equation is a representation of a polynomial equation of degree 2.

    Key Properties

    • Descending Order: The terms are written in order of decreasing exponent (degree) of the variable.

    Symbols and Terminology

    • $ax^2\quad$ The quadratic term.
    • $bx\quad$ The linear term.
    • $a\quad$ Leading coefficient, must be non-zero ($a\neq0$) otherwise the equation cannot be quadratic.
    • $c\quad$ The constant term
    • $x_1,x_2\quad$ roots or solutions of the equation, determined using the Discriminant Formula:
    • $\Delta\quad$ Discriminant, defined $\Delta=b^2-4ac$.

    Notation

    • Standard Form:
      expressed as: $$ax^2\pm bx \pm c=0 \qquad a\neq0$$

    Resolution

    • Quadratic Formula:
      Can be Applied to a Quadratic standard form (or that can be converted into) can retrieve the roots of the equation. $$x_{1,2}=\cfrac{-b\pm\sqrt{\Delta}}{2a}\qquad\Delta=b^2-4ac$$

    Properties

    • Graphic Representation:
      A quadratic equation in standard form can represent a Parabola (Analytic Geometry), which is a conic section, when rewritten as a function by introducing an extra variable: $$y=ax^2+bx+c$$ This transformation allows the equation to describe the shape of a parabola in the Cartesian plane.
    • Vieta's Formulas:
      Given a quadratic equation in standard form, the sum and product of its roots can be quickly calculated from its coefficients: $$x_1+x_2=-\cfrac{b}{a}$$ $$x_1x_2=\cfrac{c}{a}$$

    Applications

    • Parabola (Analytic Geometry): Graphic representation of the Standard form as a function.
