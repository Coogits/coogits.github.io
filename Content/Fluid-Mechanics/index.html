    Fluid Mechanics

    Fluid Mechanics is the branch of physics that studies the behavior of fluids (liquids and gases) and their interactions with solid boundaries. It encompasses both the fluid's behavior at rest (fluid statics) and in motion (fluid dynamics). Fluid mechanics is essential in understanding phenomena such as pressure, flow, turbulence, viscosity, and the forces that act on fluids. It plays a key role in various fields such as engineering, meteorology, medicine, and oceanography.
