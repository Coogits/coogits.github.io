    Calculus

    Calculus is a branch of mathematics that studies continuous change, focusing on two main operations: differentiation and integration. It provides powerful tools for understanding and modeling how quantities change over time, space, or other variables.

    Fundamental Theorem

    • Statement:
      The fundamental theorem of calculus establishes the inverse relationship between differentiation and integration: $$F'(x)=f(x)$$

    Differential Calculus

    Examines how functions change at an instant, using derivatives.

    • Derivative
    • Antiderivative

    Integral Calculus

    Explores the accumulation of quantities and areas, using integrals.

    • Integral
    • Indefinite Integral

    Multivariable Calculus

    Extends calculus to functions of multiple variables, involving partial derivatives and multiple integrals.

    Vector Calculus

    Deals with vector fields and operations like gradient, divergence, and curl.

    Calculus of Variations

    Studies optimization problems where the goal is to find a function that minimizes or maximizes a functional.

    Complex Analysis

    Extends calculus to functions of complex numbers, including contour integration and holomorphic functions.

    History and Development

    Calculus was independently developed by Isaac Newton and Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz in the late 17th century. Newton applied calculus to physics, formulating his laws of motion and gravitation, while Leibniz contributed to its formal notation, much of which is still used today.

    Applications of Calculus

    • Physics – Describes motion, forces, and energy (e.g., Newtonian mechanics, electromagnetism).
    • Engineering – Used in designing structures, electrical circuits, and control systems.
    • Economics – Models optimization problems, cost functions, and marginal analysis.
    • Biology – Analyzes population growth, rates of biochemical reactions, and medical imaging.
    • Computer Science – Helps in algorithms, graphics, machine learning, and simulations
