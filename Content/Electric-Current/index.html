    Electric Current

    Electric current is the rate of flow of electric charge through a conductor or circuit. It quantifies how much charge passes a given point per unit time and is a fundamental concept in both electricity and electronics.

    Symbols

    • $I\quad$ is the electric current (unit: Ampere (A)
    • $Q\quad$ is the Electric Charge (unit: Coulomb (C)
    • $t\quad$ is the Time (Physics) (unit: second (s)

    Formulas

    • Mathematical Definition:
      The electric current $I$ is defined as the rate of change of charge with respect to time: $$I(t)=\cfrac{dQ(t)}{dt}$$
    • With constant current:
      When the current is constant, the average current is given by: $$I=\cfrac{\Delta{Q}}{\Delta{t}}=\cfrac{Q_1-Q_0}{t_1-t_0}\qquad$$
    • From the Ohm's Laws:
      According to the first Ohm's Law ($\Delta{V}=RI$), the current can be expressed as: $$I=\cfrac{\Delta V}{R}$$

    Unit of the Electric Current

    • SI Unit: The International System's Unit of the Electric Current is the Ampere: $$I=\left(\cfrac{C}{s}\right)=A$$
