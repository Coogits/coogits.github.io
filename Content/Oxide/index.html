    Oxide

    An oxide is a binary compound that consists of oxygen combined with another element. In most oxides, oxygen has an oxidation state of âˆ’2.

    Symbols

    • $E_x\quad$ the Chemical Element bonded to oxygen (metal or non-metal), e.g. $Na, C,\ldots$
    • $x\quad$ the number of atoms of element $E$ e.g. $Na_2, C_3,\ldots$
    • $O_y\quad$ the Oxygen (in oxide have always Oxidation State = -2)
    • $y\quad$ the number of oxygen atoms
    • $Me\quad$ Metal
    • $nMe\quad$ Nonmetal

    Formula

    • Oxide Formula:
      Oxide is a molecule where one of it's element is Oxygen: $$E_xO_y$$

    Types of Oxides

    Basic Oxide

    A basic oxide is an oxide that reacts with water to form a base (typically a hydroxide) or reacts with acids to form salt and water.

    Basic oxides are usually formed by Metals, especially those from Group 1 and Group 2 of the periodic table.

    • Formula
      can be used the cross formula: $$Me_2+O_n\to\text{Basic Oxide}$$ where
      • $Me_2\quad$ is the Metal and the pedice 2 si the Oxygen oxidation number
      • $n\quad$ is the oxidation number of the Metal

    Acid Oxide

    An acidic oxide (or acid anhydride) is an oxide that reacts with water to form an acid, or reacts with a base to form salt and water.

    Acidic oxides are usually formed by Nonmetals, especially from the upper right of the periodic table (like sulfur, nitrogen, carbon, phosphorus).
