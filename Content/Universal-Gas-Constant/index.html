    Universal Gas Constant

    The universal gas constant (often denoted by $R$) is a fundamental physical constant that appears in the ideal gas law, which describes the behavior of ideal gases. It serves as a bridge between the macroscopic properties of a gas (like pressure, volume, and temperature) and the microscopic properties (such as the number of molecules).

    Formulas

    • Definition in Terms of Microscopic Constants: can be defined in terms of the Boltzmann constant and Avogadro's number as: $$R=N_Ak_B\simeq8,31\;\frac{J}{mol\cdot K}$$
    • Numerical Value: is approximately: $$R\simeq8.314462618\;\frac{J}{mol\cdot K}$$

    Symbols

    • $N_A\quad$ Avogadro Constant ( $N_A=6,022\cdot10^{23}\,mol^{-1}$ )
    • $k_B\quad$ Boltzmann Constant ( $k_B\simeq1,38\cdot 10^{-23}\frac{J}{K} )$

    Properties

    • Universality:
      The term "universal" indicates that $R$ has the same value for all ideal gases, regardless of their chemical nature. This makes it a central constant in both thermodynamics and statistical mechanics.
