    Isobaric Transformation

    An isobaric transformation is a thermodynamic process in which the pressure remains constant while other state variables, such as volume and temperature, change.

    Formulas

    • Isobaric Transformation of a Ideal Gas: From the Ideal Gas Law and the first law of Gay-Lussac arrest $\cfrac{V}{T}=\text{constant}$: $$\cfrac{V}{T}=\cfrac{Nk_B}{p}\begin{cases} V(T)=T\cfrac{Nk_B}{p} \\ T(V)=V\cfrac{Nk_B}{p}\end{cases}$$
    • Heat exchanged of a Ideal Gas in a Isobaric Transformation: From the first law of thermodynamics: $$Q=\Delta U+W$$
    • The work done by the gas during expansion or compression is: $$W=p\Delta V=nR\Delta V$$

    Symbols

    • $p\quad$ Pressure constant in the Isobaric transformation ( unit: Pascale (Pa) )
    • $V\quad$ Gas Volume ( unit: cubic meters ($m^3$) )
    • $T\quad$ Absolute Temperature ( unit: Kelvin (K) )
    • $N\quad$ Number of gas molecules
    • $n\quad$ Number of Mole (unit: mole (mol) )
    • $k_B\quad$ Boltzmann Constant ( $k_B\simeq1,38\cdot 10^{-23}\frac{J}{K} )$
    • $R\quad$ is the Universal Gas Constant ( $R=N_Ak_B\simeq8,31\frac{J}{mol\cdot K}$ )
    • $Q\quad$ Heat exchanged in the process
