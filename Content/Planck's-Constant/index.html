    Planck's Constant

    Planck’s constant is a fundamental physical constant that describes the quantum of action in quantum mechanics.
    It relates the energy of a photon to the frequency of its associated electromagnetic wave.

    Symbols

    • $h$ is Planck’s constant $h = 6.62607015 \times 10^{-34}\ \text{J·s}$
    • $\hbar$ is the Reduced Planck's constant $\hbar=\cfrac{h}{2\pi}$
    • $E$ is the energy of a photon (unit: joules, J)
    • $f$ is the Frequency of the electromagnetic wave (unit: hertz, Hz)

    Formula

    • Planck's constant
      Planck's constant is defined as: $$E=hf$$
    • Value:
      Planck's constant value: $$h = 6.62607015 \times 10^{-34}\ \text{J·s}$$

    Reduced Planck's constant

    The reduced Planck constant, denoted by ℏ (h-bar), is a fundamental constant used extensively in quantum mechanics.

    • Formula:
      It is derived by dividing Planck’s constant (ℎ) by 2π, making it more convenient for equations involving circular motion or waveforms: $$\hbar=\cfrac{h}{2\pi}$$
    • Value
      Reduced Planck's constant value: $$\hbar = 1.054571817 \times 10^{-34}\ \text{J·s}$$

    Properties

    • Quantum Effect:
      It defines the scale at which quantum effects become significant.
    • Quantum Physics:
      Central to quantum physics, especially in phenomena like the photoelectric effect and quantum energy levels.
      • Appears in many core equations, such as Heisenberg's uncertainty principle and Schrödinger’s equation.
