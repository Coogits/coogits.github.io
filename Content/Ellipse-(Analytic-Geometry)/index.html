    Ellipse

    An ellipse is the set of all points in a plane where the sum of the distances to two fixed points, called foci, is constant. It is a type of conic section formed by the intersection of a plane and a cone at an angle less than that of the cone's side but not parallel to its base.

    Symbols

    • $(x_0,y_0)\quad$ Origin
    • $a\quad$ Horizontal axis
    • $b\quad$ Vertical axis

    Standard Equation of the Ellipse

    • Horizontal major axis (a>b):
      with origin at $(x_0,y_0)$ $$\cfrac{(x-x_0)^2}{a^2}+\cfrac{(y-y_0)^2}{b^2}=1$$
    • Vertical major axis (b>a):
      with origin at $(x_0,y_0)$: $$\cfrac{(x-x_0)^2}{b^2}+\cfrac{(y-y_0)^2}{a^2}=1$$

    Parameters Formulas

    Name Horizontal major axis Vertical major axis
    Foci $F_{1,2}(x_0\pm c, y_0)$ $F_{1,2}(x_0, y_0\pm c)$
    Eccentricity $e=\cfrac{c}{a}$ $e=\cfrac{c}{b}$
    Distance foci-origin $c=\sqrt{a^2-b^2}$ $c=\sqrt{b^2-a^2}$
