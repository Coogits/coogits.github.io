    Principles of Dynamics

    Principles of Dynamics refer to the fundamental laws and concepts that govern the motion of objects under the influence of forces. These principles form the foundation of classical mechanics.

    • inertial reference frames only:
      Principles of Dynamics are strictly valid only in inertial reference frames (frames that are either at rest or moving at a constant velocity relative to an absolute space).

    Newton's Laws of Motion

    First Law (Inertia)

    • Inertia
      An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion at a constant velocity unless acted upon by an external force. $$\sum\overrightarrow{F}=0\implies \Delta\overrightarrow{v}=0$$

    Second Law

    • Force and Acceleration
      The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass. $$\sum\overrightarrow{F}=m\overrightarrow{a}=\cfrac{d\overrightarrow{p}}{dt}\qquad\overrightarrow{p}=m\overrightarrow{v}$$

    Third Law

    • Action and Reaction
      For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. $$\overrightarrow{F}_{AB}=-\overrightarrow{F}_{BA}$$

    Conservation of Momentum:

    The total momentum of a closed system remains constant if no external forces act on it.

    Conservation of Energy:

    In a closed system, the total energy (kinetic + potential) remains constant, unless work is done by external forces.
