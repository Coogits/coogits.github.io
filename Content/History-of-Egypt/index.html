    History of Egypt

    • Start: c. 5.500 BCE
    • End: 641 CE

    Timeline

    Prehistory

    Neolithic

    Predynastic Period of Egypt

    (c. 5500–3100 BCE)

    • Development of agriculture, villages, and early religious practices.
    • Upper and Lower Egypt emerge as distinct cultural regions.
    • Ends with the unification of Egypt by Narmer (Menes).

    Ancient Egypt

    • Start: c. 3.150 BCE
    • End: 30 BCE

    Bronze Age

    Early Dynastic Period of Egypt

    (c. 3150–2686 BCE, Dynasties 1–2)

    Old Kingdom of Egypt

    (c. 2686–2181 BCE, Dynasties 3–6)

    First Intermediate Period of Egypt

    (c. 2181–2055 BCE, Dynasties 7–11)

    Middle Kingdom of Egypt

    (c. 2055–1650 BCE, Dynasties 11–13)

    Second Intermediate Period of Egypt

    (c. 1650–1550 BCE, Dynasties 14–17)

    New Kingdom of Egypt

    (c. 1550–1070 BCE, Dynasties 18–20)

    Iron Age

    Third Intermediate Period of Egypt

    (c. 1070–664 BCE, Dynasties 21–25)

    Late Period of Egypt

    (c. 664–332 BCE, Dynasties 26–31)

    Classical Age

    Ptolemaic Period of Egypt

    (332–30 BCE)

    Roman Egypt

    (30 BCE–641 CE)

    Medieval Age

    Early Middle Ages

    Byzantine Egypt

    (30 BCE–641 CE)

    • Arab conquest in 641 CE marks the end of ancient Egyptian civilization.
