    Potential Energy

    Potential Energy is the energy stored in an object due to its position or configuration relative to other objects or fields (e.g., gravitational, elastic, etc.). It is the energy that has the potential to do work when the object undergoes a change in position or state.

    Symbols

    • $\Delta U\quad$ is the change in potential energy,
    • $W\quad$ Work (Physics) done by the force that caused the change in position (or state).
    • $U_f\quad$ is the potential energy at the final position,
    • $U_i\quad$ is the potential energy at the initial position,

    Formula

    • Definition:
      The change in potential energy $\Delta U$ of an object is the difference in its potential energy between two positions or states. It is given by: $$\Delta U=U_f-U_i=-W$$

    Properties

    • Mechanical Energy's component:
      Its a component of the Mechanical Energy: $$E=K+U$$

    Unit

    • SI Units:
      The SI Unit is Joule: $$U=J$$

    Potential Energy Forms

    • Gravitational Potential Energy:
      The energy stored due to an object's position in a gravitational field: $$U_g=mg(y_f-y_i)$$
      • $U_g\quad$ is the gravitational potential energy,
      • $m\quad$ is the object's mass,
      • $g\quad$ is the acceleration due to gravity,
      • $(y_f-y_i)\quad$ is the height above a reference point (usually the ground).
    • Elastic Potential Energy:
      The energy stored in an object when it is stretched or compressed, like in a spring. It is given by Hooke's Law for an ideal spring: $$U_e=\frac{1}{2}kx^2$$
      • $U_e\quad$ is the elastic potential energy,
      • $k\quad$ is the spring constant (a measure of stiffness),
      • $x\quad$ is the displacement from the spring's equilibrium position.
