    Voltage Supply

    A voltage supply is a source that provides electrical potential difference (voltage[1]Voltage A physical quantity that measures the electrical potential difference between two points, typically the positive and negative poles.) to a circuit, enabling the flow of electric current[2]Electric Current A physical quantity that measures the rate of charge flow between two points in a circuit.. It serves as the power source for electronic and electrical systems

    • Ideal Voltage Source
    • Ideal Current Source
    • Battery Source

    Types of Voltage Supplies

    Based on Current Type

    • Direct Current (DC) Supply:
      Provides a constant voltage (e.g., batteries, DC power adapters).
    • Alternating Current (AC) Supply:
      Voltage varies periodically (e.g., mains electricity, transformers).

    Based on Regulation

    • Unregulated Power Supply:
      Output voltage fluctuates with load changes (e.g., simple rectifiers).
    • Regulated Power Supply:
      Maintains a stable output voltage (e.g., linear and switching regulators).

    Common Sources

    • Batteries:
      Provide DC voltage for portable devices.
    • Power Adapters:
      Convert AC mains to a usable DC voltage.
    • Generators & Alternators:
      Generate AC or DC for large-scale applications.
