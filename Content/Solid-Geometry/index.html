    Solid Geometry

    Solid geometry is the branch of geometry that deals with the study of three-dimensional shapes (also called solids) and their properties, such as volume, surface area, edges, vertices, and faces. Unlike plane geometry, which focuses on flat, two-dimensional figures, solid geometry explores objects with depth, width, and height.

    Properties Analyzed

    • Volume: The measure of space enclosed within the solid
    • Surface Area (Solid Geometry) The total area of the outer surface of the solid.
    • Faces, Edges, and Vertices:
      • Example: A cube has 6 faces, 12 edges, and 8 vertices.

    Shapes Studied

    • Polyhedron (solids with flat polygonal faces):
      • Examples: Cube, prism, pyramid.
    • Curved solids:
      • Examples: Sphere, cylinder, cone.

    Applications

    • Found in architecture, engineering, physics, and 3D modeling.
