    Pressure

    Pressure is defined as the force exerted per unit area on the surface of an object. It is a scalar quantity that measures the intensity of the force acting on an area. In the context of fluids, pressure arises due to the continuous collisions of fluid molecules with the surface of objects immersed in the fluid.

    Formulas

    • Pressure: Describes the relation between force and area: $$p=\cfrac{F_{\perp}}{A}$$
    • Hydrostatic pressure: It is derived from the principle of fluid statics, and it describes the pressure exerted by a fluid at a certain depth $h$: $$p(h)=\rho gh$$
    • Ideal gas law in terms of density: Relates the pressure of a gas to its density and temperature: $$p=\rho RT$$
    • barometric formula (use for Atmospheric Pressure): Describes how atmospheric pressure changes with altitude: $$p(h)=p_0e^{\frac{-mgh}{kT}}$$
    • Pressure exerted by a gas: Derived from the kinetic theory of gases, this equation calculates the pressure based on the number of gas molecules and their average velocity: $$p=\frac{1}{3}\cfrac{Nm}{V}v^2$$

    Symbols

    • $p\quad$ Pressure in Pascal
    • $F_{\perp}\quad$ is the force applied perpendicular to the surface,
    • $A\quad$ is the area over which the force is distributed.
    • $h\quad$ is the depth or height of the fluid above the point where the pressure is measured.
    • $\rho\quad$ is the Density (Physics) of the fluid,
    • $g\quad$ is the acceleration due to gravity,
    • $R\quad$ is the specific gas constant (which depends on the particular gas),
    • $T\quad$ is the absolute temperature of the gas (in Kelvin).

    SI Unit

    • Is Pascal: $$p=1\,Pa=1\left[\cfrac{N}{m^2}\right]$$

    Properties

    • Intensive Quantity: does not depend on the size or the amount of matter in the system.
