    Simple Harmonic Motion

    Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM) is a type of periodic oscillatory motion in which an object moves back and forth around an equilibrium position. The restoring force that drives the motion is directly proportional to the displacement from the equilibrium position and acts in the opposite direction.


    Formulas

    • Equation of Motion
      The displacement at time $t$; represents the position of the oscillating object at any given time: $$s(t)=A\sin(\omega t+\theta_0)$$
    • Velocity
      At time $t$; describes how the speed of the oscillating object changes with time: $$v(t)=-\omega A\cos(\omega t+\theta_0)$$
    • Acceleration
      At time $t$; is proportional to the displacement and always points towards the equilibrium position: $$a(t)=-\omega^2s(t)$$
    • Pulsation
      defines the rate at which the oscillating object completes a cycle, measured in radians per unit time: $$\omega=\cfrac{2\pi}{T}=2\pi f=\cfrac{\Delta\theta}{\Delta t}$$
    • Period is inversely related to the frequency and defines the duration of one full oscillation: $$T=\cfrac{2\pi}{\omega}=\cfrac{1}{f}$$
    • Frequency
      determines how many complete cycles the object goes through in one second: $$f=\cfrac{1}{T}=\cfrac{\omega}{2\pi}$$
    • Maximum Velocity
      is the peak velocity reached as the object moves through the equilibrium position: $$v_{max}=A\omega$$
    • Maximum Acceleration
      represents the greatest rate of change of velocity experienced by the object: $$a_{max}=A\omega^2$$
    • Amplitude
      is the maximum extent of the oscillation, representing the farthest point the object reaches from the center of motion: $$A=\cfrac{v_{max}}{\omega}=\cfrac{a_{max}}{\omega^2}$$ N.B. The sine and cosine functions in the Equations of Motion and Velocity can be interchanged, depending on the phase or initial conditions of the system..
