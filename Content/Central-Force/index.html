    Central Force

    A central force is a force that acts along the line connecting two objects and points directly towards or away from a specific central point (often the center of mass of one of the objects). The magnitude of the force depends only on the distance between the objects, and its direction is always along the line joining the two objects.


    Types of Central Forces

    • Universal Gravitation Force
    • Hooke's Law

    The angular momentum remains constant

    To demonstrate In a central force field, the angular momentum remains constant, we can use the fact that the cross product of two parallel vectors is zero. From the definition of a derivative: $$\textbf{if: }\cfrac{d\vec L}{dt}=0\implies\vec L=\text{constant}$$

    • Demonstration
      1. Angular Momentum: defined as: $$\vec L=\vec r\times \vec p$$
      2. Cross Product (Vector): Since in a central force field the force $\vec F$ is always directed along the line connecting the two objects, we have: $$0=\vec r\times \vec F$$
      3. Torque (Physics): This leads to the expression for the torque $\vec\tau$ being zero: $$\vec\tau=\cfrac{d\overrightarrow L}{dt}=0$$
      4. Therefore, since the time derivative of the angular momentum is zero, it implies that the angular momentum $\vec L$ is constant: $$\vec L=\text{constant}$$
