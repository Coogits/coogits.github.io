    From Decimal to Binary Conversione

    The conversion from Decimal (base 10) to Binary (base 2) can be represented using the following method, expressed as a series of divisions by 2 (which is the base of the binary system) and recording the remainders.

    division-remainder algorithm (or repeated division method):

    1. While: while the decimal number $N_i$ is not zero:
      • The remainder of $N_i$ by 2 will be the digit position and the index $i$ it's positional weight.
      • Reiterate using a new decimal integer number dividing the current one by two $N_i=\cfrac{N_i}{2}$
    2. Result: We got the Expanded Form (Numeral System) of the equivalent binary number.

    $$ \begin{align} &\textbf{Math Pseudocode} \\ &\text{Initialize:} \quad i \leftarrow 0 \\ &\textbf{while: } N_{10}>0 \\ &\qquad a_i\leftarrow\mbox{mod}(N_{10},2) \\ &\qquad N_{10} \leftarrow \left\lfloor \cfrac{N_{10}}{2} \right\rfloor \\ &\qquad i\leftarrow i+1 \\ &n\leftarrow i+1 \\ &N_2=\displaystyle\sum_{i=0}^{n}a_i\cdot 2^i \end{align} $$

    • Symbols
      • $N_{10}\quad$ The base 10 number.
      • $N_2\quad$ The base 2 number.
      • $a_i\quad$ The base 2 digit at positional weight $i$
      • $i\quad$ The base 2 digit's positional weight.
      • $n\quad$ The count of the binary digits.
