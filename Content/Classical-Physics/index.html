    Classical Physics

    Classical Physics Is the branch of physics that deals with the laws of nature, developed before the advent of quantum mechanics and relativity.

    Characteristics

    • Deterministic: Classical physics is largely deterministic, meaning that the future behavior of a system can be predicted with precision, given its initial conditions.
    • Macroscopic: Primarily concerned with large-scale systems, as opposed to the atomic and subatomic scales, which are studied in quantum physics.
    • Continuous: Classical theories assume continuous changes and do not account for the discrete nature of matter and energy, which are central to quantum mechanics.

    Limitations

    • Fails to explain phenomena at very small scales (atomic and subatomic).
    • Fails to explain phenomena at speeds close to the speed of light.

    NB. Despite the limitations, classical physics is still valid for macroscopic phenomena at velocities significantly lower than the speed of light.
