    Centripetal Force

    Centripetal force is the net force required to keep an object moving in a circular path. This force is always directed toward the center of the circle along which the object is moving. It does not cause the object to speed up or slow down (in terms of its speed along the path), but it continually changes the direction of the object's velocity, thereby keeping it in circular motion.


    Symbols

    • $\vec F_c\quad$ Centripetal Force (unit: Newton ($N = \cfrac{kg \cdot m}{s^2}$))
    • $F_c\quad$ Centripetal Force Magnitude (unit: Newton ($N = \cfrac{kg \cdot m}{s^2}$))
    • $v\quad$ Speed; is the linear speed at which the object is moving along the circular path (unit: $\cfrac{m}{s}$)
    • $r\quad$ Radius; is the distance from the center of the circle to the object (unit: meter ($m$)
    • $m\quad$ Inertial Mass measures its resistance to acceleration (unit: kilogram $kg$).
    • $\omega\quad$ Angular Velocity is the rate at which the object rotates around the circle (unit: radians per second $\cfrac{rad}{s}$)

    Formulas

    • Definition:
      From the Principles of Dynamics; This states that the centripetal force is equal to the mass of the object multiplied by its centripetal acceleration (which is directed towards the center of the circle).: $$\vec{F_c}=m\vec{a_c}$$
    • Magnitude:
      For an object of mass $m$ moving at a speed $v$ in a circle of radius $r$, the magnitude of the centripetal force is given by: $$F_c=m\cfrac{v^2}{r}$$
    • Alternative Expression:
      If the object is rotating with an angular velocity $\omega$, the centripetal force can also be expressed as: $$F_c=m\omega^2r$$
