    Integer Binary Representation

    The binary representation of integers refers to how whole numbers are stored and processed using the binary numeral system, where values are expressed using only two digits: 0 and 1. Since computers operate using electrical signals that represent two states (on/off), all integer values are ultimately represented in binary at the hardware level.

    Symbols

    • $N_2\quad$ Binary number.
    • $b_n,\ldots,b_1,b_0\quad$ Binary Digit (Bit), bits of the number.
    • $\color{red}S\quad$ Bit for the sign of the binary number; in most platforms it is the most significative bit.
      • $\color{red}\pm\quad$ is the mathematical representation

    Internally Representation

    • Whole Number (Unsigned):
      Assuming a 8-bit binary number, it is internally represented with 8 binary digits $b$: $$ +N_2= \begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|} \hline b_7 & b_6 & b_5 & b_4 & b_3 & b_2 & b_1 & b_0 \\ \hline \end{array} $$
    • Integer Number (Signed):
      Assuming a 8-bit number, the sign is represented with the most significative bit $S=b_7$: $$ {\color{red}\pm}N_2= \begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|} \hline \color{red}S & b_6 & b_5 & b_4 & b_3 & b_2 & b_1 & b_0 \\ \hline \end{array} $$ where:
      • $S=0\implies +N_2\quad$ if the sign is 0 the number is positive
      • $S=1\implies -N_2\quad$ if the sign is 1 the number is negative

    Unsigned vs. Signed Representation

    Integers can be stored in memory in different ways depending on whether they need to represent negative values:

    • Unsigned Integers:
      • Represent only non-negative values (0 and positive numbers).
      • The range for an n-bit unsigned integer is from 0 to 2ⁿ - 1.
    • Signed Integers:
      • Represent both positive and negative numbers.
      • The most common representation is Two’s Complement, where the leftmost bit (most significant bit, MSB) acts as a sign bit:
        • 0 for positive numbers.
        • 1 for negative numbers.
      • The range for an n-bit signed integer using two’s complement is -2ⁿ⁻¹ to 2ⁿ⁻¹ - 1.

    Two’s Complement Representation

    • Negating a Number (flipping sign):
      1. Invert all bits.
      2. Add 1 to the result.
      • Example:
        5₁₀ (00000101₂) → Negate → 11111010₂ → Add 1 → 11111011₂ (Now -5₁₀).

    Key Properties

    • Fixed Bit Width:
      Integers are stored in a fixed number of bits (e.g., 8-bit, 16-bit, 32-bit, 64-bit).
    • Overflow & Underflow:
      When a result exceeds the available bit width, it wraps around due to modular arithmetic.
    • Arithmetic Operations Follow Binary Logic:
      Addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division are performed directly on the binary representation.
