    Complete Factoring (Polynomial)

    Complete Factoring: or Factoring out the Greatest Common Factor (GCF) is the process of identifying and factoring out the greatest common factor (GCF) from all terms in a polynomial. The GCF is the largest monomial that divides all terms of the polynomial without leaving a remainder.

    Symbols

    • $x,y,z\quad$ Variables: N.B. they can be also the same variables ($x,y,z\to x,x,x$) or ($x,y,z\to x,x,y$).

    Definition

    • Polynomial Complete Factoring:
      The Complete Factor of a generic polynomial $P(x)$ is the product between the Polynomial GCD and the quotient of the polynomial for the Polynomial GCD: $$P(x)\quad\to\quad \mbox{GCD}\bigl(P(x)\bigr)\left(\cfrac{P(x)}{\mbox{GCD}(P(x))}\right)$$
    • Polynomial Complete Factoring with explicit Polynomial:
      Assuming the Polynomial with it's generic monomials $P(x)=ax+by+cz$ , the Polynomial GCD is equal to it's Monomial GCD: $$ax+by+cz \quad\to\quad \mbox{GCD}(ax,by,cz)\left(\cfrac{ax+by+cz}{\mbox{GCD}(ax,by,cz)}\right)$$

    Steps

    Steps to Perform Complete Factoring:

    1. Polynomial GCD:
      Identify the Polynomial GCD :
      • Monomials GCD:
        The Polynomial GCD can be Identify by calculating the Monomial GCD of all it's terms.
        • Coefficient GCD:
          Find the Coefficients GCD of the coefficients (numerical part) of all terms.
        • Indeterminates GCD:
          Determine the smallest power of each variable common to all terms.
        • Compose GCD:
          put together the Coefficient GCD and Indeterminates GCD.
      • Write the Polynomial GCD follower by a brace where will be write the quotient of the polynomial for the GCD.
    2. Divide:
      Divide each term of the polynomial by the GCD.
      • Divide each term of the polynomial by the GCD, placing the results inside the parentheses.
      • Result: We got the result after the division.
    3. Verify:
      optional
      • Multiply the GCD back through the terms in parentheses to confirm that the original polynomial is obtained.

    Example

    Compute the GCD of $12x^6+8x^3+16x^2y$

    1. Polynomial GCD:
      • Coefficient: $\mbox{GCD}(12,8,16)=4$
      • Indeterminates: $\mbox{GCD}(x^6,x^3,x^2y)=x^2$
      • Compose: $\mbox{GCD}(12x^6,8x^3,16x^2y)=4x^2$
      • Write: $4x^2(\text{division})$
    2. Divide:
      • $4x^2(\cfrac{12x^6+8x^3+16x^2y}{4x^2})$
      • $4x^2(3x^4+2x+4y)\quad\leftarrow$ Result
    3. Verify (optional):
      • $4x^2\cdot(3x^4+2x+4y)=12x^6+8x^3+16x^2y$
