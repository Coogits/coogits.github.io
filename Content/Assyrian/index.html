    Assyrian

    Timeline

    Periods

    Early Assyrian Period

    (c. 2600–1365 BCE)

    Origins and City-State Development
    • Assyria emerges as a city-state centered in Ashur.
    • Influenced by Sumerians, Akkadians, and Babylonians.
    Old Assyrian Kingdom (c. 2025–1365 BCE)
    • Early kings establish trade networks (notably with Anatolia).
    • Assyria remains a regional power but is often overshadowed by Babylon and Mitanni.

    Middle Assyrian Period

    (c. 1365–912 BCE)

    Rise of Assyria as an Imperial Power
    • King Ashur-uballit I (1365–1330 BCE) breaks free from Mitanni control and expands Assyria.
    • Assyria becomes a strong military power, competing with Hittites and Babylonians.
    Tukulti-Ninurta I

    (1243–1207 BCE)

    • Conquers Babylon but later faces internal rebellion.
    Assyrian Decline

    (c. 1100–950 BCE)

    • Weakened by Aramaean invasions, Assyria loses power.
    • Cities shrink, and control over territory weakens.

    Neo-Assyrian Empire

    (c. 912–609 BCE) → Assyria at its Peak

    Expansion and Brutal Military Campaigns
    • Ashurnasirpal II (883–859 BCE) and Shalmaneser III (859–824 BCE) expand the empire.
    • Assyria dominates Mesopotamia, Syria, and parts of Anatolia.
    Height of Assyrian Power

    (745–627 BCE)

    • Tiglath-Pileser III (745–727 BCE): Military reforms, aggressive expansion.
    • Sargon II (722–705 BCE): Captures Israel’s capital, Samaria.
    • Sennacherib (705–681 BCE): Attacks Jerusalem, builds Nineveh.
    • Ashurbanipal (668–627 BCE): Creates famous Library of Ashurbanipal.
    Fall of Assyria

    (612–609 BCE)

    • Babylonian and Median forces destroy Nineveh (612 BCE).
    • Battle of Carchemish (609 BCE): Final defeat by Babylon and Egypt.

    Post-Assyrian Period

    (609 BCE–650 CE)

    • Assyrian culture and people survive under Babylonian, Persian, Greek, and Roman rule.
    • Eventually assimilated into later Mesopotamian civilizations.
