    Akkadian

    • Start: 2.334 BCE
    • End: 2.154 BCE

    Timeline

    Periods

    Rise of the Akkadian Empire

    (c. 2334–2279 BCE) – Sargon of Akkad

    • Sargon the Great (r. 2334–2279 BCE) founds the Akkadian Empire, unifying Mesopotamia under a single rule for the first time.
    • Defeats the Sumerian city-states, including Uruk, Kish, and Lagash.
    • Expands the empire from the Persian Gulf to the Mediterranean Sea.
    • Establishes Akkadian as the official language, replacing Sumerian in administration.
    • Builds a strong military, introduces efficient governance, and controls major trade routes.

    Peak of the Akkadian Empire

    (c. 2279–2193 BCE) – Naram-Sin’s Expansion

    • Rimush (r. 2279–2270 BCE) and Manishtushu (r. 2270–2255 BCE) maintain the empire but face rebellions.
    • Naram-Sin (r. 2254–2218 BCE), Sargon’s grandson, expands the empire to its greatest extent.

      • Declares himself "King of the Four Corners of the World" and a living god (first Mesopotamian king to do so).

      • Conquers Elam, Subartu, and parts of Anatolia and Syria.

      • The empire reaches its economic and military peak.

    • However, internal revolts begin to weaken central control.

    Decline & Collapse of the Akkadian Empire

    (c. 2193–2154 BCE) – Gutian Invasions

    • Shar-Kali-Sharri (r. 2217–2193 BCE) faces constant internal rebellions and foreign invasions.
    • The empire is weakened by climate change (possibly a drought or famine).
    • The Gutians, a mountain tribe from the Zagros region, invade and take control of Akkad.
    • The Akkadian Empire collapses, leading to a brief period of Gutian rule (c. 2154–2112 BCE) before the rise of the Neo-Sumerian Ur III Dynasty.
