    Cross Product (Vector)

    The cross product (also known as the vector product) of two vectors $\overrightarrow{v}$ and $\overrightarrow{u}$ is a vector that is perpendicular to both $\overrightarrow{v}$ and $\overrightarrow{u}$. The magnitude of the cross product is given by:

    Symbols

    • $\overrightarrow w\quad$ Cross Product
    • $\alpha\quad$ Angle between the vectors $\overrightarrow{v}$ and $\overrightarrow{u}$
    • $\sin\alpha\quad$ Sine (Trigonometry) Function.

    Formulas

    Definition

    • Cross Product (Vector Form)
      Given two vectors the cross product is: $$\vec w=\vec{v} \times \vec{u} = (v_y u_z - v_z u_y) \hat{i} + (v_z u_x - v_x u_z) \hat{j} + (v_x u_y - v_y u_x) \hat{k}$$
    • Cross Product (Magnitude):
      the magnitude is given by the product between the vector's magnitudes and the Sine of the angle between the vectors: $$\lvert\vec w\rvert=\lvert\vec v\times\vec u\rvert=\lvert\vec v\rvert\cdot\lvert\vec u\rvert\sin\alpha$$

    Properties

    • Not commutative
      Is not commutative: $$\overrightarrow{v} \times \overrightarrow{u}=-(\overrightarrow{u} \times \overrightarrow{v})$$
    • Distributive
      Is Distributive $$\overrightarrow{v_3}\times(\overrightarrow{v_1}+\overrightarrow{v_2})=\overrightarrow{v_3}\times\overrightarrow{v_1}+\overrightarrow{v_3}\times\overrightarrow{v_2}$$
    • Parallel Vectors
      The cross product of two parallel vectors is zero: $$\overrightarrow{v} \times \overrightarrow{u}=0\qquad\text{if }\overrightarrow{v}\parallel\overrightarrow{u}$$

    Geometrical Interpretation:

    • The magnitude of the cross product represents the area of the parallelogram formed by $\overrightarrow{v}$ and $\overrightarrow{u}$
    • The direction of the cross product vector is perpendicular to the plane containing $\overrightarrow{v}$ and $\overrightarrow{u}$.
