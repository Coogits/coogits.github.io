    Classical Age

    The Classical Age was a period of great cultural, political, and intellectual achievements, mainly associated with the civilizations of Ancient Greece and Rome, but also including powerful empires in India, China, and Persia. It shaped modern philosophy, government, art, and science.

    • Start: c. 500 BCE
    • End: 476 CE

    Timeline

    Civilizations

    Ancient Greece

    • Start: c. 800 BCE
    • End: 146 BCE (Roman conquest)
    • Key Features: The birth of democracy, philosophy, art, and the Olympic Games. Notable figures include Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle.

    Persian Civilization

    (Achaemenid Empire, 539–330 BCE)

    • Mesopotamia became a province under Persian control after Cyrus the Great conquered Babylon.

    Ancient Rome

    • Start: 27 BCE
    • End: 476 CE (Western Roman Empire fall)
    • Key Features: Expansion across Europe, the development of Roman law, and the spread of Christianity.

    Ancient Egypt

    • Start: c. 3,100 BCE
    • End: 30 BCE (Roman conquest)
    Ptolemaic Period of Egypt
    • Start: 332 BCE
    • End: 30 BCE (Roman conquest)

    Key Features

    Rise of Great Civilizations:

    • Ancient Greece (800 BCE – 146 BCE):
      • Birth of democracy in Athens.
      • Contributions in philosophy, art, theater, and science (Socrates, Plato, Aristotle, Hippocrates).
      • Olympic Games and Greek mythology flourished.
      • Alexander the Great spread Greek culture (Hellenistic Era).
    • Ancient Rome (753 BCE – 476 CE):
      • From Republic to Empire, ruled by figures like Julius Caesar and Augustus.
      • Engineering marvels: aqueducts, roads, Colosseum.
      • Development of Roman law and government systems.
      • Spread of Latin and the Roman military dominance.
    • Persian Empire (550 BCE – 330 BCE):
      • Largest empire of its time under Cyrus the Great.
      • Advanced administration and road systems.
    • Indian Empires (Maurya & Gupta, 321 BCE – 550 CE):
      • Ashoka spread Buddhism.
      • Advances in mathematics (zero, decimal system), medicine, and astronomy.
    • Chinese Dynasties (Zhou, Qin, Han, 1046 BCE – 220 CE):
      • Confucianism and Daoism shaped philosophy.
      • The Great Wall of China and Silk Road trade developed.

    Cultural and Intellectual Advancements:

    • Philosophy & Literature: Greek philosophy (Plato, Aristotle), Roman poetry (Virgil, Ovid), and Indian epics (Mahabharata, Ramayana).

    • Architecture & Art: Greek temples (Parthenon), Roman architecture (Pantheon, aqueducts).

    • Science & Mathematics: Pythagoras, Euclid (geometry), Archimedes (physics).

    3. Political and Military Developments:

    • Greek city-states (Athens, Sparta) and their conflicts (Peloponnesian War).

    • Roman conquests leading to the Pax Romana (peaceful period in Rome).

    • The spread of empires through military campaigns (Alexander the Great, Roman legions).

    4. Religion and Spiritual Movements:

    • Greek and Roman polytheism (Zeus, Jupiter, etc.).

    • Growth of Christianity in Rome, leading to its adoption as the state religion (Edict of Milan, 313 CE).

    • Spread of Hinduism and Buddhism in Asia.

    End of the Classical Age:

    The fall of the Western Roman Empire (476 CE) marks the traditional end of the Classical Age, transitioning into the Middle Ages in Europe. However, in the East, the Byzantine Empire continued Roman traditions, and great civilizations thrived in India and China.
