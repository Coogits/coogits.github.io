    Geometry

    Geometry is the branch of mathematics concerned with the properties, relationships, and measurements of points, lines, shapes, and spaces. It is one of the oldest areas of mathematics and has applications across various fields, including physics, engineering, architecture, and art.

    Subcategories

    Euclidean Geometry

    The study of geometry based on Euclid's postulates, focusing on flat, two-dimensional spaces and three-dimensional solids.

    Analytic Geometry

    The study of geometry using coordinate systems and algebraic equations, combining algebra with geometric concepts.

    Trigonometry

    The branch of mathematics that studies the relationships between the angles and sides of triangles, using functions like sine, cosine, and tangent.

    Non-Euclidean Geometry

    Geometries that reject Euclid's parallel postulate, such as hyperbolic and elliptic geometry.

    Differential Geometry

    The study of curved spaces and surfaces, using calculus to analyze the properties of objects in higher dimensions.

    Applications

    Geometry is essential in fields like:

    • Architecture: Designing buildings, structures, and spaces.
    • Physics: Describing the properties of space, time, and matter.
    • Computer Graphics: Creating and manipulating images and models.
    • Navigation: Calculating distances and directions in both two and three dimensions.
