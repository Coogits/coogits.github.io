    Heat

    Heat is the transfer of energy between systems or objects due to a temperature difference. It flows spontaneously from a hotter body to a colder one until thermal equilibrium is reached. Heat is a form of energy transfer and is not a property of a system, unlike internal energy.

    Symbols

    • $Q\quad$ is the heat exchanged, ( unit: joule (J) )
    • $m\quad$ is the Mass of the substance ( unit: kilogram (kg) ),
    • $c\quad$ is the Specific Heat capacity,
    • $\Delta T\quad$ is the Temperature change ( unit: kelvin (K) ).
    • $L\quad$ Latent Heat

    Formulas

    • Heat: Mathematically, heat is often represented as: $$Q=mc\Delta{T}$$
    • Relation with Latent Heat: $$Q=mL$$

    Heat Unit

    • SI Unit (International System): The standard unit of heat is the joule (J): $$Q=1\,J=1\,kg\cdot m^2\cdot s^{-2}$$
    • Alternative Unit: The calorie ($cal$) is sometimes used, with the conversion: $$1\,cal=4,186\,J$$

    Properties

    • Work and Heat as Energy Transfer
      Heat and Work (Physics) are both ways of transferring energy between systems, but they differ in how the transfer occurs:

      • Work: Energy is transferred through a force acting over a distance (e.g., compressing a gas in a piston).
      • Heat: Energy is transferred due to a temperature difference between two systems (e.g., heating a metal rod in a flame).

    • Heat Transfers Internal Energy
      Since heat is a form of energy transfer, it affects the internal energy of a system. Internal energy includes thermal energy, which is related to the motion of atoms and molecules within the system.

    • Heat Is Not a Stored Form of Energy
      Unlike internal energy, heat is not a property of a system, it only exists when energy is being transferred. A system contains internal energy, but heat only exists during energy exchange due to a temperature difference.
