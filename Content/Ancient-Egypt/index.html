    Ancient Egypt

    • Start: c. 3.150 BCE
    • End: 476 BCE

    Timeline

    Bronze Age

    Early Dynastic Period of Egypt

    (c. 3150–2686 BCE, Dynasties 1–2)

    • Establishment of Pharaonic rule and centralized government.
    • Memphis founded as the capital.
    • Writing system (hieroglyphs) and religious structures begin developing.

    Old Kingdom of Egypt

    (c. 2686–2181 BCE, Dynasties 3–6)

    • Also called the "Age of the Pyramids".
    • Pharaohs seen as divine rulers with absolute power.
    • Major pyramid constructions, including the Great Pyramid of Giza (4th Dynasty, c. 2580 BCE).
    • Declines due to weak rulers, economic strain, and local uprisings.

    First Intermediate Period of Egypt

    (c. 2181–2055 BCE, Dynasties 7–11)

    • Political fragmentation; local rulers (nomarchs) gain power.
    • Civil war and famine weaken central authority.
    • Ends when Mentuhotep II of Thebes reunifies Egypt.

    Middle Kingdom of Egypt

    (c. 2055–1650 BCE, Dynasties 11–13)

    • Political stability and economic growth return.
    • Expansion into Nubia and increased foreign trade.
    • Literature, art, and irrigation projects flourish.
    • Declines as weak rulers lose control, and foreign invaders (Hyksos) gain power.

    Second Intermediate Period of Egypt

    (c. 1650–1550 BCE, Dynasties 14–17)

    • Hyksos (Asiatic rulers) conquer northern Egypt and introduce horses, chariots, and bronze weapons.
    • Thebes remains independent in the south.
    • Ends when Ahmose I expels the Hyksos and reunifies Egypt.

    New Kingdom of Egypt

    (c. 1550–1070 BCE, Dynasties 18–20)

    • Egypt’s Golden Age – military expansion and wealth.
    • Famous pharaohs:
      • Hatshepsut (first female pharaoh, trade expeditions).
      • Thutmose III (Napoleon of Egypt, empire builder).
      • Akhenaten (religious revolution, worship of Aten).
      • Tutankhamun (restored traditional gods).
      • Ramses II (greatest builder, Battle of Kadesh vs. Hittites).
    • Decline due to internal strife and foreign invasions (Sea Peoples, Libyans, Nubians).

    Iron Age

    Third Intermediate Period of Egypt

    (c. 1070–664 BCE, Dynasties 21–25)

    • Political fragmentation; Egypt ruled by Libyans, Nubians, and local warlords.
    • Assyrians invade in 671 BCE, briefly ruling Egypt.

    Late Period of Egypt

    (c. 664–332 BCE, Dynasties 26–31)

    • Egypt briefly regains independence under Psamtik I (Saite Dynasty, 26th).
    • Persians conquer Egypt (525 BCE, 27th Dynasty), ruling as foreign pharaohs.
    • Egypt becomes a Persian satrapy until Alexander the Great conquers it in 332 BCE.

    Classical Age

    Ptolemaic Period of Egypt

    (332–30 BCE)

    • Greek rule under the Ptolemies (descendants of Alexander’s general Ptolemy I).
    • Fusion of Greek and Egyptian culture (Alexandria as a major city).
    • Cleopatra VII is the last ruler before Egypt falls to Rome.

    Roman Egypt

    (30 BCE–476 CE)

    • Egypt becomes a Roman province (30 BCE) after Cleopatra and Mark Antony’s defeat.
    • Christianity spreads and replaces Egyptian religion.
