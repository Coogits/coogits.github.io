    Electric Potential Energy

    Electric Potential Energy ($U$) is the energy stored in a system of electric charges due to their relative positions in an electric field. It represents the work required to assemble the charge configuration or the work needed to move a charge within an electric field.

    Symbols

    • $U\quad$ Electric potential energy (unit: Joule (J))
    • $Q\quad$ source Charge (unit: Coulomb (C))
    • $q\quad$ test Charge (unit: Coulomb (C))
    • $q_1,q_2\quad$ Two interacting point charges (unit: Coulomb (C))
    • $V\quad$ Electric Potential (unit: Volt (V))
    • $r\quad$ Distance between two charges (unit: meter (m))
    • $\epsilon_0\quad$ Vacuum permittivity ($\epsilon_0\simeq8,854\cdot10^{-12}\;\frac{C^2}{N\cdot m^2}$)
    • $W_{AB}\quad$ Work of the Electric Force done when moving a charge from point A to B (unit: Joule (J))
    • $\Delta U\quad$ Change in electric potential energy (unit: Joule (J))
    • $r_A,r_B\quad$ Initial and final distances from the source charge (unit: meter (m))
    • $\sum\quad$ Summation (Arithmetic)

    Formulas

    • Electric Potential Energy of Two Point Charges:
      For two point charges $q_1$ and $q_2$ separated by a distance $r$: $$U(r)=\cfrac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\cfrac{q_1q_2}{r}$$
    • Potential Energy Difference:
      The difference in potential energy when a charge moves from point $A$ to point $B$: $$\Delta{U}=U_B-U_A=-W_{AB}=\cfrac{qQ}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\left(\cfrac{1}{r_B}-\cfrac{1}{r_A}\right)$$
    • Relation with Electric Potential:
      For a charge q in an external electric potential $V$: $$U=qV$$

    Unit

    • SI Unit: $$U=1\;J$$

    Properties of Electric Potential Energy

    • inversely proportional to the distance
      The electric potential energy $U(r)$ is inversely proportional to the distance $r$ between two point charges. This means that as the distance increases, the potential energy decreases: $$U(r)\propto r^{-1}$$
    • Superposition Principle:
      The total electric potential energy in a system of multiple charges is the sum of the potential energies of all pairs of charges: $$U_{tot}=\sum_{i<j}\cfrac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\cfrac{q_iq_j}{r_{ij}}$$
      • $q_i,q_j\quad$ the electric charges
      • $r_{ij}\quad$ is the distance between the charges
