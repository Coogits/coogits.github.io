    Ancient Rome

    Ancient Rome was one of the greatest civilizations in history, known for its military power, advanced engineering, law, and governance. It began as a small city-state, grew into a vast empire, and influenced modern government, architecture, and culture

    • Start: 753 BCE
    • End: 476 CE

    Timeline

    Home > History > Ancient History > Iron Age

    Iron Age

    Roman Kingdom

    (753 BCE – 509 BCE)

    • 753 BCE – Legendary founding of Rome by Romulus.
    • 753–616 BCE – Rule of the first four kings (Romulus, Numa Pompilius, Tullus Hostilius, Ancus Marcius).
    • 616–509 BCE – Etruscan rule (Tarquins); expansion of Rome.
    • 509 BCE – Overthrow of King Tarquinius Superbus, beginning of the Roman Republic.

    Home > History > Ancient History > Classical Age

    Classical Age

    Roman Republic

    (509 BCE – 27 BCE)

    • 509–390 BCE – Early Republic, wars with neighboring cities (Latin League, Etruscans, Samnites).
    • 390 BCE – Sack of Rome by the Gauls.
    • 343–290 BCE – Samnite Wars, Rome dominates central Italy.
    • 264–146 BCE – Punic Wars against Carthage, Rome emerges as a Mediterranean superpower.
    • 146 BCE – Rome destroys Carthage and Corinth, dominating the Mediterranean.
    • 133–27 BCE – Political crises, civil wars (Marius vs. Sulla, Pompey vs. Caesar).
    • 44 BCE – Assassination of Julius Caesar.
    • 31 BCE – Battle of Actium, Octavian defeats Mark Antony & Cleopatra.
    • 27 BCE – Octavian becomes Augustus, end of the Republic.

    Roman Empire

    (27 BCE – 476 CE)

    High Empire

    Principate (27 BCE – 284 CE)

    • 27 BCE – 14 CE – Augustus establishes the empire.
    • 14–68 CE – Julio-Claudian dynasty (Tiberius, Caligula, Claudius, Nero).
    • 69 CE – Year of the Four Emperors, instability.
    • 96–180 CE – Five Good Emperors (Nerva, Trajan, Hadrian, Antoninus Pius, Marcus Aurelius).
    • 180–235 CE – Decline begins under Commodus, leading to instability.
    • 235–284 CE – Crisis of the Third Century, 26 emperors in 50 years, economic collapse.

    Late Empire

    Dominate (284–476 CE)

    • 284–305 CE – Diocletian reforms, introduces Tetrarchy.
    • 312 CE – Constantine wins at Milvian Bridge, converts to Christianity.
    • 330 CE – Constantinople becomes the new capital.
    • 395 CE – Death of Theodosius I, empire permanently split.
    • 410 CE – Sack of Rome by Visigoths.
    • 455 CE – Sack of Rome by Vandals.
    • 476 CE – Fall of Western Roman Empire (Odoacer deposes Romulus Augustulus).
