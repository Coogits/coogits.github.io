    Heat Transfer

    Heat transfer is the process by which thermal energy is exchanged between physical systems due to a temperature difference. It occurs when heat flows from regions of higher temperature to regions of lower temperature. There are three primary modes of heat transfer:

    • Thermal Conduction:

      • Mechanism: Direct transfer of heat through a material due to molecular collisions and the movement of electrons.
      • Example: Heating one end of a metal rod causes the other end to eventually become warmer.

    • Convection (Heat Transfer)

      • Mechanism: Transfer of heat by the bulk movement of a fluid (liquid or gas). It can be natural (driven by buoyancy forces due to density differences) or forced (using external means such as a pump or fan).
      • Example: Warm air rising from a heater and circulating around a room.

    • Thermal Radiation

      • Mechanism: Transfer of heat via electromagnetic waves (primarily infrared radiation), which does not require a medium to travel.
      • Example: The heat from the sun warming the Earth.
