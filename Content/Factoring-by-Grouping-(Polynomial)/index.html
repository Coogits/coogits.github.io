    Factoring by Grouping (Polynomial)

    Factoring by Grouping is a method used to factor a polynomial, particularly when the polynomial has four terms. The process involves grouping terms in pairs or sets and factoring out the greatest common factor (GCF) from each group. Then, if possible, you factor out the common binomial factor that remains after grouping.

    Method

    1. Identify the terms: Start with a polynomial of the form where you have four terms in the polynomial.
    2. Group in pairs: Group the terms into two pairs that have common factors.
    3. Factor out the GCF: For each group of terms, factor out the greatest common factor (GCD). If necessary, factor the common factor from both groups.
    4. Binomial factor: After factoring, check if the two groups have a common binomial factor. If they do, factor out the common binomial factor:
    5. More Factorization: Check for more factorizations . If so, continue factoring until the polynomial is fully factored.

    Example

    1. Identify the term: $x^3+x^2+3x+3$
    2. Group in pairs
      1. $\underline{x^3+x^2}+\underline{3x+3}$
    3. Factor out the GCF
      1. $\underline{x^2(x+1)}+\underline{3(x+1)}$
    4. Binomial factor:
      1. $x^2(x+1)+3(x+1)=(x+1)(x^2+3)$
