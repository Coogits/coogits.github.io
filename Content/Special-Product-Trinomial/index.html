    Special Product Trinomial

    A trinomial is a polynomial with three terms. The term special product trinomial refers to a type of quadratic or higher degree trinomial that can be factored easily using a pattern. This pattern relies on two numbers that satisfy the following conditions:

    • The sum of the two numbers equals the coefficient of the middle term.
    • The product of the two numbers equals the product of the leading coefficient (the coefficient of $x^{2n}$ and the constant term. This method is typically applied to both monic polynomials (those where the leading coefficient is 1) and non-monic polynomials (those with a leading coefficient other than 1).

    Factoring non-monic trinomials

    When the leading coefficient $a$ of a trinomial is not 1, we use a specific complete formula to factor the polynomial. In these cases, we rely on finding two numbers that satisfy certain conditions and then use those values to break up the middle term.

    Non-Monic Trinomials Formulas

    • Complete Formula
      For a non-monic trinomial of the form: $$ax^{2n}+bx^n+c$$ The general factoring form is: $$(x^n+u)(x^n+v)$$ where $u$ and $v$ are two numbers that satisfy: $$\begin{align}&u+v=b\\&u\times v=ac\end{align}$$
    • Simplified Formula
      For non-monic trinomials where $n=1$, the formula is simplified in: $$ax^2+bx+c$$ This can be factored as: $$(x+u)(x+v)$$ where $u$ and $v$ are two numbers that satisfy: $$\begin{align}&u+v=b\\&u\times v=ac\end{align}$$

    Factoring monic trinomials

    For a monic trinomial (where the leading coefficient is 1), the process of factoring is simpler. The leading coefficient does not affect the product of the two numbers we are looking for.

    Monic Trinomial Formula

    • **Factoring monic trinomials
      For a general monic quadratic trinomial of the form: $$x^{2n}+bx^n+c$$ The factoring form is: $$(x^n+u)(x^n+v)$$ where $u$ and $v$ are two numbers that satisfy: $$\begin{align}&u+v=b\\&u\times v=c\end{align}$$
    • Factoring monic trinomials Simplified:
      if the exponent is unit value $n=1$ the formula can be simplified in: $$x^2+bx+c$$ the trinomial can be factorized as: $$(x+u)(x+v)$$ where $u$ and $v$ are two numbers that satisfy: $$\begin{align}&u+v=b\\&u\times v=c\end{align}$$
