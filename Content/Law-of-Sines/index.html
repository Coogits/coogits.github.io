    Law of Sines

    The Law of Sines relates the angles and sides of a triangle. It states that the ratio of the length of a side of a triangle to the sine of the angle opposite that side is constant for all three sides of the triangle. The law can be applied to any triangle (not necessarily a right triangle), and is especially useful for solving non-right triangles (oblique triangles).

    Symbols

    • $a,b,c\quad$ are the lengths of the sides of the triangle.
    • $\alpha,\beta,\gamma\quad$ are the angles opposite sides $a,b,c$, respectively.
    • $r\quad$ is the radius of the unit circumference
    • $\sin\quad$ Sine (Trigonometry)

    Formulas

    • Formula:
      Is defined as: $$2r=\dfrac{a}{\sin\alpha}=\dfrac{b}{\sin\beta}=\dfrac{c}{\sin\gamma}$$

    Applications

    • Solving triangles when two sides and the included angle are known (SAS case).
    • Solving triangles when all three sides are known (SSS case).
    • Finding angles when all sides of the triangle are known.
