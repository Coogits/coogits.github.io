    Tangent (Trigonometry)

    The Tangent function ($\tan$) is a fundamental trigonometric function that relates the angle of a right triangle to the ratio of its sides.

    Symbols

    • $\text{opposite}\quad$ The side opposite to the angle $\theta$.
    • $\text{adjacent}\quad$ The side next to the angle $\theta$ excluding the hypotenuse.
    • $x\quad$ horizontal coordinate of the point on the unit circle corresponding to the angle $\theta$.
    • $y\quad$ vertical coordinate of the point on the unit circle corresponding to the angle $\theta$.

    Definition

    • Right Triangle Definition: For a given angle $\theta$ in a right triangle: $$\tan\theta=\cfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}$$
    • Unit Circle Definition - In the unit circle, where a point $(x, y)$ represents an angle $\theta$ measured from the positive $x$-axis: $$\begin{align}\tan\theta=\cfrac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}=\cfrac{y}{x} \\ \text{where }\cos\theta=x\neq0\end{align}$$

    Properties

    • Periodicity: $\quad\tan(\theta+\pi)=\tan\theta$
    • Odd Function: $\quad\tan(-\theta)=-\tan\theta$
    • Range: $\quad-\infty<\tan\theta<+\infty$
    • Asymptotes: $\quad\theta=\cfrac{\pi}{2}+k\pi \qquad k\in\mathbb{Z}$
