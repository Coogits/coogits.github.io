    Differentiation

    Differentiation is the mathematical process of finding the derivative of a function, which quantifies the rate of change of the function with respect to its input variable. It provides the slope of the tangent line to a curve at a given point.

    Symbols

    • $f(x), g(x)\quad$ Functions
    • $f'(x), g'(x)\quad$ Function's Derivative
    • $x\quad$ Variable

    Formulas

    • Definition:
      For a function $f(x)$, the derivative $f'(x)$ is defined as: $$f'(x)=\lim_{x\to 0}\cfrac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}$$

    Basic Differentiation Rules

    Some fundamental rules make differentiation easier:

    • Power Rule:
      The derivative of $x^n$ is given by: $$\cfrac{d}{dx}x^n=nx^{n-1}$$
    • Constant Rule
      The derivative of a constant is always zero: $$\cfrac{d}{dx}C=0$$
    • Sum Rule:
      The derivative of a sum is the sum of the derivatives: $$\cfrac{d}{dx}[f(x)+g(x)]=f'(x)+g'(x)$$
    • Product Rule:
      The derivative of a product follows: $$\cfrac{d}{dx}[f(x)g(x)]=f'(x)g(x)+f(x)g'(x)$$
    • Quotient Rule:
      The derivative of a quotient is given by: $$\cfrac{d}{dx}\left(\cfrac{f(x)}{g(x)}\right)=\cfrac{f'(x)g(x)-f(x)g'(x)}{g(x)^2}$$
    • Chain Rule:
      The derivative of a composite function is: $$\cfrac{d}{dx}f(g(x))=f'(g(x))\cdot g'(x)$$
