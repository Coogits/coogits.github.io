    French Revolution

    The French Revolution (1789‚Äì1799) was a political and social upheaval in France that led to the overthrow of the monarchy, the rise of democracy, and the radical reorganization of society. It was driven by economic hardship, social inequalities, and the growing demand for political representation.

    • Start: 1789 CE
    • End: 1899 CE

    Timeline

    Causes

    • Social Inequality (The Three Estates)
      • Society was divided into three classes:
        • First Estate: Clergy (privileged, no taxes)
        • Second Estate: Nobility (privileged, no taxes)
        • Third Estate: Commoners (paid heavy taxes, had no privileges)
      • The Third Estate resented this unfair system.
    • Economic Crisis
      • France was in deep debt due to costly wars, including the American Revolution.
      • Heavy taxation on commoners while nobles and clergy were exempt.
      • Food shortages and rising bread prices led to mass starvation.
    • Weak Leadership
      • King Louis XVI was indecisive and failed to address the crisis.
      • Queen Marie Antoinette was unpopular for lavish spending.
    • Enlightenment Ideas
      • Philosophers like Voltaire, Rousseau, and Montesquieu promoted ideas of liberty, equality, and democracy, inspiring revolutionaries.

    Major Events

    1. Estates-General & National Assembly (1789)
      • The Third Estate broke away and formed the National Assembly, declaring a new government.
    2. Storming of the Bastille (July 14, 1789) üéá
      • A mob stormed the Bastille prison, a symbol of royal oppression. This event marked the start of the revolution.
    3. Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen (1789)
      • This document proclaimed liberty, equality, and fraternity as the foundation of a new France.
    4. Abolition of the Monarchy & Execution of King Louis XVI (1793)
      • The king was put on trial and guillotined. France became a republic.

      • Led by Robespierre, thousands of "enemies of the revolution" were executed by guillotine, including Queen Marie Antoinette.
    6. Rise of Napoleon (1799)
      • After years of instability, Napoleon Bonaparte seized power in a coup and ended the revolution, becoming Emperor of France.
