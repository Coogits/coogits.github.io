    Ionic Bond

    An ionic bond is a type of chemical bond that occurs when electrons are transferred from one atom to another, resulting in the formation of oppositely charged ions.
    This typically happens between a metal and a non-metal.

    Process

    • Condition:
      the two atoms have a difference in electronegativity greater than 1.9
    • Formation:
      • The metal atom loses one or more electrons to become a positively charged ion (cation).
      • The non-metal atom gains the electrons to become a negatively charged ion (anion).
    • Attraction:
      The oppositely charged ions are attracted to each other due to the Electrostatic Force, forming a stable compound.

    Metals and non-Metals

    A list of Metals (Blue) and non-Metals (red) elements

    Example:

    In sodium chloride (NaCl):

    • Sodium (Na) loses one electron to become Na⁺.
    • Chlorine (Cl) gains one electron to become Cl⁻.
    • The positive Na⁺ and negative Cl⁻ ions are attracted to each other, forming the ionic bond in NaCl.
