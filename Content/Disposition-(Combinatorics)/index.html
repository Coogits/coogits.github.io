    Disposition (Combinatorics)

    A disposition (also known as an arrangement) refers to a selection of $k$ elements from a set of $n$ elements, where the order of the elements matters. Dispositions can be classified as:

    Symbols

    • $n\quad$ Total number of elements in the set.
    • $k\quad$ Number of elements being chosen.
    • $D_{n,k}\quad$ Number of simple dispositions.
    • $D'_{n,k}\quad$ Number of dispositions with repetition.

    Formulas

    • Simple Disposition (Without Repetition):
      Elements are selected without repetition: $$D_{n,k}=\cfrac{n!}{(n-k)!}\qquad k\geq n$$
    • Disposition with Repetition:
      Each element can be selected more than once, with repetition allowed up to $k$ times: $$D_{n,k}'=n^k$$

    Examples

    • Simple Disposition: $D_{4,2}$ : $$\{a,b,c,d\}\begin{cases} \begin{cases}a \begin{cases}b\to ab \\ c\to ac \\ d\to ad \end{cases} \end{cases} \\ \begin{cases}b \begin{cases}a\to ab \\ c\to bc \\ d\to bd \end{cases} \end{cases} \\ \begin{cases}c \begin{cases}a\to ac \\ b\to bc \\ d\to cd \end{cases}\end{cases} \\ \begin{cases}d \begin{cases}a\to ad \\ b\to bd \\ c\to cd \end{cases}\end{cases} \end{cases}=12$$
    • Disposition with Repetition: $D'_{4,2}$: $$\{a,b,c,d\}\begin{cases} \begin{cases}a \begin{cases}a\to aa \\ b\to ab \\ c\to ac \\ d\to ad \end{cases} \end{cases} \\ \begin{cases}b \begin{cases}a\to ab \\ b\to bb \\ c\to bc \\ d\to bd \end{cases} \end{cases} \\ \begin{cases}c \begin{cases}a\to ac \\ b\to bc \\ c\to cc \\ d\to cd \end{cases}\end{cases} \\ \begin{cases}d \begin{cases}a\to ad \\ b\to bd \\ c\to cd \\ d\to dd \end{cases}\end{cases} \end{cases}=16$$
