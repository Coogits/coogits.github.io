    Permutation (Combinatorics)

    A permutation is an arrangement of all or part of a set of distinct elements into a specific order. It represents a way to order or sequence the elements in a set.

    Symbols and Terms

    • $P\quad$ Permutation
    • $n\quad$ Total number of elements in the set.
      • $n=n_1+n_2+\cdot\ldots\cdot+n_k\quad$: In the permutation with repetitions.
    • $n!\quad$ Factorial of $n$, which is the product of all integers from $n$ to 1.
    • $n_1!\cdot n_2!\cdot\ldots\cdot n_k!\quad$ the counts of each distinct element in the set.
    • $k\quad$ Is the number of distinct types of elements in the multiset.

    Formulas

    • Simple Permutations:
      When all elements of a set are used and arranged in order: $$P_n=n!\qquad n=|A|$$
    • Permutations with Repetition:
      When some elements in the set can repeat, and the arrangement includes all elements: $$P_n^{n_1,n_2\ldots}=\cfrac{n!}{n_1!\cdot n_2!\cdot\ldots\cdot n_k!}$$

    Examples

    • Simple Permutation: $\{a,b,c,d\}\rightarrow P_4=4!=24$ $$\begin{align} abcd,abdc,acbd,acdb,adbc,adcb \\ bacd,badc,bcad,bcda,bdac,bdca \\ cabd,cadb,cbad,cbda,cdab,cdba \\ dabc,dacb,dbac,dbca,dcab,dcba \end{align}$$
    • Permutations with Repetition: $\{a,a,b,c\}\to P_4^{2,1,1}=\cfrac{4!}{2!\cdot1!\cdot1!}=12$ $$\begin{align} aabc,aacb\\ baac,caab\\ bcaa,cbaa\\ abca,acba\\ abac,acab\\ baca,caba \end{align}$$
    • Permutazioni Circolari: ${a,b,c,d}\quad\to\quad P_4^c=(4-1)!=6$ $$\begin{align} abcd,abdc\\ acbd,acdb\\ adbc,adcb\\ \end{align}$$

    Applications

    • Arranging people in a line.
    • Shuffling cards.
    • Generating passwords or codes.
    • Analyzing probability and combinatorics.
