    Thermodynamics

    Thermodynamics is the branch of physics that studies the relationships between heat, work, energy, and entropy in physical systems. It describes how energy is transferred and transformed, particularly in processes involving changes in temperature, pressure, and volume.

    Thermodynamics is governed by four fundamental laws:

    1. Zeroth Law: Defines thermal equilibrium and temperature.
    2. First Law: States the conservation of energy (heat and work are energy transfers). $$\Delta{U}=Q-W$$
    3. Second Law: Introduces entropy and the direction of spontaneous processes. $$Q_A=W+Q_C$$
    4. Third Law: Describes the behavior of systems as they approach absolute zero temperature.
