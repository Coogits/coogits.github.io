    Electric Charge

    Electric Charge is a fundamental property of matter that quantifies the ability of particles to exert and experience electromagnetic forces. It is essential for understanding the behavior of charged particles and the interactions between them.

    Symbols

    • $Q\quad$ Source Charge (unit: Coulombs (C))
      • This is the charge that creates the electric field or electric potential in space.
      • It is usually assumed to be fixed in position.
    • $q\quad$ Test Charge (unit: Coulombs (C))
      • This is a small charge placed in the electric field generated by $Q$.
      • It is usually assumed to be small enough that it does not disturb the field created by $Q$
    • $q_1,q_2\quad$ Two interacting point charges (unit: Coulombs (C))
    • $I\quad$ Electric Current (unit: Ampere (A ))
    • $t\quad$ Time (Physics) (unit: seconds (s))
    • $\Delta Q\quad$ Electric Charge Difference (unit: Coulombs (C))
    • $Q_0,Q_1\quad$ Electric Charge initial and final (unit: Coulombs (C))
    • $e\quad$ Elementary Charge (unit: Coulombs (C))
    • $\sum\quad$ Summation (Arithmetic).

    Formulas

    • Definition via Current:
      indicates that the total charge transferred is the product of the current and the duration for which it flows: $$Q=It$$
    • Elementary Charge:
      The elementary charge (e) is the smallest unit of charge that exists independently and is carried by fundamental particles such as protons and electrons: $$e=1.602\,176\,634\times10^{-19}\;C$$

    Properties

    • Electric Charge Conservation Principle:
      the total electric charge remains constant: $$\Delta Q=Q_1-Q_0$$ Where in a Isolated System: $\Delta Q=0$ If charge is added/removed: $\Delta Q\neq0$

    • Superposition Principle:
      The total electric charge is the sum of all the electric charges in the system: $$Q_{tot}=\displaystyle\sum_{i}Q_i$$

    • Quantization of Charge:
      Electric Charge is quantized, meaning that any observable charge Q is an integer multiple of the Elementary Charge: $$Q=ne\qquad n\in\mathbb{Z}$$
