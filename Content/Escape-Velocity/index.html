    Escape Velocity

    Escape Velocity is the minimum speed an object must have in order to break free from the gravitational influence of a celestial body (such as a planet, moon, or star) without further propulsion.

    The object must have enough kinetic energy to overcome the gravitational potential energy that holds it to the body. Escape velocity does not depend on the mass of the object, but rather on the mass of the celestial body and the distance from its center.

    Formula:

    • The escape velocity $v_f$ at a distance $r$ from the center of a body with mass $M$ is given by: $$v_f=\sqrt{\cfrac{2GM}{r}}$$

    Symbols

    • $v_f\quad$ is the escape velocity.
    • $G\quad$ is the Gravitational Constant
    • $M\quad$ is the Gravitational Mass of the celestial body.
    • $r\quad$ is the distance from the center of the body (typically the radius of the body if the object starts on the surface).
