    Uniformly Accelerated Linear Motion

    Accelerated linear motion refers to the motion of an object moving along a straight path with a changing velocity due to the presence of a nonzero acceleration. This means the object's speed or direction (or both) are varying over time.

    Symbols

    • $s(t)\quad$ Position at time $t$; in meters
    • $v(t)\quad$ Velocity at time $t$; in $\left[\cfrac{m}{s}\right]$
    • $a\quad$ Acceleration (constant); in $\left[\cfrac{m}{s^2}\right]$
    • $t\quad$ Time; in seconds
    • $t_0\quad$ Initial time; in seconds
    • $v_0\quad$ Initial velocity; in $\left[\cfrac{m}{s}\right]$
    • $s_0\quad$ Initial position.

    Formulas

    • Motion Equation
      How to find the position at time $t$: $$s(t)=\cfrac{1}{2}a(t-t_0)^2+v_0(t-t_0)+s_0\qquad t\ge t_0$$
    • Velocity Equation
      How to find velocity at time $t$: $$v(t)=a(t-t_0)+v_0$$

    Graphical Representation:

    • The position-time graph (s vs. t) is a curve (parabolic if acceleration is constant).
    • The velocity-time graph (v vs. t) is a straight line with a slope equal to acceleration.
    • The acceleration-time graph (a vs. t) is a horizontal line if acceleration is constant.
