    Symmetric Difference (Set Theory)

    The symmetric difference of two sets $A$ and $B$, denoted as $A\triangle B$, is the set of elements that belong to either $A$ or $B$, but not to both.

    In the image:
    Venn Diagram about a symmetric difference ($A\triangle B\triangle C$)

    Symbols

    • $A\triangle B\quad$ Symmetric difference of $A$ and $B$.
    • $A\setminus B\quad$ Relative Complement of $B$ in $A$.

    Definition and Notations

    • Definition:
      Is the union of the relative complement of $B$ in $A$ $(A\setminus B)$ and the relative complement of $A$ in $B$ $(B\setminus A)$: $$A\triangle B=(A\setminus B)\cup(B\setminus A)$$
    • Definition using XOR: $$A\triangle B=\{x:(x\in A)\oplus(x\in B)\}$$
    • Alternative Notations: $$A\triangle B= A\ominus B = A\oplus B$$

    Properties

    • Commutative:
      The symmetric difference is commutative: $$A\triangle B=B\triangle A$$
    • Associative:
      The symmetric difference is associative: $$(A\triangle B)\triangle C=A\triangle(B\triangle C)$$
    • The empty:
      The empty-set is neutral: $$A\triangle\emptyset=A$$
