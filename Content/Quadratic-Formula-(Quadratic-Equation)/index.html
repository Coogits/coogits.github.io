    Quadratic Formula (Quadratic Equation)

    Quadratic Formula is used to find the nature of the roots of a quadratic equation in a Standard Form.

    • Key Points
      • Standard Form only: This method is valid for quadratic equations in standard form

    Symbols

    • $a,b,c\quad$ Coefficients
    • $x\quad$ Variable
    • $x_{1,2}\quad$ Roots or Solutions, they are represented as $x_1$ and $x_2$

    Formulas

    • Quadratic equation in Standard Form: $$ax^2+bx+c=0$$
    • Discriminant: $$\Delta = b^2-4ac$$
    • Roots or solutions: $$x_{1,2}=\cfrac{-b\pm\sqrt{\Delta}}{2a}$$

    Resolution

    The value of the discriminant determines the type of solutions (roots) of the quadratic equation:

    • $\Delta >0$:
      If the Discriminant is greater than zero the equation has two distinct real roots. the roots are: $$\begin{cases} x_1=\cfrac{-b+\sqrt{\Delta}}{2a} \\ x_2=\cfrac{-b-\sqrt{\Delta}}{2a}\end{cases}$$
    • $\Delta=0$:
      If the Discriminant is zero The equation has one repeated real root (both roots are the same). the root is: $$x=\cfrac{-b}{2a}$$
    • $\Delta<0$:
      If the Discriminant is less than zero The equation has no real roots (the roots are complex or imaginary). $$\begin{cases} x_1=\cfrac{-b+i\sqrt{\Delta}}{2a} \\ x_2=\cfrac{-b-i\sqrt{\Delta}}{2a}\end{cases}$$
