    point-slope form (Line)

    The point-slope form is an equation of a straight Line (Analytic Geometry) that is defined by a point on the line and the slope of the line. It is useful for writing the equation of a line when you know a point $(x_1,x_2)$ on the line and its slope $m$.

    • Point-slope form: $$y-y_1=m(x-x_1)$$
    • Slope: From two points $P_1=(x_1,y_1)\quad P_2=(x_2,y_2)$: $$m=\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}=\cfrac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}\qquad x_1\neq x_2$$
    • Intercept: From two points $P_1=(x_1,y_1)\quad P_2=(x_2,y_2)$: $$q=-\cfrac{x_2y_1-x_1y_2}{x_2-x_1} \qquad b\neq0$$
