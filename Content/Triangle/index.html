    Triangle

    A triangle is a three-sided polygon defined by three vertices $A,B,C$ three sides $a,b,c$ and three internal angles $\alpha,\beta,\gamma$. It is the simplest polygon and is defined by the sum of its internal angles, which always equals $180^\circ$.

    Symbols

    • $A,B,C\quad$ Vertices (points in coordinate form, e.g., $A=(x,y)$)
    • $a,b,c\quad$ Sides of the triangle (segments)
    • $\alpha,\beta,\gamma\quad$ Angles, opposite to their respective sides
    • $\mathcal{A}\quad$ Area (Plane Geometry)
    • $2p\quad$ Perimeter (Geometry)
    • $p\quad$ Semi-perimeter $(p=\frac{2p}{2})$
    • $R\quad$ Circumradius
    • $r\quad$ Inradius

    Formulas

    • Perimeter: $$2p=a+b+c$$
    • Area:
      Generic Area: $$\mathcal{A}=\frac{1}{2}\times\text{base}\times\text{height}$$ Heron's Formula (when all sides are known): $$\mathcal{A}=\sqrt{p(p-a)(p-b)(p-c)}$$

    Special Circles

    • Circumradius:
      The radius of the circumcircle, which is the unique circle that passes through all three vertices of the triangle. $$R=\cfrac{abc}{4\mathcal{A}}$$
    • Inradius:
      The radius of the incircle, which is the largest circle that fits inside the triangle and is tangent to all three sides. $$r=\cfrac{\mathcal{A}}{p}$$

    Special Points

    • Median Point
      Segment's median point: $$M_a=\cfrac{B+C}{2}$$
    • Centroid:
      The intersection of the three medians, dividing each median in a 2:1 ratio: $$G=\cfrac{ A+B+C}{3}$$
    • Orthocenter:
      Intersection of altitudes (not necessarily inside the triangle).
    • Circumcenter:
      The intersection of the perpendicular bisectors, acting as the center of the circumcircle.

    Special Segments

    • Medians:
      Line segments from a vertex to the midpoint of the opposite side: $$\overline{AM_a}$$
    • Altitudes:
      Perpendicular segments from a vertex to the opposite side (or its extension), meeting at the orthocenter.
    • Angle Bisectors:
      Segments dividing an angle into two equal parts, meeting the opposite side at the incenter.
    • Perpendicular Bisectors:
      Lines perpendicular to a side's midpoint, meeting at the circumcenter. $$\overline{ M_a O} \quad\text{such that}\quad \overline{ M_a O}\perp \overline{BC}$$

    Trigonometric Relations

    • Law of Sines:
      Trigonometric relation using the Law of Sines: $$2R=\dfrac{a}{\sin\alpha}=\dfrac{b}{\sin\beta}=\dfrac{c}{\sin\gamma}$$
    • Law of Cosines:
      Trigonometric relation using the Law of Cosines: $$c^2=a^2+b^2-2ab\cos\gamma$$

    Types by angles

    Types of Triangles (by angles):

    Right Triangle

    One angle is exactly 90°.

    Acute

    All angles are less than 90°.

    Obtuse:

    One angle is greater than 90°.

    Types by sides

    Types of Triangles (by sides):

    • Equilateral:
      All three sides are equal.
    • Isosceles:
      Two sides are equal.
    • Scalene:
      All three sides have different lengths.

    Properties

    • Sum of the Angles:
      The sum of the internal angles is $180^\circ$
