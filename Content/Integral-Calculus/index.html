    Integral Calculus

    Integral calculus is the branch of calculus that studies the accumulation of quantities and the areas under and between curves. It is concerned with the concept of integration, which is the reverse process of differentiation. Integral calculus is used to compute areas, volumes, total accumulated values, and other quantities that arise from summing infinitely small parts.

    Concepts

    Integral

    An integral calculates the total accumulation of a quantity, often interpreted as the area under a curve. It is used in physics for computing work, energy, and probability distributions.

    Indefinite Integral

    A function whose derivative is the given function.
