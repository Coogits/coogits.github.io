    Tension (Physics)

    Tension is a force transmitted through a string, rope, cable, or any other type of flexible connector. It arises when the object is pulled from either end, causing the rope or string to stretch. The tension force is always directed along the length of the rope (or other material) and is equal in magnitude but opposite in direction on both sides of the object being pulled.

    Symbols

    • $T$ – Tension (Force in the rope, unit: Newton ($N = \frac{kg \cdot m}{s^2}$))
    • $F$ – Applied Force (unit: Newton ($N$))
    • $A$ – Cross-sectional Area of the rope (unit: $m^2$)
    • $\sigma$ – Tensile Stress (force per unit area, unit: Pascal ($Pa = \frac{N}{m^2}$))

    Formula

    • Tension (Force) in the Rope:
      Tension is simply the force applied to the rope: $$T=F$$
    • Tensile Stress (Force per Unit Area):
      If we consider the force distribution over a cross-sectional area, the tensile stress is defined as:$$\sigma=\cfrac{F}{A}$$

    Properties

    • Tension is a contact force because it requires direct contact between objects.
    • It is always directed along the axis of the rope, pulling objects at either end toward each other.
    • The tension is the same throughout the rope, assuming the rope is massless and there are no other forces acting on it.
