    Greatest Common Divisor (Polynomial)

    Polynomial Greatest Common Divisor: The GCD of one, two or more polynomials is the polynomial of the highest degree that divides all the given polynomials without leaving a remainder. This polynomial must be a common divisor of all the input polynomials, and its leading coefficient is typically taken as 1 (monic polynomial) to ensure uniqueness.

    • Single Polynomials: Assuming the generic polynomial $P(x)=m_1\pm m_2\pm\ldots$ the Polynomial GCD is to the GCD of it's monomials.
      • $\mbox{GCD}\bigl(P(x)\bigr)=\mbox{GCD}\bigl(m_1,m_2,\ldots)\bigr)$
    • Multiple Polynomials: Assuming the generic polynomials $P(x)=m_1\pm m_2\pm\ldots$ and $Q(x)=m_3\pm m_4\pm\ldots$ the Polynomial GCD is the GCD of all the monomials in both polynomials:
      • $\mbox{GCD}\bigl(P(x),Q(x)\bigr)=\mbox{GCD}\bigl(m_1,m_2,m_3,m_4,\ldots)\bigr)$
