    Conic Section

    Conics (or conic sections) are the curves obtained by the intersection of a plane and a double-napped cone. The shape of the resulting curve depends on the angle at which the plane intersects the cone.


    Formulas

    • General Equation of a Conic Section: $$ax^2+bxy+cy^2+dx+ey+f=0$$

    Types of Conic Sections:

    • Circle
      The plane is perpendicular to the cone's axis.
    • Ellipse
      The plane cuts the cone at an angle, but does not intersect the base.
    • Parabola
      The plane is parallel to a generating line of the cone.
    • Hyperbola
      The plane cuts both nappes of the cone.
