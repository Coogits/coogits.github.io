    Divisibility Rules

    Divisibility Rules are rules that allow you to determine in advance whether a number is divisible by another specific number without the need to perform the division.

    Symbols

    • $a\quad$ the number to divide (with all digits).
    • $a_n,a_{n-1},\ldots,a_0\quad$ the digits of the number starting from the most significant to the least significant.
    • $a_0\quad$ the least significant digit (the units).
    • $a_n\quad$ the most significant digit.
    • $a'\quad$ the number without the least significant digit.

    Common Divisibility Rules

    • Divisibility by 2:
      If it is even. $$a=2k\qquad k\in\mathbb{N}$$
    • Divisibility by 3:
      If the sum of its digits is a multiple of 3. $$a_n+a_{n-1}+\ldots+a_0=3k \qquad k\in\mathbb{N}$$
    • Divisibility by 5:
      If its least significant digit is either 0 or 5. $$a_0\in\{0, 5\}$$
    • Divisibility by 7:
      If the difference between the number without its least significant digit and twice the least significant digit is 0 or a multiple of 7. (For large numbers, this process is repeated.) $$a'-2a_0=7k \qquad k\in\mathbb{N}$$
    • Divisibility by 11: if the difference between the sum of the digits in odd positions and the sum of the digits in even positions is 0 or a multiple of 11. $$\displaystyle\sum_{i=0}^na_{2i}-\sum_{i=0}^na_{2i+1}=11k \qquad k\in\mathbb{N}$$
