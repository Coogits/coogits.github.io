    Iron Age

    The Iron Age was the period when iron became the dominant material for tools, weapons, and everyday objects, replacing bronze. It marked the rise of powerful empires, advanced warfare, and the spread of writing and culture.

    • Start: c. 1200 BCE
    • End: 500 BCE

    Timeline

    Civilizations

    Ancient Egypt

    • Start: c. 3,100 BCE
    • End: 30 BCE (Roman conquest)
    • Key Features: The rise of pharaohs, the building of pyramids, and advances in mathematics, astronomy, and medicine.

    Mesopotamian Civilization

    • Start: c. 3,000 BCE
    • End: 539 BCE
    • Key Features: The Sumerians, Akkadians, Babylonians, and Assyrians. Development of cuneiform writing, the wheel, and early legal codes (e.g., the Code of Hammurabi).

    Ancient Greece

    • Start: c. 800 BCE
    • End: 146 BCE

    Persian Civilization

    • Start: c. 550 BCE
    • End: 331 BCE
    • Key Features: Mesopotamia became a province under Persian control after Cyrus the Great conquered Babylon.

    Roman Kingdom

    • Start: 753 BCE
    • End: 509 BCE

    Key Features

    1. Iron Tools & Weapons:
      • Iron was stronger, more abundant, and cheaper than bronze, leading to improved plows, swords, and armor.
      • Advanced blacksmithing techniques developed, allowing better craftsmanship.
    2. Rise of Great Empires & Civilizations:
      • Many major civilizations flourished, including:
        • Assyrian Empire (Mesopotamia)
        • Persian Empire (Middle East)
        • Ancient Greece (Europe)
        • Roman Empire (Europe & Mediterranean)
        • Maurya Empire (India)
        • Zhou & Han Dynasties (China)
    3. Warfare & Military Innovations:
      • Iron weapons led to stronger armies and large-scale battles.
      • The use of chariots, cavalry, and siege weapons changed warfare.
    4. Urbanization & Trade:
      • Cities expanded, with roads, markets, and fortifications.
      • Long-distance trade spread goods like silk, spices, and glass across continents.
    5. Cultural & Philosophical Advancements:
      • Greek philosophy (Socrates, Plato, Aristotle) and Confucianism (China) emerged.
      • The first historical writings recorded events, such as Herodotus' histories.
    6. Religious Evolution:
      • Polytheism was widespread (Greek, Roman, Norse, Hindu gods).
      • Monotheistic religions like Judaism, Christianity, and Zoroastrianism developed and spread.

    End of the Iron Age

    The Iron Age gradually ended as societies transitioned into the Classical and Medieval periods, with the fall of Rome (476 CE) marking a major turning point in Europe. In some regions, the Iron Age lasted longer due to slower technological advancements.
