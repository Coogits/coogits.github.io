    Complex Number

    A complex number is a mathematical concept used to extend the idea of numbers beyond the real number line, enabling the solution of equations that have no real solutions.
    It is expressed as the sum of two components: a real part and an imaginary part

    Symbols

    • $\mathbb{C}\quad$ Complex numbers set
    • $z\quad$ Complex Number. usually used to represent the complex number.
    • $a\quad$ Real Part. Written as $Re(z)=a$, where $a$ represent a Real Number
    • $b\quad$ Imaginary Part. Written as $Im(z)=b$, where $b$ represent a Real Number
    • $i\quad$ Imaginary Unit, with the defining property that $i^2=-1$

    Formulas

    • Algebraic Form Notation
      A complex number is a number that combines a real part and an imaginary part. It is typically written in the form: $$z=a+bi$$ Due to the commutative property, can be written as (apparently less common): $$z=a+ib$$

    Properties

    • Pure Real Number
      The complex number is purely real if the imaginary part is zero: $$b=0$$
    • Pure Imaginary Number
      The complex number is called pure imaginary number if the real part is zero: $$a=0\text{ and }b\neq0$$

    Graphic Representation

    Complex numbers can be visualized on the **complex plane**, where: - The **real part** $a$ is plotted along the horizontal axis (x-axis), - The **imaginary part** $b$ is plotted along the vertical axis (y-axis). This graph represents the point corresponding to the complex number $z=a+ib$
