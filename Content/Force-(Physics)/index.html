    Force (Physics)

    Force is a physical quantity which describe the interaction that, when unopposed, will change the motion of an object. It is a vector quantity, meaning it has both magnitude and direction. Forces can cause an object to accelerate, decelerate, remain at rest, or change its shape.
    Total Force refers to the vector sum of all the individual forces acting on an object. It represents the net effect of all the forces, taking both their magnitudes and directions into account.


    Symbols

    • $\vec F\quad$ Force (unit: Newton ($N = \cfrac{kg \cdot m}{s^2}$))
    • $m\quad$ Inertial Mass (unit: kilogram ($kg$))
    • $\vec v\quad$ Velocity (unit: meter per second $(\frac{m}{s})$)
    • $\vec p\quad$ Linear Momentum (unit: $\cfrac{kg\cdot m}{s}$)

    Formulas

    • From the Newton's Second Law:
      Is defined in Dynamics from the Principles of Dynamics: $$\vec F=m\vec a$$
    • Force from Momentum:
      the force can be calculate with the Derivative of the momentum: $$\vec{F}=\cfrac{d\vec{p}}{dt}$$

    Properties

    • Superposition Principle:
      Total force calculated as the sum of forces and the rate of change of momentum: $$\vec{F}_{tot}=\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{n}\vec{F}_i$$
