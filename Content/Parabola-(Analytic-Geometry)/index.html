    Parabola

    A parabola is a U-shaped curve that is a conic section formed by the intersection of a plane parallel to the side of a cone. It can be thought of as the set of all points that are equidistant from a fixed point called the focus and a fixed straight line called the directrix.

    Symbols

    • $a\quad$ direction (upward or downward) and width (narrow or wide) of the parabola.
    • $b\quad$ tilt of the parabola (the axis of symmetry's position).
    • $c\quad$ y-intercept, the point where the parabola crosses the y-axis

    Vertical Parabola

    • General form or Quadratic form:
      General representation with the Quadratic Equation[1]Quadratic Equation is the mathematical representation of the parabola : $$y=ax^2+bx+c$$
    • Vertex form:
      Useful for quickly identifying the vertex of the parabola: $$(x-x_0)^2=4p(y-y_0)$$

    Orientation and Parameters

    A parabola's orientation depends on its equation.
    The axis of symmetry is the line that divides the parabola into two mirror-image halves. The focus is a fixed point inside the parabola, while the directrix is a fixed line outside it. Every point on the parabola is equidistant from the focus and the directrix.
