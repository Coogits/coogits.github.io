    Energy (Physics)

    In Physics, energy is a Physical Quantity that measure the capacity to perform work. It is a fundamental concept that quantifies the ability of a system to cause changes in itself or its surroundings. Energy can exist in various forms, such as kinetic energy, potential energy, thermal energy, and more, and can be transferred or transformed between different forms but is never created or destroyed (according to the law of conservation of energy).

    SI Unit

    • The SI Unit is the Joule ($1J$): $$E=1J=1N\cdot m=1kg\cdot m^2\cdot s^{-2}$$

    Energy Forms

    • Classical Mechanics
      • Mechanical Energy $E=K+U$
      • Kinetic Energy $K$
      • Potential Energy $U$
