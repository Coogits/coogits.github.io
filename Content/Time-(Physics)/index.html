    Time (Physics)

    In Physics, time is a fundamental Physical Quantity that measures the duration of events and the intervals between them. It provides a way to order events from the past, through the present, and into the future. Time is considered one of the basic dimensions of the universe, alongside space, and is used to quantify motion, change, and the sequence of events.

    SI Unit

    • The SI Unit is the second (s): $$\textbf{T}=1s$$

    Properties

    • Scalar: Time is a scalar quantity, meaning it has only magnitude and no direction.

    Constant and Absolute

    • In Classical Mechanics, time is typically treated as a constant and absolute parameter,
    • In Modern Physics time can vary depending on the observer's frame of reference.
