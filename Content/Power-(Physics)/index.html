    Power (Physics)

    In Physics, power is a physical quantity that defines the rate at which work is done or energy is transferred or converted.
    Mathematically, it is the derivative of work with respect to time or the ratio of work done (or energy transferred) to the time taken to do that work.

    Symbols

    • $P\quad$ is the power (in watts, W),
    • $W\quad$ is the work done (in joules, J),
    • $t\quad$ is the Time (in seconds, s).

    Formulas

    • Average Power:
      Defined from the Work: $$P=\cfrac{\Delta W}{\Delta{t}}$$
    • Instantaneous Power:
      using the Derivative: $$P(t)=\cfrac{dW}{dt}$$

    SI Units

    • The SI Unit is the Watt (W): $$P=1W=1\cfrac{J}{s}$$
