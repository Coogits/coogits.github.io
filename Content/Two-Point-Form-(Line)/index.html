    Two-Point Form (Line)

    The two-point form of a line is an equation derived when two distinct points on the line are known.

    Symbols

    • $P_1(x_1,y_1)\quad$ A Point
    • $P_2(x_2,y_2)\quad$ Another Point
    • $m\quad$ Slope (Analytic Geometry)

    Definition

    • Two-Point Form
      Given two points $P_1(x_1,y_1)$ and $P_2(x_2,y_2)$, the equation of the line passing through these points is given by: $$\cfrac{y-y_1}{y_2-y_1}=\cfrac{x-x_1}{x_2-x_1}$$

    Conversion

    the Slope and the Intercept for the Slope-intercept form (Line) $y=m x+q$ is given by:

    • Slope:
      From two points $P_1=(x_1,y_1)\quad P_2=(x_2,y_2)$ the slope is: $$m=\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}=\cfrac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}\qquad x_1\neq x_2$$
    • Intercept:
      From two points $P_1=(x_1,y_1)\quad P_2=(x_2,y_2)$ the Intercept is: $$q=-\cfrac{x_2y_1-x_1y_2}{x_2-x_1} \qquad b\neq0$$

    Special Cases

    • Vertical Line:
      If $x_1=x_2$, the denominator becomes zero, indicating an undefined slope. The equation simplifies to: $$x=x_1$$
    • Horizontal Line:
      If $y_1=y_2$, the slope is zero, and the equation simplifies to: $$y=y_1$$
