    Heat Capacity

    Heat capacity is a physical property of a substance that indicates the amount of heat energy required to raise its temperature by a given amount.

    Formulas

    • Definition: This expresses the heat capacity as the amount of heat $\Delta Q$ required to change the temperature by $\Delta T$: $$C=\cfrac{\Delta Q}{\Delta T}$$
    • Relation with Specific Heat: $$C=mc$$
    • At constant volume: In a Isochore transformation: $$C_V=\left(\cfrac{\partial Q}{\partial T}\right)_V$$
    • At constant pressure: In a Isobar transformation: $$C_p=\left(\cfrac{\partial Q}{\partial T}\right)_p$$

    Symbols

    • $\Delta Q\quad$ is the amount of Heat added to the system
    • $\Delta T\quad$ is the resulting change in Temperature.
    • $c\quad$ Specific Heat $c=\cfrac{Q}{m\Delta T}$
    • $\partial\quad$ Partial Derivative

    Heat Capacity Unit

    • SI Unit: $$C=1\;\cfrac{J}{K}=1\;kg\cdot \cfrac{J}{kg\cdot K}$$

    Properties

    • Extensive quantity: its value depends on the amount of substance in the system.
