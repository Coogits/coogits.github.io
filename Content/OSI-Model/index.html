    OSI Model

    The OSI (Open Systems Interconnection) Model is a conceptual framework that standardizes the functions of a telecommunication or computing system into seven hierarchical layers.
    It was developed by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) in 1984 to provide a universal reference model for network communication.

    Each layer in the OSI model defines specific tasks and interactions, ensuring interoperability between different systems, devices, and protocols.
    The model helps design, troubleshoot, and understand network communication by clearly separating different functionalities, from physical transmission to application-level services.

    OSI Model Layers

    Protocols are structured based on the OSI (Open Systems Interconnection) Model, which consists of seven layers:

    1. Physical Layer (OSI)

    (Layer 1) – Transmission Medium & Hardware

    • Electromagnetic Wave – Basics of wireless transmission.
      • Frequency & Wavelength – Spectrum allocation (AM/FM, UHF/VHF, microwave).
      • Signal Power & Decibels (dB) – Measuring signal strength.
    • Signal Modulation
      • AM/FM/PM – Analog modulation types.
      • ASM/FSM/PSM – Digital modulation types.
      • QAM (Quadrature Amplitude Modulation) – Digital modulation ASM and PSM.
      • OFDM (Orthogonal Frequency-Division Multiplexing) – Used in Wi-Fi and 4G/5G.
    • Multiplexing Techniques
      • FDM (Frequency Division Multiplexing).
      • TDM (Time Division Multiplexing).
      • CDMA (Code Division Multiple Access).
      • SDM (Space Division Multiplexing) – Used in fiber optics and MIMO.
      • OFDMA (Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiple Access) – Used in LTE and 5G.
    • Bitrate vs Baud Rate – Data transmission speed vs signal changes.
    • Transmission Media:
      • Copper-based Networks – Twisted Pair (Ethernet), Coaxial Cable (Cable TV).
      • Fiber Optic Networks – Single-mode vs Multi-mode fibers.
      • Wireless Technologies – Cellular (2G-5G), Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Satellite, Microwave.
    • Fiber Optic Communication – Optical data transmission for high-speed networking.
    • DSL (Digital Subscriber Line) & Modems – Broadband internet over telephone lines.
    • Advanced Concepts:
      • MIMO (Multiple Input Multiple Output) – Used in 4G, 5G, and Wi-Fi to improve speed and reliability.
      • Link Budget & Path Loss – Calculating signal degradation over distance.
      • Radio Wave Propagation Models – Reflection, Diffraction, Scattering.
      • Power Line Communication (PLC) – Data transmission over electrical wiring.

    2. Data Link Layer (OSI)

    (Layer 2) – Reliable Data Transfer & Addressing

    • MAC (Medium Access Control) – Controls how devices access the transmission medium.
    • LLC (Logical Link Control) – Manages frame synchronization and error checking.
    • HDLC (High-Level Data Link Control) – Used in WANs for reliable communication.
    • PPP (Point-to-Point Protocol) – Used in direct communication links.
    • VLAN (Virtual LAN) – Isolates network segments within a LAN.
    • Ethernet (802.3) – Standard for wired LAN communication.
    • Wi-Fi (802.11) – Wireless LAN standard.
    • Error Detection & Correction – Parity, CRC, Hamming Code.
    • Wireless Technologies:
      • Wi-Fi (IEEE 802.11) – Wireless communication in local networks.
      • Bluetooth (IEEE 802.15) – Short-range wireless communication.
      • 2G (GSM, CDMA) – Circuit-switched voice and SMS.
      • 3G (UMTS, CDMA2000) – Introduced packet-switching.
      • 4G (LTE, LTE-Advanced) – High-speed mobile broadband.
      • 5G NR (New Radio) – mmWave & Sub-6 GHz – Ultra-fast mobile data.
    • Key Protocols:
      • CSMA/CD (Carrier Sense Multiple Access with Collision Detection) – Used in Ethernet.
      • CSMA/CA (Carrier Sense Multiple Access with Collision Avoidance) – Used in Wi-Fi.
      • Frame Relay & ATM (Asynchronous Transfer Mode) – Older but still relevant in some networks.
    • Industrial & IoT Networks:
      • Industrial Ethernet (PROFINET, EtherCAT, Modbus-TCP) – Used in manufacturing.
      • Fieldbus Protocols (CAN, PROFIBUS) – Real-time control systems.

    3. Network Layer (OSI)

    (Layer 3) – Addressing & Routing

    • IP (Internet Protocol) – IPv4 & IPv6 – Governs addressing and packet delivery.
      • IPv6 Transition Mechanisms – Dual Stack, Tunneling, NAT64.
      • Autonomous Systems & ASN (Autonomous System Numbers) – Used in BGP routing.
    • ICMP (Internet Control Message Protocol) – Error reporting and diagnostics.
    • ARP (Address Resolution Protocol) – Maps IP addresses to MAC addresses.
    • NAT (Network Address Translation) – Enables multiple devices to share a public IP.
    • Routing Protocols:
      • Static vs Dynamic Routing.
      • BGP (Border Gateway Protocol) – Manages routing between ISPs.
      • OSPF (Open Shortest Path First) – Dynamic routing for large networks.
      • RIP (Routing Information Protocol) – Simple distance-vector routing protocol.
    • Subnetting & IP Address Allocation – CIDR, NAT, DHCP.
    • SDN (Software-Defined Networking) & Virtualization:
      • SDN (Software-Defined Networking) – Separates control and data planes.
      • NFV (Network Function Virtualization) – Virtualizing network services (firewalls, load balancer

    4. Transport Layer (OSI)

    (Layer 4) – End-to-End Communication

    • TCP (Transmission Control Protocol) – Reliable, connection-oriented communication.
    • UDP (User Datagram Protocol) – Fast, connectionless communication.
    • SCTP (Stream Control Transmission Protocol) – Enhanced version of TCP for signaling.
    • DCCP (Datagram Congestion Control Protocol) – Supports real-time multimedia applications.
    • Flow Control & Congestion Control – Windowing, Buffering.
    • Network Performance Metrics – Latency, Jitter, Packet Loss. Advanced
    • QUIC (Quick UDP Internet Connections) – Faster than TCP, used by Google and HTTP/3.
    • RTP/RTCP (Real-time Transport Protocol & Control Protocol) – Used in VoIP and streaming.

    5. Session Layer (OSI)

    (Layer 5) – Connection Management

    • SIP (Session Initiation Protocol) – Establishes and manages VoIP and multimedia sessions.
    • PPTP (Point-to-Point Tunneling Protocol) – Used for VPNs.
    • RPC (Remote Procedure Call) – Enables communication between programs on different systems.
    • NetBIOS (Network Basic Input/Output System) – Used for LAN communication.
    • Authentication & Authorization – Managing user sessions.
    • Session Establishment & Termination – Handshake mechanisms.
    • LEO/MEO/GEO Satellites – Starlink, OneWeb, VSAT.
    • Ka-band, Ku-band Communications – Satellite frequency bands.

    6. Presentation Layer (OSI)

    (Layer 6) – Data Formatting & Encryption

    • SSL/TLS (Secure Sockets Layer / Transport Layer Security) – Encrypts network communication.
    • JPEG, PNG, MP3, MP4 – Compression and formatting for images, audio, and video.
    • ASCII, Unicode – Text encoding standards.
    • MIME (Multipurpose Internet Mail Extensions) – Formats multimedia email content.
    • Data Encoding & Compression – ASCII, Unicode, MPEG, JPEG.
    • Data Encryption & Security – TLS/SSL, AES, RSA.
    • Cybersecurity & Network Management:
      • RADIUS & TACACS+ – Authentication protocols for ISPs and enterprises.
      • Zero Trust Networking (ZTN) – Security approach for modern networks.
      • Network Telemetry & AI-driven Optimization – Used in 5G and SD-WAN.

    7. Application Layer (OSI)

    (Layer 7) – High-Level Communication Services

    • Web Protocols:
      • HTTP/HTTPS (Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure) – Web communication.
    • File Transfer Protocols:
      • FTP (File Transfer Protocol), SFTP (Secure FTP) – File transfers.
    • Email Protocols:
      • SMTP (Simple Mail Transfer Protocol), IMAP, POP3 – Email transmission.
    • Name Resolution:
      • DNS (Domain Name System) – Translates domain names to IP addresses.
    • VoIP (Voice over IP) Protocols:
      • SIP (Session Initiation Protocol) – Manages voice/video calls.
      • RTP (Real-time Transport Protocol) – Transports multimedia data.
      • H.323 – VoIP and video conferencing standard.
