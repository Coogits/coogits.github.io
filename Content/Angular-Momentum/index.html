    Angular Momentum

    Angular momentum is a quantity that represents the rotational analog of linear momentum. It is a measure of the rotational motion of an object and is defined as the cross product of the position vector and the linear momentum of the object.

    Symbols

    • $\vec L\quad$ is the angular momentum,
    • $\vec r\quad$ is the position vector of the object relative to the axis of rotation,
    • $\vec p=m\vec v\quad$ is the linear momentum of the object, with $m$ being the object's mass and $\vec v$ being its velocity.
    • $I\quad$ Moment Of Inertia
    • $\vec\omega\quad$ Angular Velocity
    • $\theta\quad$ is the angle between $\vec r$ and $\vec p$

    Formulas

    • Angular Momentum Definition:
      Is given by the Cross Product (Vector): $$\vec L=\vec r\times \vec p$$ where the Magnitude is given by the cross product magnitude definition: $$L=rp\sin\theta$$
    • Angular Momentum around a fixed axis:
      If the object is rotating around a fixed axis, the angular momentum simplifies to: $$\vec L=I\vec\omega$$
    • Angular Momentum Theorem (Time Derivative Form):
      Also known as the Rotational Analog of Newtonâ€™s Second Law, this theorem states that: $$\vec\tau=\cfrac{d\vec L}{dt}$$
      • $\vec\tau\quad$ is the net external torque acting on the system.
    • Angular Momentum Conservation Theorem:
      In a Isolated System the angular moment is constant: $$\cfrac{d\vec L}{dt}=0 \quad\implies\quad \vec L_i=\vec L_f$$

    SI Unit

    • The Si unit is the kilogram-meter squared per seconds : $$\vec L=\cfrac{kg\cdot m^2}{s}$$

    Key Points:

    1. Angular momentum is a vector quantity, meaning it has both magnitude and direction.
    2. It is conserved in an isolated system if no external torque acts on the system (angular momentum conservation).
    3. Angular momentum is related to the rotational inertia and angular velocity of a rotating object.
