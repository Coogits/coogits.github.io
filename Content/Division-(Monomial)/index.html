    Division (Monomials)

    When dividing monomials, you divide the numerical coefficients and subtract the exponents of corresponding variables. This process applies to each variable separately.

    Formulas

    • Rule:
      To divide monomials, divide the coefficients and subtract the exponents of each corresponding variable. For example, given: $$\cfrac{ax_0^{k_0}x_1^{k_1}\ldots x_n^{k_n}}{bx_0^{m_0}x_1^{m_1}\ldots x_n^{m_n}}$$ The result is: $$\cfrac{a}{b}bx_0^{k_0-m_0}x_1^{k_1-m_1}\ldots x_n^{k_n-m_n}$$

    Examples

    • Example 1: $$\cfrac{4x^5}{2x^3} \quad\rightarrow\quad \cfrac{4}{2}x^{5-3} \quad\rightarrow\quad 2x^2$$
    • Example 2: $$\cfrac{4x^5y}{2x^3} \quad\rightarrow\quad \cfrac{4}{2}x^{5-3}y \quad\rightarrow\quad 2x^2y$$
    • Example with a Variable in the Divisor Not Present in the Dividend:
      In cases where a variable appears in the divisor but not in the dividend (implying an exponent of 0 in the dividend), the result will include a negative exponent. For instance: $$\cfrac{4x^5}{2x^3y} \quad\rightarrow\quad \cfrac{4}{2}x^{5-3}y^{-1} \quad\rightarrow\quad 2x^2y^{-1}$$
    • Example with a Smaller Exponent in the Dividend:
      If the exponent of a variable in the dividend is smaller than that in the divisor, the result will also feature a negative exponent: $$\cfrac{4x^2}{2x^3} \quad\rightarrow\quad \cfrac{4}{2}x^{2-3} \quad\rightarrow\quad 2x^{-1}$$

    Limitations

    Conditions for a Monomial Division to Yield Another Monomial (in the Traditional Sense):

    • Presence of Variables:
      Every variable in the divisor must also be present in the dividend. This ensures that each variable in the divisor has a corresponding term in the dividend.

    • Exponent Condition:
      The exponent of each variable in the dividend must be greater than or equal to the exponent in the divisor. This condition ensures that subtracting the exponents does not result in a negative exponent.

    • Important Note:
      If either condition is not met, the division is still mathematically valid, but the result will not be a monomial in the traditional sense. Instead, it may involve negative exponents or represent a rational expression.
