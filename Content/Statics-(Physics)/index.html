    Statics (Physics)

    Statics is the branch of Classical Mechanics that studies objects in equilibrium, meaning they are either at rest or moving at a constant velocity (i.e., with zero acceleration). It focuses on analyzing forces and torques that act on a system to ensure it remains in a stable state.

    Key Principles of Statics

    1. Equilibrium Conditions – An object is in equilibrium if:
      • Translational Equilibrium: The sum of all external forces is zero: $$\sum\vec F=0$$
      • Rotational Equilibrium: The sum of all external torques about any point is zero: $$\sum\vec\tau=0$$
    2. Rigid Body Assumption – Objects are often assumed to be rigid, meaning they do not deform under applied forces.

    Difference Between Statics and Dynamics

    • Statics: Studies objects with zero acceleration $\vec a=0$.
    • Dynamics: Studies objects undergoing acceleration due to unbalanced forces ($\vec a\neq0$).
