    Trigonometric Function

    Trigonometric functions are mathematical functions that relate the angles of a right-angled triangle to the ratios of its sides. They are fundamental in trigonometry and are widely used in geometry, physics, engineering, and periodic phenomena.

    Functions

    The six primary trigonometric functions are:

    Sine

    • $\sin()$:
      Ratio of the opposite side to the hypotenuse: $$\sin\theta=\cfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$$

    Cosine

    • $\cos()$
      Ratio of the adjacent side to the hypotenuse: $$\cos\theta=\cfrac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$$

    Tangent

    • $\tan()$:
      Ratio of the opposite side to the adjacent side: $$\tan\theta=\cfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}=\cfrac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$$

    Cosecant

    • $\csc()$
      Reciprocal of sine: $$\csc\theta=\cfrac{1}{\sin\theta}$$

    Secant

    • $\sec()$:
      Reciprocal of cosine: $$\sec\theta=\cfrac{1}{\cos\theta}$$

    Cotangent

    • $\cot()$:
      Reciprocal of tangent: $$\cot\theta=\cfrac{1}{\tan\theta}$$

    Unit Circle Representation

    Trigonometric functions can also be defined using the unit circle, where a point $(x,y)$ on the circle satisfies:

    • $\sin\theta=y$
    • $\cos\theta=x$
    • $\tan\theta=\cfrac{y}{x}\qquad x\neq0$
