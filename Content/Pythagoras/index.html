    Pythagoras

    Pythagoras was an ancient Greek mathematician, philosopher, and religious leader best known for the Pythagorean Theorem in mathematics. He founded the Pythagorean school, which combined mathematics, philosophy, and mysticism.

    • Born: c. 570 BC
    • Died: c. 495 BC

    Timeline

    Key Contributions

    Pythagorean Theorem

    • The famous theorem states:
      (In a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides.) $$c^2=a^2+b^2$$

    Mathematics

    • Although the theorem was known in Babylonian mathematics earlier, Pythagoras or his followers provided a logical proof.

    Numerical Mysticism & Philosophy

    • Pythagoreans believed numbers had divine properties and could explain the universe.
    • They saw 1 as unity, 2 as duality, 3 as harmony, and 10 as the perfect number.
    • Introduced the idea of mathematics as a fundamental truth of nature.

    Music & Harmony

    • Pythagoras studied how musical notes and harmonies are based on mathematical ratios.
    • He discovered that the length of a vibrating string determines its pitch, laying the foundation for music theory.

    Astronomy & Cosmology

    • Proposed that the Earth was a sphere (though not the first to suggest it).
    • Believed in a geocentric model (Earth at the center) and that planets moved in perfect mathematical harmony.

    Pythagorean Brotherhood

    • A secret philosophical and religious society that followed strict rules, including vegetarianism.
    • Focused on mathematics, spirituality, and moral teachings.
    • Members followed the belief in reincarnation and the immortality of the soul.

    Legacy & Influence:

    • Pythagoras greatly influenced Plato, Aristotle, and later mathematicians.
    • His ideas shaped Western philosophy, mathematics, music, and astronomy.
    • His theorem is still a fundamental principle in geometry and engineering today.
