    Sine (Trigonometry)

    The sine function ($\sin$) is a fundamental trigonometric function that relates the angle of a right triangle to the ratio of its sides.

    Symbols

    • $\text{opposite}\quad$ The side opposite to the angle $\theta$.
    • $\text{hypotenuse}\quad$ The longest side of the right triangle.
    • $y\quad$ vertical coordinate of the point on the unit circle corresponding to the angle $\theta$.

    Definitions

    • Right Triangle Definition:
      For a given angle $\theta$ in a right triangle: $$\sin\theta=\cfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$$
    • Unit Circle Definition:
      In the unit circle, where a point $(x, y)$ represents an angle $\theta$ measured from the positive $x$-axis: $$\sin\theta=y$$

    Properties

    • Periodicity: $\quad\sin(\theta+2\pi)=\sin\theta$
    • Odd Function: $\quad\sin(-\theta)=-\sin\theta$
    • Range: $\quad-1\leq\sin(\theta)\leq1$
    • Pythagorean Identity: $\quad \sin^2\theta-\cos^2\theta=1$

    Theorems

    Most common Theorems (Trigonometry) are:

    • Angle Addition and Subtraction Formulas: $$\sin(\alpha\pm\beta)=\sin\alpha\cos\beta\pm\sin\beta\cos\alpha$$
    • Law of Sines: $$2r=\dfrac{a}{\sin\alpha}=\dfrac{b}{\sin\beta}=\dfrac{c}{\sin\gamma}$$
