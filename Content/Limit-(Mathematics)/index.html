    Limit (Mathematic)

    The limit is a fundamental concept used to describe the behavior of a function or sequence as its argument approaches a particular point, often related to concepts like continuity, differentiation, and integration.

    Definitions

    • Intuitive Definition:
      The limit of a function $f(x)$ as $x$ approaches a value $x_0$ is written as: $$\lim_{x\to x_0}f(x)=L$$ This means that as $x$ gets arbitrarily close to $x_0$, the function value $f(x)$ approaches $L$.

    • Limit of a Sequence (Formal Definition):
      For a sequence ${a_n}$, the limit as $n$ approaches infinity is given by: $$\displaystyle\lim_{n\to \infty}a_n=L$$ which means that for every $\epsilon > 0$, there exists an $N \in \mathbb{N}$ such that for all $n > N$: $$\lvert a_n-L\rvert<\epsilon$$ This describes how the sequence gets arbitrarily close to $L$ as $n$ increases.

    • Formal Definition of a Limit (Epsilon-Delta Definition):
      For a function $f(x)$ defined on some open interval around $x_0$, the limit of $f(x)$ as $x$ approaches $x_0$ is: $$\lim_{x\to x_0}f(x)=L$$ which formally means: $$\forall\epsilon>0,\;\exists\delta>0\;:\;0<\lvert x-x_0\rvert<\delta\implies\lvert f(x)-L\rvert<\epsilon$$ This means that for every small $\epsilon$ (desired closeness to $L$), there exists a corresponding small $\delta$ (closeness of $x$ to $x_0$) such that whenever $x$ is within this $\delta$-distance of $x_0$ (but not equal to $x_0$), then $f(x)$ will be within $\epsilon$-distance of $L$.

    • Formal Definition (Compact Notation):
      A more compact way to express the definition using neighborhoods: $$\displaystyle\lim_{x\to x_0} f(x)=L\in\mathbb{R} \iff \forall V_{\epsilon}(L)\;\exists U_{\delta}(x_0) \;:\; x\in U_{\delta}(x_0) \cap \operatorname{Dom}\setminus\{x_0\} \implies f(x)\in V_{\epsilon}(L)$$where:

      • $V_{\epsilon}(L) = (L - \epsilon, L + \epsilon)$ is an $\epsilon$-neighborhood of $L$.
      • $U_{\delta}(x_0) = (x_0 - \delta, x_0 + \delta)$ is a $\delta$-neighborhood of $x_0$.
      • $\operatorname{Dom}(f)\quad$ refers to the domain of $f$.
      • $:\quad$ mean "such that"
      • $\setminus\{x_0\}\quad$ The exclusion of ${x_0}$ ensures that the limit only depends on nearby values, not necessarily the function's value at $x_0$ itself.

    One-Sided Limits

    In some cases, it is useful to consider limits from one direction:

    • Left-hand limit: $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to x_0^-}f(x)=L$ (approaching $x_0$ from the left).
    • Right-hand limit: $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to x_0^+}f(x)=L$ (approaching $x_0$ from the right).

    For a two-sided limit to exist, the left-hand and right-hand limits must both exist and be equal.

    Limit at Infinity

    We may also be interested in the behavior of functions or sequences as they approach infinity:

    • Finite Limits:
      When limit approach a finite value: $$\lim\limits_{x \to c} f(x) = L$$
    • Infinite Limits:
      Limit can approach to $+\infty$ or $-\infty$: $$\lim\limits_{x \to c} f(x) = \pm\infty$$

    Properties

    Limit Laws

    • Sum Rule:
      Defined as: $$\lim\limits_{x \to c} (f(x) + g(x)) = \lim\limits_{x \to c} f(x) + \lim\limits_{x \to c} g(x)$$
    • Product Rule:
      Defined as: $$\lim\limits_{x \to c} (f(x) \cdot g(x)) = \lim\limits_{x \to c} f(x) \cdot \lim\limits_{x \to c} g(x)$$
    • Quotient Rule:
      Defined As: $$\lim\limits_{x \to c} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \frac{\lim\limits_{x \to c} f(x)}{\lim\limits_{x \to c} g(x)}, \quad \text{if } \lim\limits_{x \to c} g(x) \neq 0$$

    Squeeze Theorem

    • Definition:
      If $h(x) \leq f(x) \leq g(x)$ and $\lim\limits_{x \to c} h(x) = \lim\limits_{x \to c} g(x) = L$, then $\lim\limits_{x \to c} f(x) = L$.

    Relationship to Continuity

    A function $f(x)$ is continuous at $x = c$ if:

    • $\lim\limits_{x \to c} f(x)$ exists
    • $f(c)$ is defined
    • $\lim\limits_{x \to c} f(x) = f(c)$
