    Numeral System Conversion

    Numeral system conversion is the process of transforming a number from one numerical base to another. This is essential for computations, digital systems, and various mathematical applications. Different numeral systems, such as binary (base 2), decimal (base 10), and hexadecimal (base 16), are commonly used in computing and electronics.

    Symbols

    • $N_{B}\quad$ The "from" number.
    • $N_n\quad$ The "to" number.
    • $a_i\quad$ The base "to" digit at positional weight $i$
    • $i\quad$ The base "to" digit's positional weight.
    • $n\quad$ The count of the "to" digits.

    division-remainder algorithm

    The division-remainder method (or repeated division method) is commonly used to convert integer numbers from decimal (base 10) to another base, such as binary (base 2), octal (base 8), or hexadecimal (base 16). The process involves repeated division by the target base, storing the remainders as the digits of the new base.

    1. While $N_B>0$:
      while the number $N_B$ is greater than zero:
      • Compute the remainder when $N_B$ is divided by the target base $b$: This remainder represents the next digit in the converted number. $$a_i \leftarrow \mbox{mod}(N_{B}, b)$$
      • Update $N_B$ using integer division by $b$: $$N_{B} \leftarrow \left\lfloor \cfrac{N_{B}}{b} \right\rfloor$$
      • Increase $i$ and reiterate
    2. Result:
      • The sequence of remainders, read in reverse order, forms the equivalent number in the target base.
      • This corresponds to its expanded form in the new numeral system.

    Math Pseudocode

    $$ \begin{align} &\textbf{Algorithm division-remainder} \\ &\text{Initialize:} \quad i \leftarrow 0 \\ &\textbf{while: } N_{B} > 0 \\ &\qquad a_i \leftarrow \mbox{mod}(N_{B}, b) \\ &\qquad N_{B} \leftarrow \left\lfloor \cfrac{N_{B}}{b} \right\rfloor \\ &\qquad i \leftarrow i + 1 \\ &n\leftarrow i \\ &N_b = \sum_{i=0}^{n} a_i \cdot b^i \end{align} $$
