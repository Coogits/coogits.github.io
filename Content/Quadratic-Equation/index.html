    Quadratic Equation

    Quadratic Equation is an equation that involves at least one quadratic Polynomial.

    Key Points

    • Degree:
      The highest exponent of the variable is 2.
    • Solutions:
      has up to two solutions, which may be distinct or identical.

    Symbols

    • $a,b,c,\ldots\quad$ parameters or coefficients of the equation.
    • $x,y,\ldots\quad$ variables.
    • $x_1,x_2\quad$ roots or solutions of the equation, determined using the Discriminant Formula:
    • $\Delta\quad$ Discriminant, defined $\Delta=b^2-4ac$.

    Formulas

    Definition

    • General form:
      A quadratic equation can be expressed in its most general form as: $$\sum_ia_ix_i^2+\sum_ja_jx_j+\sum_{k\leq l}a_{kl}x_kx_l+c=0$$
      • $\sum_ia_ix_i^2\quad$ represent the pure quadratic terms (e.g. $ax^2,ay^2,\ldots$);
      • $\sum_ja_jx_j\quad$ represent the linear terms (e.g. $bx,by,\ldots$)
      • $\sum_{k\leq l}a_{kl}x_kx_l\quad$ represent the mixed terms (e.g. $axy,bxy,\ldots$)

    Forms

    • Standard Form (Quadratic Equation):
      Is a representation where the terms are organized in a consistent and simplified manner: $$ax^2\pm bx \pm c=0 \qquad a\neq0$$
    • Quadratic Equation in Factored Form:
      Useful for quickly finding the roots. $$x(x\pm a)=0$$
    • Quadratic Formula form:
      Applied to a Quadratic standard form (or that can be converted into) can retrieve the roots of the equation. $$x_{1,2}=\cfrac{-b\pm\sqrt{\Delta}}{2a}\qquad\Delta=b^2-4ac$$

    Applications

    • Parabola (Analytic Geometry):
      Is the graphic representation of the Standard form
    • Circle (Analytic Geometry):
      The circle equation is a quadratic equation in two variables.
