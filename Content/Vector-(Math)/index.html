    Vector (Math)

    A vector is a mathematical object that has both magnitude (length) and direction. Vectors are fundamental to many areas of mathematics and physics, especially in representing quantities such as force, velocity, and displacement. A vector can be represented in different ways, such as an ordered pair or ordered triple (in 2D or 3D space), or more generally as an n-tuple in n-dimensional space.

    Symbols

    • $v_1,v_2,\ldots,v_n\quad$ Spatial Coordinates of the vector, mostly represented with $x,y,z,\ldots$

    Formulas and Notations

    Notations

    • Vector in n-dimensional space:
      is typically denoted as: $$\vec{v}=(v_1,v_2,\ldots,v_n)$$
    • Vector component form:
      Best for physics and applied motion problems: $$\vec{v}=v_x\hat{i} + v_y\hat{j} + v_z\hat{k}$$
    • Parametric vector form:
      Best for linear algebra-style presentation: $$\vec{v}=\begin{bmatrix}x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix}$$

    Formulas

    • Magnitude:
      Is the length of the vector, given by the Pythagorean Theorem: $$\lvert\vec{v}\lvert=\sqrt{v_1^2+v_2^2+\ldots+v_n^2}$$
    • Unit Vector:
      corresponding to $\vec{v}$, which has a magnitude of 1 and points in the same direction: $$\hat{v}= \cfrac{\overrightarrow{v}}{\lvert\overrightarrow{v}\lvert}$$

    Operators

    Basic Operators

    • Vectors Addition/Subtraction:
      Is done by summing/subtracting the vector's components. $$\vec{v_1}\pm\vec{v_2}=(x_1\pm x_2,y_1\pm y_2,\ldots)$$
    • Vector Multiplication/Division:
      Between a vector and a scalar $\lambda$: $$\lambda\vec{v}=(\lambda v_x,\lambda v_y,\ldots)$$

    Special Operators

    • Dot Product:
      Also called Scalar Product: $$\vec{v_1}\cdot\vec{v_2}$$
    • Cross Product:
      Also called Vector product: $$\vec{v_1}\times\vec{v_2}$$

    Applications

    • In physics, vectors are used to represent physical quantities such as displacement, velocity, and force.
    • In computer graphics, vectors are used to represent points, directions, and transformations in space.
    • In engineering, vectors model systems with multiple forces or velocities.
    • In machine learning, vectors represent features of data in high-dimensional space.
