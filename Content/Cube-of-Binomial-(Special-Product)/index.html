    Cube of Binomial (Special Product)

    The cube of a binomial is a Special Products refers to raising a binomial expression to the power of three. The expansion results in a polynomial with four terms, following a specific pattern based on the binomial theorem.

    Symbols

    • $A,B\quad$ Monomial.
    • $(A \pm B)^3\quad$ Factorized form or binomial cube form.
    • $A^3 \pm 3A^2B + 3AB^2 \pm B^3\quad$ Expanded form.
    • $\pm\quad$ Indicates either a plus or minus, depending on the binomial sign. For example:
      • $(A - B)^3=A^3 - 3A^2B + 3AB^2 - B^3$
      • $(A + B)^3=A^3 + 3A^2B + 3AB^2 + B^3$

    Formulas

    • Expansion:
      When raising a binomial expression (Factorized form) to the third power (Expanded form): $$(A \pm B)^3=A^3 \pm 3A^2B + 3AB^2 \pm B^3$$
    • Factorization:
      The factorization of the expanded form follows the reverse of the expansion: $$A^3 \pm 3A^2B + 3AB^2 \pm B^3=(A \pm B)^3$$
