    Scientific Notation

    Scientific Notation: is a way of expressing very large or very small numbers in the form of a product of two factors: a number between 1 and 10 (called the mantissa) and a power of 10 (called the exponent). This notation makes it easier to handle numbers that are too cumbersome to write out in standard decimal form.

    Symbols and Terminology

    • $x\quad$ Mantissa: A rational number between 1 (inclusive) and the base (exclusive), i.e. $1\leq x<b$.
    • $b\quad$ Base: the Numeral system's base.
    • $n\quad$ Exponent: The power of $b$, which is an integer (it can be positive, negative, or zero).
    • $\mathbb{Q}\quad$ Rational Number: The set of numbers that can be expressed as a fraction of two integers.
    • $\mathbb{Z}\quad$ Integer Number: The set of whole numbers, both positive, negative, and zero.

    Notation

    • Base Scientific Notation:
      This notation can be applied to any Numeral System and represents numbers in the form: $$x \cdot b^{n} \qquad x\in\mathbb{Q};\; 1\leq x<b;\; n\in\mathbb{Z}$$
    • Decimal Scientific Notation:
      The notation applied with the base $b=10$: $$x \cdot 10^{n} \qquad x\in\mathbb{Q};\; 1\leq x<10;\; n\in\mathbb{Z}$$
