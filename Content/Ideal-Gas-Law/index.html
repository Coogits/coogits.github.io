    Ideal Gas Law

    The ideal gas law is a fundamental equation in thermodynamics that relates the pressure, volume, temperature, and amount of an ideal gas.

    Formulas

    • Ideal Gas Law: $$pV=nRT$$
    • Ideal Gas Law - Alternative Form: $$pV=Nk_BT$$

    Symbols

    • $p\quad$ Pressure of the gas ( unit: Pascal (Pa) )
    • $V\quad$ is the volume it occupies (unit: meter cubic ($m^3$) )
    • $n\quad$ is the Number of Mole of the gas,
    • $R\quad$ is the Universal Gas Constant ( $R=N_Ak_B\simeq8,31\frac{J}{mol\cdot K}$ )
    • $T\quad$ Temperature (unit: Kelvin (K) )
    • $N\quad$ total number of particles ( $N=nN_A$ )
    • $k_B\quad$ Boltzmann Constant ( $k_B\simeq1,38\cdot 10^{-23}\frac{J}{K} )$
    • $N_A\quad$ Avogadro Constant ( $N_A=6,022\cdot10^{23}\,mol^{-1}$ )
