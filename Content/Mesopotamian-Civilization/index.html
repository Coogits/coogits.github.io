    Mesopotamian

    • Start: c. 3,000 BCE
    • End: 539 BCE

    Timeline

    Civilizations

    Early Mesopotamian Cultures (Prehistoric & Early Dynastic)

    • Ubaid Period (c. 6500–3800 BCE) – Early settlements, agriculture, and temple structures.
    • Uruk Period (c. 4000–3100 BCE) – First cities (e.g., Uruk), the invention of writing (cuneiform).
    • Jemdet Nasr Period (c. 3100–2900 BCE) – Proto-states, more advanced writing, trade expansion.

    Sumer

    (c. 2900–2000 BCE)

    • Early Dynastic Period (c. 2900–2334 BCE) – City-states like Ur, Uruk, and Lagash; ziggurats; first kings.
    • Akkadian Empire (2334–2154 BCE) – Founded by Sargon of Akkad, first empire in history.
    • Gutian Period (2154–2112 BCE) – Collapse of Akkadian rule, brief Gutian control.
    • Neo-Sumerian Period (Ur III Dynasty, c. 2112–2004 BCE) – Revival of Sumerian culture under rulers like Ur-Nammu.

    Babylonia

    (c. 2000–539 BCE)

    • Old Babylonian Period (c. 2000–1595 BCE) – Rise of Babylon under Hammurabi, legal codes.
    • Kassite Period (c. 1595–1155 BCE) – Foreign rulers, stability, cultural achievements.
    • Middle Babylonian Period (c. 1155–626 BCE) – Decline, Assyrian domination.
    • Neo-Babylonian Empire (626–539 BCE) – Famous rulers like Nebuchadnezzar II, Hanging Gardens, fall to Persia.

    Assyrian

    (c. 2000–612 BCE)

    • Old Assyrian Period (c. 2025–1378 BCE) – Early trading networks.
    • Middle Assyrian Period (c. 1365–1050 BCE) – Expansion, military strength.
    • Neo-Assyrian Empire (c. 911–612 BCE) – Largest empire of its time, known for warfare, fell to Babylonians and Medes.

    Persian Civilization

    (Achaemenid Empire, 539–330 BCE)

    • Mesopotamia became a province under Persian control after Cyrus the Great conquered Babylon.
