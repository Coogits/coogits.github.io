    Electric Resistivity

    Electric resistivity ($\rho$) is an intrinsic property of a material that quantifies its opposition to the flow of electric current. It reflects how strongly a material resists the movement of charge carriers and is independent of the material's shape or size.

    • Definition:
      Resistivity measures the inherent ability of a material to resist electric current.

    Symbols

    • $\rho\quad$ Electric Resistivity (unit: $\Omega\cdot m$)
    • $\sigma\quad$ Electric Conductivity (unit: Siemens per meters ($\frac{S}{m}$))
    • $R\quad$ Electrical Resistance (unit: Ohm ($\Omega$))
    • $l\quad$ Length of the conductor (unit: meter (m))
    • $A\quad$ Cross-Sectional Area of the conductor (unit: square meter ($m^2$))

    Formulas

    • Relationship to Resistance:
      The resistance $R$ of a uniform conductor is related to its resistivity by the formula $R=\rho\cfrac{l}{A}$, rearranging the expression gives: $$\rho=\cfrac{l}{RA}$$
    • Relationship to Conductivity:
      The electric resistivity is the reciprocal of the electrical conductivity: $$\rho=\cfrac{1}{\sigma}$$
