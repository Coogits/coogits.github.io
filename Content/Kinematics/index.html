    Kinematics

    Kinematics is the branch of classical mechanics that deals with the motion of objects without considering the forces that cause the motion. It focuses on describing the position, velocity, and acceleration of objects as functions of time.

    Key Concepts

    • Position ($s$):
      The location of an object in space, often described by coordinates (e.g., $x,y,z$) in a given reference frame.
    • Displacement ($\Delta s$):
      The change in an object's position, a vector quantity that points from the initial position to the final position.
    • Speed ($v$):
      The rate at which an object moves along a path, scalar in nature (magnitude only).
    • Velocity ($\vec v$ ): The rate of change of position with direction (vector quantity). It describes both the speed and direction of an object.
    • Acceleration ($a$):
      The rate of change of velocity with respect to time. It tells how quickly an object is speeding up or slowing down.

    Type of Motions

    Linear Motions

    • Uniform Linear Motion:
      Motion where an object moves at a constant velocity along a straight line.
    • Uniformly Accelerated Linear Motion:
      Motion where an object moves with constant acceleration along a straight line.
    • Parabolic Motion:
      The motion of an object projected into the air, influenced by gravity, following a curved path.

    Circular Motions

    • Uniform Circular Motion:
      Motion of an object moving in a circle at a constant speed.
    • Uniformly Accelerated Circular Motion:
      Rotation of an object with constant angular acceleration.

    Harmonic Motions

    • Simple Harmonic Motion:
      A type of oscillatory motion where the restoring force is directly proportional to the displacement from equilibrium.

    Rotational Motion

    • Rotational Motion:
      Motion involving rotation around an axis (e.g., a spinning top).
