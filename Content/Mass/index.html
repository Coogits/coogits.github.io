    Mass (Physics)

    In Physics, mass is a fundamental property of matter that quantifies the amount of substance in an object.
    It is a measure of an object's resistance to changes in its state of motion, also known as inertia.
    Mass is often associated with the gravitational attraction between objects, as it determines the strength of the gravitational force acting on an object.

    Symbols

    Types of Mass

    In physics, mass can be categorized into different types based on its behavior in various contexts. These include:

    • Inertial Mass:
      • Definition: Inertial mass measures an object's resistance to changes in its state of motion when a force is applied to it. The greater the inertial mass, the smaller the acceleration produced by a given force.

    • Gravitational Mass:
      • Definition: Gravitational mass determines the strength of the gravitational force that an object experiences when placed in a gravitational field. It is also the quantity that determines the gravitational force between two objects, according to Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation.
    • Rest Mass:
      • Definition: Rest mass refers to the intrinsic mass of an object when it is at rest (i.e., in the frame of reference in which it is not moving). This is the mass that does not change regardless of the object's velocity.
    • Effective Mass (in fields like electromagnetism and quantum mechanics):
      • Definition: In some contexts, particularly in quantum mechanics and solid-state physics, effective mass refers to the behavior of charged particles (like electrons) in a medium (like a semiconductor), where their response to forces behaves as if they have a different mass from their intrinsic rest mass.
    • Relativistic Mass:
      • Definition: In special relativity, relativistic mass refers to the increase in an object's mass as it approaches the speed of light. According to Einstein's theory of relativity, as an object moves faster, its relativistic mass increases, which in turn requires more force to continue accelerating it.
