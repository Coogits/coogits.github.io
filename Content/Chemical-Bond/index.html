    Chemical Bound

    A chemical bond is a force of attraction that holds atoms, ions, or molecules together in a chemical compound.
    These bonds form because atoms seek to achieve a more stable electron configuration, often resembling that of a noble gas (full valence shell).
    Chemical bonds involve the interaction of valence electrons between participating atoms.

    Bond Energy

    Bond energy (also known as bond dissociation energy) is the amount of energy required to break a chemical bond in a molecule, separating the atoms and converting the molecule into individual atoms.
    It represents the strength of the bond and is typically measured in kilojoules per mole (kJ/mol) or calories per mole (cal/mol).

    Strong Chemical Bonds

    Strong chemical bonds form between atoms based on the difference in electronegativity (ΔE) — a measure of how strongly an atom attracts electrons in a bond.

    • Formula:
      Electronegativity Difference ($\Delta E$) determines the type of bond:: $$\begin{cases}\Delta E<0.4&\to\text{Covalent, Nonpolar}\\\Delta E=\{0.4, 1.9\}&\to\text{Covalent, Polar}\\\Delta E>1.9&\to\text{Ionic}\end{cases}$$

    Covalent Bond

    • Definition: Formed by sharing of electrons between two atoms.
    • Occurs Between: Typically nonmetals.
    • Types:
      • Single bond = 1 shared pair of electrons
      • Double bond = 2 shared pairs of electrons
      • Triple bond = 3 shared pairs of electrons
      • Nonpolar covalent bond = equal sharing of electrons
      • Polar covalent bond = unequal sharing of electrons (due to difference in electronegativity)
    • Example: H₂O, CO₂

    Ionic Bond

    • Definition: Formed by the transfer of electrons from one atom to another.
    • Occurs Between: Metals (which lose electrons) and nonmetals (which gain them).
    • Result: Formation of oppositely charged ions (cations and anions) that attract each other.
    • Example: NaCl (Sodium Chloride)

    Metallic Bond

    • Definition: A bond formed by the delocalized sharing of free electrons among a lattice of metal atoms.
    • Gives Rise To: Electrical conductivity, malleability, ductility.
    • Example: Copper (Cu), Iron (Fe)
