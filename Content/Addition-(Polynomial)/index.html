    Addition (Polynomial)

    Polynomial Addition: Is the operation of combining two polynomial by adding their corresponding coefficients for like terms. The result of adding two polynomials is another polynomial whose degree is determined by the highest degree of the terms involved.

    Symbols

    • $P(x),Q(x)\quad$ Polynomials
    • $a,b,c,d\quad$ Polynomial Coefficients

    Notation

    • Basic Form:
      The sum of two polynomials is written as:: $$P(x) + Q(x)$$

    Steps for Addition

    Algorithm

    1. Prepare the polynomials:
      Arrange the polynomials in line. $$ax\pm bx\pm cy\pm dy\pm\ldots$$
    2. Identify like terms:
      Look for terms with the same degree. $$\underline{ax}\pm \underline{bx}\pm \underline{\underline{cy}}\pm \underline{\underline{dy}}\pm\ldots$$
    3. Combine coefficients:
      Add or subtract the coefficients of the like terms. $$(a\pm b)x\pm (c\pm d)y\pm\ldots$$
    4. Simplify:
      Write the resulting terms as a single polynomial. $$Ax\pm By\pm\ldots\qquad \text{where }\begin{align}A=a\pm b \\ B=c\pm d\end{align}$$

    Example

    Algorithm example 1
    $P(x,y)=7x-9y+4$
    $Q(x,y)=-12x+15xy-12-x$
    $P(x,y)+Q(x,y)$
    1. Prepare the polynomials: $7x-9y+4-12x+(15xy-12-x)$
    2. Identify like terms: $\underline{7x}-9y+\underline{\underline{4}}-\underline{12x}+15xy-\underline{\underline{12}}-\underline{x}$
    3. Combine coefficients: $(7-12-1)x-9y-15xy-(4-12)$
    4. Simplify: $-6x-9y+15xy-8$
