    Physical Quantity

    A physical quantity is any property or characteristic of a Physical System that can be measured or quantified. It represents a measurable aspect of the physical world and is expressed with both a numerical value and a unit. Physical quantities can be classified as either fundamental or derived.

    Physical Constant

    In Physics, a constant is a quantity that remains fixed and does not change over time or in different conditions. Constants can be classified into different types based on their nature and role in equations.

    Extensive and Intensive Quantity

    • Extensive Properties:
      • Depend on the system size or the amount of matter.
      • Are additive (e.g., mass, volume, heat capacity).
    • Intensive Properties:
      • Independent of the system size or the amount of matter.
      • Remain the same when systems are divided (e.g., temperature, pressure, density).

    Key Characteristics:

    • Numerical Value: A quantity is always paired with a number, which represents the amount or magnitude of the physical quantity.
    • Unit: Every physical quantity has a unit, which defines the standard for measurement. The unit specifies the reference used for measurement (e.g., meter for length, second for time).
