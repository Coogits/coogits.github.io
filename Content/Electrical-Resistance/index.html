    Electrical Resistance

    Electrical resistance is a measure of how much a material or an electrical component opposes the flow of electric current. It quantifies the degree to which the passage of current is hindered, causing a voltage drop across the material or component.

    Symbols

    • $R\quad$ Electrical Resistance: (unit: Ohm ($\Omega$))
    • $\rho\quad$ Electric Resistivity (unit: $\Omega\cdot m$)
    • $J\quad$ Electric Current Density (unit: Ampere per square meter ($\frac{A}{m^2}$))
    • $\Delta V\quad$ Electric Potential Difference (unit: Volt (V))
    • $I\quad$ Electric Current (unit: Ampere (A))
    • $l\quad$ Conductor Length (unit: meter (m))
    • $A\quad$ Cross-Sectional Area (unit: $m^2$)

    Formulas

    • From the Ohm's laws:
      According to the first Ohm's Law[1]Ohm's Law ($\Delta{V}=RI$), the Resistance can be expressed as: $$R=\cfrac{\Delta{V}}{I}$$
    • Resistance of a Homogeneous Conductor:
      The resistance is given by: $$R=\rho\cfrac{l}{A}$$

    Unit of the Electrical Resistance

    • SI Unit:
      The International System's Unit of the Electric Current is the Ohm: $$R=\left(\cfrac{V}{A}\right)=\Omega$$
