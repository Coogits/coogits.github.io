    Classical Mechanics

    Classical Mechanics is a branch of Classical Physics that deals with the motion of objects and the forces acting upon them, based on principles and laws that were formulated prior to the development of quantum mechanics and relativity. It provides a framework for understanding macroscopic phenomena, ranging from the motion of everyday objects to the dynamics of larger systems such as planets and galaxies.

    Subfields

    Kinematics

    The study of motion without considering the forces that cause it.

    Dynamics

    The study of forces and their effects on motion.

    Statics (Physics)

    The study of forces in equilibrium (objects at rest).

    Newtonian Gravitation

    Newtonian gravitation is the classical theory of gravity formulated by Isaac Newton, which describes the attractive force between two masses as governed by the Universal Law of Gravitation.

    Fluid Mechanics

    The study of fluids (liquids and gases) and the forces acting upon them.

    Galilean Relativity

    Galilean relativity (also called the principle of Galilean invariance) is a fundamental concept in classical mechanics stating that the laws of motion are the same in all inertial reference frames.

    Rigid-body Dynamics

    The study of the motion of solid objects, considering both their translational and rotational motion.

    Physical Quantity

    Physical Quantity comparison:

    Linear Quantity Angular Quantity
    Position $s$ Angle $\theta$
    Displacement $\Delta s$ Angular Displacement $\Delta\theta=\cfrac{\Delta s}{r}$
    Velocity Tangential $v=\cfrac{ds}{dt}$ Angular Velocity $\omega=\cfrac{v}{r}=\cfrac{d\theta}{dt}$
    Acceleration Tangential $a=\cfrac{dv}{dt}=\cfrac{d^2s}{dt^2}$ Angular Acceleration $\alpha=\cfrac{a}{r}=\cfrac{d\omega}{dt}=\cfrac{d^2\theta}{dt^2}$
    ULM $s(t)=vt+s_0\qquad$ UCM $\theta(t)=\omega t+\theta_0$
    Uniformly Accelerated Linear Motion $s(t)=\frac{1}{2}at^2+v_0t+s_0 \qquad v(t)=at+v_0$ Uniformly Accelerated Circular Motion $\theta(t)=\frac{1}{2}\alpha t^2+\omega_0 t+\theta_0 \qquad \omega(t)=\alpha t+\omega_0$
    Inertial Mass $m$ Moment Of Inertia $I=\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^n m_ir_i^2=\int r^2dm$
    Kinetic Energy $K=\frac{1}{2}mv^2$ Rotational Kinetic Energy $K=\frac{1}{2}I\omega^2$
    Force (Physics) $\vec{F}=m\vec{a}$ Torque (Physics) $\vec\tau=I\overrightarrow\alpha$
    Work (Physics) $W=\displaystyle\int_{x_0}^{x_1}F(x)dx$ Rotational Work $W=\displaystyle\int_{\theta_0}^{\theta_1}\tau(\theta)d\theta$
    Linear Momentum $\overrightarrow{p}=m\overrightarrow{v}$ Angular Momentum $\overrightarrow L=I\overrightarrow\omega$

    Applications

    • Predicting the motion of projectiles.
    • Understanding planetary motion (e.g., Kepler’s laws of planetary motion).
    • Engineering applications like the design of machines, vehicles, and structures.
    • Study of oscillations, waves, and vibrations.
