    Greatest Common Divisor (Monomials)

    The Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) of two or more monomials is the largest monomial that divides each of the given monomials without leaving a remainder.

    Symbols and Notation

    • $m_1\quad$ Generic monomials $m_1=c_1x^{a_1}y^{b_1}z^{d_1}$
    • $m_2\quad$ Generic monomials $m_2 =c_2x^{a_2}y^{b_2}z^{d_2}$
    • $c_1,c_2,\ldots\quad$ Coefficients of the monomials
    • $x,y,z\quad$ variables
    • $a_1,a_2\quad$ 1st exponent
    • $b_1,b_2\quad$ 2nd exponent
    • $d_1,d_2\quad$ 3rd exponent

    Formulas

    • Greatest Common Divisor:
      To compute the GCD of two monomials, you:
      • Find the GCD of their coefficients.
      • For each variable, take the minimum exponent present in the monomials.
        Thus, the formula is:$$\mbox{GCD}(m_1,m_2)=\mbox{GCD}(c_1,c_2)\cdot x^{\mbox{min}(a_1-a_2)}\cdot y^{\mbox{min}(b_1-b_2)}\cdot z^{\mbox{min}(d_1-d_2)}$$

    Steps

    Steps to Determine the GCD Between Monomials

    1. Coefficients GCD:
      Find the GCD of the coefficients;
      Identify the greatest common factor of the numerical coefficients of the monomials.
    2. Variables GCD:
      Find the smallest exponent for each variable;
      For each variable common to all the monomials, take the smallest exponent among them.
    3. Result:
      Combine the results;
      Multiply the GCD of the coefficients by the variables raised to their respective smallest exponents.

    Example

    Find the GCD of the monomials: $\mbox{GCD}(8x^4z^2,\quad 20x^3y^5z^3)$

    1. Coefficients GCD:
      • $\mbox{GCD}(8,20)=4$
    2. Variables GCF:
      • For $x$: $\mbox{min}(4,3)=3$
      • For $y$: $\mbox{min}(0,5)=0$
      • For $z$: $\mbox{min}(2,3)=2$
    3. Result:
      • $\mbox{GCD}(8x^4z^2,\quad 20x^3y^5z^3)=4x^3z^2$
