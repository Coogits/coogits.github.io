    Square of Binomial (Special Products)

    The Square of a Binomial refers to the result of multiplying a binomial by itself. It follows a predictable pattern, making it a commonly used algebraic identity.

    Symbols

    • $A,B\quad$ Monomial
    • $A^2\pm{2AB}+B^2\quad$ Expanded form:
    • $(A\pm B)^2\quad$ Factored form
    • $\pm\quad$ Indicates either a plus or minus, depending on the binomial sign. For example:
      • $(A-B)^2=A^2-{2AB}+B^2$).
      • $(A + B)^2=A^2 + {2AB}+B^2$

    Formula

    • Expansion:
      Description: $$(A\pm B)^2=A^2\pm{2AB}+B^2$$
    • Factorization:
      quick description: $$A^2\pm{2AB}+B^2=(A\pm B)^2$$

    Demonstration

    • $(A\pm B)^2=A^2\pm{2AB}+B^2$:
      Expansion demonstration: $$\begin{align} (A \pm B)^2 &= {(A\pm B)(A\pm B)} \\ &= {A^2\pm{AB}\pm{AB}+B^2} \\ &=A^2 \pm 2AB + B^2 \end{align}$$
