    Uniform linear motion

    Uniform linear motion (ULM) is the motion of an object along a straight path with a constant velocity. This means the object covers equal distances in equal time intervals and experiences zero acceleration.

    Symbols

    • $s(t)\quad$ Position at time $t$; in meters
    • $v\quad$ Velocity (constant); in $\left[\cfrac{m}{s}\right]$
    • $t\quad$ Time (final); in seconds
    • $t_0\quad$ Time (initial); in seconds
    • $s_0\quad$ Initial position; in meters

    Formulas

    • Motion Equation: $$s(t)=v(t-t_0)+s_0\qquad t\ge t_0$$

    Properties

    • Straight-Line Path:
      The motion occurs along a single axis without deviation. this mean the motion can be represented as a Line (Analytic Geometry).
    • Constant Velocity:
      The speed and direction of the object remain unchanged.
    • Zero Acceleration:
      Since velocity does not change, the acceleration is 0 ($a=0$).
