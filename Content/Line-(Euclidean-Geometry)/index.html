    Line (Euclidean Geometry)

    A line is a one-dimensional geometric object that extends infinitely in both directions, having no thickness or curvature. It is the shortest distance between any two points along it and is typically defined by two points on the line. A line is often understood as an abstract ideal that has infinite length, but in practical geometry, we typically study line segments (a portion of a line between two points).

    Symbols

    • $A\quad$ Point in the space $A=(x_1,y_1)$
    • $B\quad$ Point in the space $B=(x_2,y_2)$

    Key Points

    • Infiniteness:
      A line has no endpoints and extends infinitely in both directions.
    • Straightness:
      A line is perfectly straight, meaning it has no bends or curves.
    • One-Dimensional:
      A line is one-dimensional, meaning it only has length, with no width or depth.
    • Uniqueness:
      Through any two distinct points, there is exactly one straight line.

    Line Segment

    • Length of a Segment:
      A segment between two points $A$ and $B$ is written as: $$\overline{AB}$$
    • Segment Length:
      The length of a segment $\overline{AB}$ is the distance between its endpoints, often found using the distance formula in coordinate geometry: $$d=\sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2+(y_2-y_1)^2}$$
