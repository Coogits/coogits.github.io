    Polynomial

    Polynomial is a mathematical expression consisting of indeterminates and coefficient, that involves only the operations of addition, subtraction, multiplication and exponentiation to non-negative power, and has a finite number of terms.

    Symbols and Terminology

    • $x,y\quad$ Indeterminates: Represent an unspecified value.
    • $ax^3\quad$ First term of the polynomial.
    • $bx^2\quad$ Second term of the polynomial.
    • $a\quad$ Leading coefficient: Is the coefficient of the term with the highest degree in a polynomial.
    • $c\quad$ Constant Term: Is the term that does not contain any variables (not always present).
    • $a=1\quad$ A monic polynomial is a polynomial where the leading coefficient is equal to 1. For example, $x^2+5x+4$ is a monic polynomial, because the coefficient of $x^2$ is 1. If the leading coefficient is not 1, the polynomial is called non-monic.

    Notation

    Definition

    • Polynomial Generic Notation:
      A polynomial can be expressed like $P(x)$ or $Q(x)$: $$P(x)=\sum_{i=0}^na_ix^{e_i}$$
      • $e_i\quad$ are distinct non-negative integers, typically ordered as $e_0>e_1>\ldots>e_n$

    by Variables

    Classification by number of variables.

    • Monomial
      Is a particular Polynomial with exactly one term. A standard Form: $$axy$$
    • Binomial:
      Is a Polynomial with exactly two terms. A standard Form: $$ax+c$$
    • Trinomial:
      Is a Polynomial with exactly three terms. A Standard Form: $$ax^2+bx+c$$
    • Polynomial with multiple variables:
      Generic definition: $$P(x,y,\ldots)=ax+by+\ldots+c$$

    by Degree

    Classification by degree. The polynomial Degree is the highest degree of terms.

    • Quadratic Polynomial:
      Is a Polynomial with degree 2: $$ax^2+bx+c$$
    • Cubic Polynomial:
      Is a Polynomial with degree 3: $$ax^3+bx^2+c+d$$

    Monomial

    Monomial is a Polynomial with exactly one term. In other words, a monomial is a specific case of a polynomial in which the degree (or the number of terms) is equal to 1.

    Factorization (Polynomial)

    Factorization is the process of expressing a Polynomial as a product of simpler polynomials (or Monomials) that, when multiplied together, yield the original polynomial. This decomposition simplifies solving equations, analyzing polynomial behavior, and finding roots.
