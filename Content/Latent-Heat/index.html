    Latent Heat

    Latent Heat is the amount of heat energy per unit mass that must be absorbed or released by a substance during a phase change (such as melting, freezing, vaporization, or condensation) without changing its temperature.


    ### Formulas
    • Definition: $$L=\cfrac{Q}{m}$$

    Symbols

    • $L\quad$ Latent Heat
    • $Q\quad$ is the Heat exchanged, ( unit: joule (J) )
    • $m\quad$ is the Mass of the substance ( unit: kilogram (kg) ),

    Unit

    • SI Unit: Latent heat is typically measured in joules per kilogram: $$L=1\;\cfrac{J}{kg}$$

    Types of Latent Heat

    • Latent Heat of Fusion: The heat required to change a substance from solid to liquid at its melting point (or released when freezing).
    • Latent Heat of Vaporization: The heat required to change a substance from liquid to gas at its boiling point (or released when condensing).

    Properties

    • Constant Temperature:
      During a phase transition, the temperature of the substance remains constant even though heat energy is being absorbed or released.

    • Energy Absorption or Release:

      • When a substance absorbs latent heat, it undergoes a phase change from a lower-energy state to a higher-energy state (e.g., solid to liquid or liquid to gas).
      • When a substance releases latent heat, it transitions from a higher-energy state to a lower-energy state (e.g., gas to liquid or liquid to solid).
