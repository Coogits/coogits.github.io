    Dot Product (Vector)

    The dot product (also known as the scalar product or inner product) is an algebraic operation that takes two vectors and returns a single number (scalar).


    Symbols

    • $\vec{v_1},\vec{v_2}\quad$ vectors
    • $\lvert\vec{v_1}\rvert,\lvert\vec{v_2}\rvert\quad$ Magnitude of the vectors.
    • $\theta\quad$ Angle between the two vectors.

    Formulas

    Definition

    • With coordinates
      Multiplying and summing the coordinates: $$\vec{v_1}\cdot\vec{v_2}=x_1\cdot x_2+y_1\cdot y_2$$
    • With Angle
      Using the modules and the cosine of the angle $\alpha$ between the vectors: $$\vec{v_1}\cdot\vec{v_2}=\lvert\vec{v_1}\rvert\cdot\lvert\vec{v_2}\rvert\cdot\cos\theta$$

    Properties

    • Commutative
      The dot product is commutative: $$\vec{v_1}\cdot\vec{v_2}=\vec{v_2}\cdot\vec{v_1}$$
    • Distributive
      The dot Product is Distributive: $$\vec{v_3}\cdot(\vec{v_1}+\vec{v_2})=\vec{v_3}\cdot\vec{v_1}+\vec{v_3}\cdot\vec{v_2}$$
    • Perpendicular vectors
      The dot product between two perpendicular vectors is zero: $$\vec{v_1}\cdot\vec{v_2}=0\qquad\text{if }\vec{v_1}\perp\vec{v_2}$$

    Geometrical Interpretation

    The dot product gives a measure of how much one vector "projects" onto another. It is positive if the angle between the vectors is acute, negative if the angle is obtuse, and zero if the vectors are perpendicular.

    • Electric Flux
