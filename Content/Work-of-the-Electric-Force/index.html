    Work of the Electric Force

    The work done by the electric force is the energy transferred to or from a charged particle when it is moved by the electric force over a distance. It quantifies how much energy is imparted to the charge by the force acting along its displacement.

    Symbols

    • $W\quad$ Work of the Electric Force (unit: Joule (J))
    • $F\quad$ Electrostatic Force (unit: Newton (N))
    • $Q\quad$ Source Charge (unit: Coulombs (C))
    • $q\quad$ Test Charge (unit: Coulombs (C))
    • $U\quad$ Electric Potential Energy (unit: Joule (J))
    • $\Delta V\quad$ Electric Potential Difference (unit: Volt (V)). Defined as the potential at $A$ relative to $B$
      • $\Delta V=\cfrac{Q}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\left(\cfrac{1}{r_A}-\cfrac{1}{r_B}\right)$

    Formulas

    • General Case (Vector form):
      When the electric force and the displacement are not necessarily aligned, the work is defined by the Dot Product (Vector): $$W=F\cdot s=Fs\cos\theta$$
    • Work Done by an Electric Field:
      The work done on a charge $q$ when it moves through a potential difference $\Delta V$ from B to A: $$W=q\Delta{V}$$

    Properties

    • Path Independence of the Work:
      Due to the Conservative Force property of the Electrostatic Force, the work done depends only on the initial and final positions, not on the path taken.
