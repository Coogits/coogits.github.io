    Antiderivative (Indefinite Integral & Primitive)

    An antiderivative of a function $f(x)$ is a function $F(x)$ whose derivative equals $f(x)$: $$F'(x)=f(x)$$

    Symbols

    • $\int\quad$ denotes the integral symbol.
    • $f(x)\quad$ is the given function.
    • $dx\quad$ indicates integration with respect to x.
    • $F(x)$ is the antiderivative (a function whose derivative is $f(x)$.
    • $F'(x)\quad$ Derivative of $F(x)$, which results in $f(x)$
    • $C\quad$ is the constant of integration, representing an infinite family of solutions.

    Formulas

    Definition

    • Antiderivative (Indefinite Integral & Primitive):
      The derivative of the antiderivative of a function, is a function itself: $$F'(x)=f(x)$$

    Antiderivative (Primitive)

    Defined in Differential Calculus[1]In Differential Calculus the antiderivative is the inverse of Derivative as the inverse of Derivative[3]Derivative is the inverse of Antiderivative.

    • Notation:
      The notation as antiderivative is: $$F(x)$$
    • Definition:
      If $F(x)$ is an antiderivative of $f(x)$, then all antiderivatives of $f(x)$ differ by a constant $C$: $$F(x)+C$$

    Indefinite Integral

    Defined in Integral Calculus[2]In Integral Calculus the antiderivative is an Integral without specified limits as an Integral[4]Integral without specified limit is an antiderivative without specified limits.

    • Notation:
      The notation for an indefinite integral is: $$\displaystyle\int f(x)\;dx$$
    • Definition:
      The indefinite integral represents the general antiderivative of $x$: $$\displaystyle\int f(x)\;dx=F(x)+C$$
