    Gravitational Mass

    Gravitational mass (Mass) is a property of an object that determines the strength of the gravitational force it experiences in a gravitational field.
    Gravitational mass is responsible for how much an object will attract other masses (through gravity) and how much gravitational force it will experience. It is identical in value to the inertial mass, which measures an object's resistance to acceleration when a force is applied.

    Formulas

    • From the gravitational law $$M=\cfrac{F_wr^2}{Gm}$$
