    Particle Physics

    Particle physics is the branch of physics that studies the fundamental constituents of matter and the forces that govern their interactions.

    Atomic Theory

    Particles

    Quarks

    • Fundamental constituents of matter.
    • Combine to form hadrons, such as protons and neutrons.
    • Types (flavors): up, down, charm, strange, top, bottom.

    Leptons

    • Fundamental particles that do not experience the strong nuclear force.
    • Examples:
      • Electron (e⁻)
      • Muon (μ⁻)
      • Tau (τ⁻)
      • Each has a corresponding neutrino: νₑ, ν_μ, ν_τ

    Bosons

    • Force-carrying particles that mediate interactions between matter particles.
    • Examples:
      • Photon (γ) – electromagnetic force
      • Gluon (g) – strong nuclear force
      • W⁺, W⁻, Z⁰ bosons – weak nuclear force
      • Higgs boson (H⁰) – gives particles mass via the Higgs field

    Forces

    Forces described by the Standard Model, including:

    • Electromagnetic force
    • Weak nuclear force
    • Strong nuclear force
    • (Gravity is not yet fully integrated into the Standard Model.)
