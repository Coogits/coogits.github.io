    Radical

    Radical: Is the arithmetical operation of exponentiation. The n-th root of a number $a$ is a number $x$ that, when raised to the power $x$, gives back $a$.

    • Definition
      • $x=\sqrt[n]{a} \quad\text{where}\quad x^n=a$
    • Basic Form:
      • $\sqrt[n]{a}=x \qquad n\in\mathbb{N}\setminus\{0\},\; a\in\mathbb{R}$
    • Symbols
      • $a\quad$ Radicand; the number or expression inside the radical symbol that is being rooted.
      • $n\quad$ Index; indicating the degree of the root.
      • $x\quad$ Root; the result of evaluating the radical expression.

    Properties

    • Product Property (Multiplication Under a Root): The $n$-th root of a product is the product of the $n$-th roots:
      • $\sqrt[n]{a}\cdot\sqrt[n]{b}=\sqrt[n]{a\cdot b}\qquad a,b\ge0, n\ge1$
      • $\sqrt[n]{a^m}\cdot\sqrt[n]{a^n}=\sqrt[n]{a^{m+n}} \qquad a\ge0, n\ge1$
    • Quotient Property (Division Under a Root): The $n$-th root of a quotient is the quotient of the $n$-th roots:
      • $\sqrt[n]{\cfrac{a}{b}}=\cfrac{\sqrt[n]{a}}{\sqrt[n]{b}}\qquad a\ge0\land n,b>0$
    • Power Property (Root and Exponentiation): The $n$-th root of a number raised to a power can be expressed as a power of the root:
      • $\sqrt[n]{a^m}=a^{\frac{m}{n}}$
      • $(\sqrt[n]{a})^m=\sqrt[n]{a^m}$
    • Inverse of Exponentiation: Taking the $n$-th root is the inverse operation of raising a number to the power of $n$. This means:
      • $\sqrt[n]{a^n}=a$
      • $(\sqrt[n]{a})^n=a$
    • even-indexed root: When taking an even root of $a^n$, you always obtain the non-negative value of $a$, which is represented by $\left|a\right|$.
      • $\sqrt[n]{a^n}=\left|a\right| \qquad n=2k,k\in\mathbb{N}$
    • Invariant Property: When both the index of the root and the exponent of the radicand are scaled by the same factor, the overall value of the radical expression remains unchanged.
      • $\sqrt[n]{a^m}=\sqrt[n\cdot s]{a^{m\cdot s}}$
    • Root with Negative Index: It is equivalent to taking the reciprocal of the $n$-th root of $a$.
      • $\sqrt[-n]{a}=\cfrac{1}{\sqrt[n]{a}}$
    • Sum of similar radicals: Similar radicals can be added or subtracted by adding or subtracting the coefficients of the radical expressions while keeping the radical part unchanged.
      • $b\sqrt{a}+c\sqrt{a}=(b+c)\sqrt{a}$
