    Pythagorean Theorem

    The Pythagorean Theorem states that in a right triangle, the square of the length of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the squares of the lengths of the other two sides.

    Key Points

    • The Pythagorean Theorem applies only to right-angled triangles.
    • The Generalized Pythagorean Theorem (Law of Cosines) extends this concept to any triangle.
    • It is fundamental in Euclidean geometry and has applications in trigonometry, physics, and engineering.
    • It is used to calculate distances, verify triangle properties, and solve problems involving right triangles.

    Symbols

    • $c\quad$ Hypotenuse
    • $a,b\quad$ Legs (other two sides)
    • $\alpha,\beta,\gamma\quad$ Angles

    Formulas

    • Pythagorean Theorem (Right Triangle Formula):
      This is a special case of the Generalized Pythagorean Theorem, applicable only to right triangles: $$c^2=a^2+b^2$$ Solving for different sides: $$\begin{cases}c=\sqrt{a^2+b^2} \\ b=\sqrt{c^2-a^2} \\ a=\sqrt{c^2-b^2}\end{cases}$$
    • Generalized Pythagorean Theorem (Law of Cosines):
      For any triangle (not just right triangles): $$\begin{align} a^2=b^2+c^2-2bc\cdot\cos\alpha \\ b^2=a^2+c^2-2ac\cdot\cos\beta \\ c^2=a^2+b^2-2ab\cdot\cos\gamma \end{align}$$

    Application Examples

    • Vector Magnitude: $|\overrightarrow{v}|=\sqrt{v_x^2+v_y^2}$
