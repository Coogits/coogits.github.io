    Least Common Multiple (Monomials)

    Least Common Multiple (Monomials): (LCM) of two or more Monomials is the smallest monomial (in terms of divisibility) that is divisible by all the given monomials.

    • Notation: Where $m_1,m_2$ are generic monomials $\begin{cases}m_1=c_1x^{a_1}y^{b_1}z^{d_1} \\ m_2 =c_2x^{a_2}y^{b_2}z^{d_2} \end{cases}\quad$.
      • $\mbox{LCM}(m_1,m_2)=\mbox{LCM}(c_1,c_2)\cdot x^{\mbox{max}(a_1-a_2)}\cdot y^{\mbox{max}(b_1-b_2)}\cdot z^{\mbox{max}(d_1-d_2)}$
    • Symbols
      • $m_1\quad$ Generic monomials
      • $m_2\quad$ Generic monomials

    Steps to Determine the LCM Between Monomials

    1. Numerica Coefficient: Find the LCM of the coefficients:
      Identify the least common multiple of the numerical coefficients of the monomials.
    2. Variables: Find the greater exponent for each variable:
      For each variable common to all the monomials, take the greater exponent among them.
    3. Result: Combine the results:
      Assign it the highest exponent found among the monomials.

    Example

    1. $\mbox{LCM}(8x^4z^2,\quad 20x^3y^5z^3)$
      1. Numerica Coefficient: LCM of the coefficients
        • $\mbox{LCM}(8,20)=4$
      2. Variables: Greater exponents
        • For $x$: $\mbox{min}(4,3)=4$
        • For $y$: $\mbox{min}(0,5)=5$
        • For $z$: $\mbox{min}(2,3)=3$
      3. Combine the result:
        • $\mbox{GCD}(8x^4z^2,\quad 20x^3y^5z^3)=4x^4y^5z^3$

    Properties:

    • The LCM of monomials is always another monomial.
    • It includes every factor (variable or coefficient) needed to make all the given monomials divisors of the result.
