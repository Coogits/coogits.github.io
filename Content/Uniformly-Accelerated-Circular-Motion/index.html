    Uniformly Accelerated Circular Motion

    Uniformly Accelerated Circular Motion occurs when a point moves along a circular path while its angular velocity changes at a constant rate over time. This motion is analogous to linear motion with constant acceleration, but in rotational terms.

    Formulas

    • Equation of Motion
      Calculates angular displacement over time under constant angular acceleration: $$\theta(t)=\frac{1}{2}\alpha t^2 +\omega_0 t+\theta_0$$
    • Instantaneous Angular Velocity
      The angular velocity $\omega$ at time $t$ with constant acceleration: $$\omega(t)=\alpha t+\omega_0$$
    • Angular Acceleration
      Is Constant; The rate of change of angular velocity: $$\alpha=\cfrac{\Delta\omega}{\Delta t}=\cfrac{\omega_1-\omega_0}{t_1-t_0}$$
    • Centripetal Acceleration
      Is Constant; The acceleration directed towards the center of the circle: $$a_c=\cfrac{v^2}{r}=\omega^2 r$$
    • Tangential Acceleration
      Is Constant; Acceleration along the tangent to the path: $$a_T=\alpha r$$
    • Total Acceleration
      With the Pythagorean Theorem; The combined effect of centripetal and tangential accelerations: $$a_{tot}=\sqrt{a_T^2+a_c^2}$$

    Key Characteristics:

    • The angular acceleration $\alpha$ remains constant.
    • The angular velocity $\omega$ changes uniformly over time.
    • The angular displacement $\theta$ follows kinematic equations similar to those in linear motion.
