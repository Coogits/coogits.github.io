    Least Common Multiple

    Greatest Common Divisor: Is the smallest positive integer that is divisible by each of the given numbers.

    Notation

    • Least Common Multiple:
      Is represented as: $$\mbox{LCM}(a, b,\ldots)$$

    GCD Method

    • LCM Greatest Common Divisor Method:
      An efficient way to calculate the LCM is to divide the absolute value of the product of the given terms by their GCD[1]Greatest Common Divisor. $$\mbox{LCM}(a, b,\ldots)=\cfrac{\left|a\cdot b \cdot\ldots\right|}{\mbox{GCD}(a, b,\ldots)}$$

    Prime Factorization Method

    Steps Prime Factorization Method Example
    $\mbox{GCD}(60,16,100)$
    1 Prime Factorization $\begin{array}{r\|l} 60 & 2 \\ 30 & 2 \\ 15 & 3 \\ 5 & 5 \\ 1 &\\\hline&2^2\cdot3\cdot5 \end{array}\qquad$ $\begin{array}{r\|l} 16 & 2 \\ 8 & 2 \\ 4 & 2 \\ 2 & 2 \\ 1 & \\\hline&2^4\end{array}\qquad$ $\begin{array}{r\|l} 100 & 2 \\ 50 & 2 \\ 25 & 5 \\ 5 & 5 \\ 1 & \\\hline&2^2\cdot5^2\end{array}$
    2 Multiply the common and
    non common factors with
    the greater exponent    		 $2^4\cdot3\cdot5^2=16\cdot3\cdot25=1200$
    $\mbox{LCM}(60,16,100)=1200$

    Key points

    • The LCM is useful for finding a common multiple shared by numbers.
    • It is always greater than or equal to the largest of the given numbers.
