    Properties of Equality

    Properties of Equality: These properties describe how Equations can be manipulated while maintaining the equality between both sides. They ensure that any operation you perform on one side of an equation must also be performed on the other side to preserve the balance or equality.

    Most common properties of equality:

    • Reflexive Property:
      Any quantity is equal to itself: $$a=a$$
    • Symmetric Property:
      This property allows to reverse the sides of an equation: $$a=b \quad\implies\quad b=a$$
    • Transitive Property:
      This property allows to substitute one equal expression for another: $$\begin{cases} a=b \\ b=c\end{cases}\quad\implies\quad a=c$$
    • Addition Property or Addition Rule:
      You can add the same value to both sides of an equation without changing the equality: $$a=b \quad\implies\quad a+c=b+c$$
    • Subtraction Property or Subtraction Rule:
      You can subtract the same value from both sides of an equation without changing the equality: $$a=b \quad\implies\quad a-c=b-c$$
    • Multiplication Property or Multiplication Rule:
      You can multiply both sides of an equation by the same non-zero value: $$a=b \quad\implies\quad a\cdot c=b\cdot c \qquad (c\neq0)$$
    • Division Property or Division Rule:
      You can divide both sides of an equation by the same non-zero value: $$a=b \quad\implies\quad \cfrac{a}{c}=\cfrac{b}{c} \qquad (c\neq0)$$
    • Substitution Property:
      If $a=b$, then $a$ can be substituted for $b$ in any equation or expression and viceversa.
      • $a=b$
    • Zero Product Property:
      If $a$ or $b$ is zero, their product is also zero. (often used in factoring) This is often used when solving Quadratic Equation: $$a=0\lor b=0 \quad\implies\quad a\cdot b=0$$

    Principles

    1. First Principle (Addition and Subtraction): We can add or subtract the same quantity from both sides of the equation:
      • $x+a=x \quad\to\quad x=x-a$
    2. Second Principle (Multiplication and Division): We can multiply or divide both sides of the equation by the same non-zero quantity:
      • $bx=a \quad\to\quad \cfrac{bx=a}{b} \quad\to\quad x=\cfrac{a}{b}$
