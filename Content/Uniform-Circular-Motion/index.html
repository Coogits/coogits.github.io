    Uniform Circular Motion

    Uniform Circular Motion (UCM) occurs when an object moves along a circular path with a constant speed. Although the magnitude of velocity remains unchanged, its direction continuously changes, meaning the object experiences an acceleration called centripetal acceleration.

    Symbols

    • $\theta(t)\quad$ Angle at the time $t$; in radians
    • $\theta_0\quad$ the Initial Angle; in radians.
    • $\omega\quad$ Constant Angular Velocity; in $\cfrac{\text{rad}}{s}$.
    • $t\quad$ Time in seconds
    • $f\quad$ Frequency

    Formulas

    • Equation of Motion:
      Describes the angular position as a function of time: $$\theta(t)=\omega t+\theta_0$$
    • Angular velocity:
      in UCM is Constant, Measures the rate of change of the angle over time and can be expressed in different forms: $$\omega=\cfrac{\Delta\theta}{\Delta t}=\cfrac{\theta-\theta_0}{t-t_0}=\cfrac{2\pi}{T}=\cfrac{v_t}{r}$$

    Derivates

    • Tangential velocity:
      The velocity of a point along the circumference: $$v_t=\cfrac{2\pi r}{T}=\omega r$$
    • Period:
      The time required to complete one full revolution: $$T=\cfrac{1}{f}=\cfrac{2\pi r}{v_t}=\cfrac{2\pi}{\omega}=\cfrac{2\pi v_t}{a_c}$$
    • Frequency:
      The number of revolutions per second: $$f=\cfrac{1}{T}$$
    • Radius of the orbit:
      The distance of the moving point from the center of the circular path: $$r=\cfrac{v_t}{\omega}$$
    • Centripetal acceleration:
      Always directed toward the center, keeping the motion circular: $$a_c=\cfrac{v_t^2}{r}=\omega^2 r$$

    Characteristics:

    • Constant Speed: The object's speed does not change, but its velocity (a vector) changes due to direction.
    • Centripetal Acceleration: Directed toward the center of the circle, keeping the object in motion along the circular path.
    • No Tangential Acceleration: Since speed is constant, there is no acceleration in the tangential direction.
