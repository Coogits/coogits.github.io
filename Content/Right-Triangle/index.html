    Right Triangle

    A right triangle (or right-angled triangle) is a triangle that has one of its angles measuring 90 degrees (a right angle). The side opposite the right angle is called the hypotenuse, which is the longest side of the triangle. The other two sides, which form the right angle, are called the legs.

    Symbols

    • $2p\quad$ Perimeter (Geometry)
    • $a\quad$ Leg opposite to angle $\alpha$ and adjacent to $\beta$
    • $b\quad$ Leg adjacent to $\alpha$ and opposite to $\beta$
    • $c\quad$ hypotenuse, the longest side
    • $\alpha\quad$ Angle (Trigonometry) opposite to leg $a$ and adjacent to $b$
    • $\beta\quad$ Angle (Trigonometry) adjacent to $a$ and opposite to $b$
    • $\gamma\quad$ Right angle, always $90^\circ$, opposite to the hypotenuse $c$
    • $\mathcal{A}\quad$ Area (Plane Geometry)
    • $R\quad$ Circumradius the radius of the circumscribed circle.
    • $r\quad$ Inradius the radius of the inscribed circle.
    • $m_c\quad$ Median to the Hypotenuse, half of the hypotenuse.
    • $\sin,\cos,\tan\quad$ Trigonometric Function

    Formulas

    • Perimeter: The sum of the sides: $$2p=a+b+c$$
    • Area: Half the product of the legs: $$\mathcal{A}=\cfrac{ab}{2}$$
    • Height:
      The altitude from the right angle to the hypotenuse: $$h=\cfrac{ab}{c}$$
    • Circumradius and Median to the Hypotenuse:
      The circumradius and the median to the hypotenuse are equal: $$R=m_c=\cfrac{c}{2}$$
    • Inradius (for right triangles):
      The radius of the inscribed circle: $$r=\cfrac{a+b-c}{2}$$
    • Legs in terms of Hypotenuse and Angles:
      Using trigonometric ratios: $$\begin{align} \alpha=c\sin\alpha=c\cos\beta \\ b=c\sin\beta=c\cos\alpha\end{align}$$
    • Trigonometric Definitions:
      Fundamental trigonometric ratios in a right triangle: $$\begin{align} \sin\alpha=\cfrac{a}{c}\quad;\quad\cos\alpha=\cfrac{b}{c}\quad;\quad\tan\alpha=\cfrac{a}{b} \\ \sin\beta=\cfrac{b}{c}\quad;\quad\cos\beta=\cfrac{a}{c}\quad;\quad\tan\beta=\cfrac{b}{a} \end{align}$$

    Properties

    • Angle Sum Property:
      In a right triangle, the two non-right angles are always complementary. $$\alpha+\beta=90^\circ$$
    • Pythagorean Theorem:
      The square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the legs. $$c^2=a^2+b^2$$
    • Law of Sines (for right triangles):
      Relates side lengths to their opposite angles: $$\cfrac{a}{\sin\alpha}=\cfrac{b}{\sin\beta}=c$$
