    Ponderal Laws

    The ponderal laws refer to a set of fundamental laws of chemical combination that are based on the masses (or weights) of substances involved in chemical reactions.

    "Ponderal" comes from the Latin ponderare, meaning to weigh — so these are the laws based on weight/mass in chemistry.

    Symbols

    • $m_{A}\quad$ mass of element $A$ that binds with another element
    • $m_{B}\quad$ mass of element $B$ that binds with element $A$
    • $m_{B1}\quad$ mass of element $B$ in one compound with a fixed mass of A
    • $m_{B2}\quad$ mass of element B in a different compound with the same fixed mass of A
    • $m_C\quad$total mass of the compound formed by the combination of $m_A$ and $m_B$
    • $m_D\quad$ mass of any other compound (not necessarily formed by $m_A$ and $m_B$)

    Laws

    Law of Conservation of Mass

    (Antoine Lavoisier)

    Mass can neither be created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction.

    • Formula:
      Is give by: $$m_{A}+m_{B}=m_C$$

    Law of Definite Proportions

    (Joseph Proust)

    A given compound always contains the same elements in the same fixed ratio by mass.

    • Formula: Is given by: $$m_{A}+m_{B}=m_C+m_D$$ In other terms, the mass ratio of $A$ to $B$ is constant for a pure compound $$\cfrac{m_A}{m_B}=c$$

    Law of Multiple Proportions

    (John Dalton)

    If two elements combine to form more than one compound, the masses of one element that combine with a fixed mass of the other are in the ratio of small whole numbers.

    • Formula:
      is given by: $$\cfrac{m_{B1}}{m_{B2}}=\cfrac{n_1}{n_2}\quad \text{where }\quad n_1,n_2\in\mathbb{N}$$

    Law of Reciprocal Proportions

    (by Richter)

    If element A combines with B and also with C, then B and C will combine with each other in the same ratio or a simple multiple of the ratio in which they combine with A.

    Gay-Lussac’s Law of Gaseous Volumes

    Gases react together in volumes that bear a simple ratio to each other and to the volume of the products (if gaseous), at the same temperature and pressure.
