    Dry Friction

    Dry Friction is the force that opposes the relative motion between two solid surfaces in contact, caused by the microscopic roughness of the surfaces. It can be divided into two types: static friction, which prevents the initiation of motion, and kinetic friction, which resists motion once it has started. The magnitude of dry friction is generally proportional to the normal force between the surfaces.

    Symbols

    • $\overrightarrow{F}_{fs}\quad$ Static Friction, is the force that resists the start of relative motion between two objects.
    • $\overrightarrow{F}_{fd}\quad$ Kinematic Friction, is the force that opposes the motion of an object already in motion.
    • $\overrightarrow{F}\quad$ Is the external force applied to the object.
    • $\overrightarrow{F}_{\perp}\quad$ Normal Force The force perpendicular to the surface, which can be derived from the Dot Product (Vector) between the unit vector normal to the surface and the unit vector of the applied force.
      • $\overrightarrow{F}_{\perp}=|\overrightarrow{F}|(\hat n\cdot\hat F)$
    • $\hat n\quad$ unit vector normal to the surface.
    • $-\hat{v}\quad$ unit vector opposite to the motion of the body.
    • $\mu_s,\mu_d\quad$ are the coefficients of static and dynamic friction, respectively; the static friction coefficient is greater than or equal to the dynamic one: $\mu_s \geq \mu_d$; in practice, common values are:
      • $\mu_s \simeq 0.6\quad$ average static friction
      • $\mu_d \simeq 0.4\quad$ average dynamic friction
      • $\mu_s \simeq 1\quad$ friction of tires on asphalt

    Formulas

    • Static Friction:
      The frictional force that resists the initiation of motion between two objects in contact: $$\overrightarrow{F}_{fs}=-\mu_s\;|\overrightarrow{F}_{\perp}|\hat{F} \qquad \text{with } |\overrightarrow{F}_{fs}|\leq|\overrightarrow{F}|$$
    • Kinetic Friction:
      or dynamic friction, is the frictional force that opposes the motion of an object already in motion: $$\overrightarrow{F}_{fd}=-\mu_d\;|\overrightarrow{F}_{\perp}|\hat{v}$$

    Properties

    • vector quantity
    • resistive force
    • Non-conservative Force (dissipative force)

    // --- Compute the total forces ---
if (actualVelocity.x * horizontalInput < maxVelocity)
	totalForces += characterMass * walkAcceleration * horizontalInput * Vector2.Dot(Vector2.right, groundVersor) * groundVersor;
// --------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Vector2 staticFriction, dynamicFriction;
// --- Normal Force ---
float normalForce = totalForces.magnitude * Vector2.Dot(groundNormal, totalForces.normalized);
// --- Dynamic Friction ---
dynamicFriction = staticFrictionCoefficient * normalForce * actualVelocity.normalized;
// --- Static Friction ---
staticFriction = dynamicFrictionCoefficient * normalForce * totalForces.normalized;
staticFriction = Mathf.Min(staticFriction.magnitude, totalForces.magnitude) * staticFriction.normalized;
// --------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Vector2 frictionForce = Vector2.Lerp(staticFriction, dynamicFriction, actualVelocity.magnitude / minVelocity);
totalForces += frictionForce;
