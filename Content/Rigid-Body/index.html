    Rigid Body

    A rigid body is Physical System represented as a physical object in which the distances between any two points within the body remain constant, regardless of the external forces or moments acting on it. In other words, a rigid body does not deform under the influence of forces, and its shape and size are preserved.

    Key features of a rigid body:

    • The relative positions of the points within the body do not change.
    • It maintains a fixed shape and volume, even when subjected to external forces or torques.
    • It can still experience motion (translation or rotation), but its internal structure remains unchanged.

    In classical mechanics:

    • Rigid bodies are idealized models used to simplify the analysis of the motion of objects, especially when considering translational motion (moving in a straight line) and rotational motion (spinning around an axis).
    • In reality, all objects can experience deformation under extreme forces, but the rigid body approximation is very useful in most engineering and physics problems for objects that do not significantly change shape under typical conditions.

    Rigid body dynamics is a branch of mechanics focused on studying the motion of rigid bodies and the forces that cause this motion.
