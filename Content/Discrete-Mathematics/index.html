    Discrete Mathematics

    Discrete Mathematics is the branch of mathematics that deals with structures that are countable or finite, meaning they do not involve continuity. It focuses on objects that can be enumerated or arranged in distinct steps, making it essential for computer science, logic, and combinatorial analysis.

    Arguments

    Logic

    The study of reasoning and principles of valid inference.

    • Study of formal reasoning using propositions, predicates, and logical connectives (AND, OR, NOT, etc.).
    • Includes truth tables, logical equivalences, and proofs.
