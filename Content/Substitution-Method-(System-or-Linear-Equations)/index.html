    Substitution Method

    The substitution method is an algebraic technique for solving a system of equations by isolating one variable in one equation and substituting its expression into the other equation(s). This process reduces the system to a simpler form, allowing for the step-by-step determination of each variable.

    Applications

    • When to use this method:
      • When one equation can be easily solved for a single variable.
      • When dealing with small systems (e.g., 2x2 or 3x3) where direct substitution is efficient.
      • When an exact algebraic solution is preferred over numerical approximations.
    • This method can be applied to:
      • 2x2 System of Linear Equations
      • 3x3 System of Linear Equations

    Steps

    1. Solve for One Variable:
      Express one variable in terms of the others from one of the equations.

    2. Substitute:
      Replace the chosen variable in the remaining equations to reduce the system.

    3. Simplify and Solve for Another Variable:
      Solve one of the new equations to find another variable.

    4. Repeat Substitution for Remaining Variables:
      Continue substituting the known values into the remaining equations until all variables are found.

    5. Back-Substitution:
      Use previously determined values to find the remaining unknowns.

    6. Calculate:
      Compute the numerical values of all variables.

    7. Verify:
      Substitute the solutions back into the original system to ensure they satisfy all equations

    Example

    Step Resolution 2x2 System example 3x3 System example
    0 Given System $\begin{cases} x+y=5 \\ x-y=1\end{cases}$ $\begin{cases} x+y+z=6 \\ x-y+z=2 \\ x+2y-z=6\end{cases}$
    1 Solve for one variable: $\begin{cases} x=5-y \\ x-y=1\end{cases}$ $\begin{cases} x=6-y-z \\ x-y+z=2 \\ x+2y-z=6\end{cases}$
    2 Substitute: $\begin{cases} x=5-y \\ (5-y)-y=1\end{cases}$ $\begin{cases} x=6-y-z \\ (6-y-z)-y+z=2 \\ (6-y-z)+2y-z=6\end{cases}$
    3 Simplify and resolve $\begin{cases} x=5-y \\ y=2\end{cases}\quad$ $\begin{cases} x=6-y-z \\ y=2 \\ y-2z=0 \end{cases}$
    4 Repeat substitution
    for remaining variables    		 $\begin{cases} x=6-y-z \\ y=2 \\ (2)-2z=0 \end{cases}$
    $\begin{cases} x=6-y-z \\ y=2 \\ z=1 \end{cases}$
    5 Back-substitution $\begin{cases} x=5-(2) \\ y=2\end{cases}\quad$ $\begin{cases} x=6-(2)-(1) \\ y=2 \\ z=1 \end{cases}$
    6 Calculate $\begin{cases} x=3 \\ y=2\end{cases}\quad$ $\begin{cases} x=3 \\ y=2 \\ z=1 \end{cases}$
    7 Verify $\begin{cases} 3+2=5 \\ 3-2=1\end{cases}$ $\begin{cases} 3+2+1=6 \\ 3-2+1=2 \\ 3+2(2)-1=6\end{cases}$
