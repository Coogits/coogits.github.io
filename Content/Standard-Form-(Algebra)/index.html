    Standard Form (Algebra)

    Standard form in algebra refers to a conventional way of writing mathematical expressions or equations, ensuring clarity and consistency. The definition varies depending on the type of mathematical object

    Symbols

    • $a_i,a,b,c,\ldots\quad$ represent constants or variables.
    • $n\quad$ is the degree of the polynomial.

    Standard Forms

    • Number Standard form:
      The representation of a number in standard form is the Expanded Form (Numeral System) in a positional system: $$\sum_{i=m}^na_ib^i$$
      • $b\quad$ is the base of the number (e.g. $b=10$ for decimal)
      • $m,n\quad$ are the least significative and the most significative digits, respectively.
    • Polynomial Standard Form:
      A Polynomial written in ordered from highest to lowest degree of its terms, following this general structure: $$\sum_{i=0}^na_ix^i$$ Explicitly: $$a_nx^{n}+a_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\ldots+a_1x+a_0\qquad a_n\neq0$$
    • Polynomial Equation in Standard Form:
      A Polynomial Equation written in standard form follows this general structure: $$\sum_{i=0}^na_ix^i=0$$ Explicitly: $$a_nx^{n}+a_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\ldots+a_1x+a_0=0\qquad a_n\neq0$$
    • Linear Equation Standard Form:
      A Linear Equation is in standard form when is represented as: $$ax+by=c \qquad a\neq0,b\neq0$$
