    Division (Polynomial)

    Polynomial division is a operation of dividing one polynomial (the dividend) by another polynomial (the divisor) to obtain a quotient and possibly a remainder. It is analogous to the long division of numbers.

    • Notations:
      • $P(x)\div Q(x)=O(x)$
      • $\cfrac{P(x)}{Q(x)}=O(x)$
    • Symbols
      • $P(x)\quad$ Dividend polynomial: It can be any generic polynomial.
      • $Q(x)\quad$ Divisor polynomial: It can be any generic polynomial.
      • $O(x)\quad$ Quotient polynomial: It can be any generic polynomial.

    Long Division Method

    Algorithm

    1. Standard Form:
      • Write the Division in Standard Form arranging both the dividend $P(x)$ and divisor $Q(x)$ in decreasing order of powers.
      • Important: If $P(x)$ is not completed fill the empty place with a zero.
    2. Divide the Leading Terms:
      • Divide (monomials) the leading term (the term with the highest degree) of the dividend $P(x)$ by the leading term of the divisor $Q(x)$.
      • The result becomes the next term of the quotient, which is added to the overall quotient.
    3. Multiply and Subtract:
      • Multiply the entire divisor $Q(x)$ by the term obtained in step 2.
    4. Subtract and bring down
      • Subtract the result just obtained from the appropriate term of the original dividend, then bring down the next term from the dividend
    5. Repeat:
      • Repeat the process with the new dividend (the remainder from the subtraction) until the degree of the remainder is less than the degree of the divisor.

    Long Division Method: Example.

    Steps Example 1
    $P(x)=3x^2+2x^3-2-x$
    $Q(x)=x+1$    		 $P(x)=x^3-2x^2+4$
    $Q(x)=x-3$
    1. Standard Form: $\cfrac{2x^3+3x^2-x-2}{x+1}$ $\cfrac{x^3-2x^2+0x+4}{x-3}$
    2. Divide: $\cfrac{2x^3}{x}=2x^2$ $\begin{array}{rcl} & x^2 \\ \hline x-3)\quad x^3 &- 2x^2 &+ 0x &+ 4 \end{array}$
    3. Multiply: $2x^2\cdot(x+1)=2x^3+2x^2$
    $\begin{array}{rcl} & x^2 \\ \hline x-3)\quad x^3 &- 2x^2 &+ 0x &+ 4 \\ x^3 & -3x^2 \\ \end{array}$
    4. Subtract $(2x^3+3x^2-x-2)-(2x^3+2x^2)=x^2-x-2$ $\begin{array}{rcl} & x^2 \\ \hline x-3)\quad x^3 &- 2x^2 &+ 0x &+ 4 \\ x^3 & -3x^2 \\ \hline &+x^2 &+0x\\\end{array}$
    4. Repeat: $\cfrac{x^2}{x}=x$
    $x\cdot(x+1)=x^2+x$
    $(x^2-x-2)-(x^2+x)=-2x-2$    		 $\begin{array}{rcl} & x^2 &+x \\ \hline x-3)\quad x^3 &- 2x^2 &+ 0x &+ 4 \\ x^3 & -3x^2 \\ \hline &+x^2 &+0x \\ &+x^2 &-3x \\ \hline & &+3x &-4x\end{array}$
    5. Repeat: $\cfrac{-2x}{x}=-2$
    $-2\cdot(x+1)=-2x-2$
    $(-2x-2)-(-2x-2)=0$    		 $\begin{array}{rcl} & x^2 &+x &+3 \\ \hline x-3)\quad x^3 &- 2x^2 &+ 0x &+ 4 \\ x^3 & -3x^2 \\ \hline &+x^2 &+0x \\ &+x^2 &-3x \\ \hline & &+3x &-4x \\ & &+3x &-9x \\ \hline & & &+5x \end{array}$
    $\cfrac{2x^3+3x^2-x-2}{x+1}=2x^2+x-2$ $\cfrac{x^3-2x^2+0x+4}{x-3}=x^2+x+3$

    Divisor $Q(x)$ as Monomial in the Division

    • If $Q(x)$ is a monomial, the division can often be simplified by dividing each term of $P(x)$ individually by $Q(x)$.
