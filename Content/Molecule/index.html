    Molecule

    A molecule is a group of two or more atoms that are chemically bonded together in a fixed arrangement. It is the smallest unit of a chemical substance that retains the composition and chemical properties of that substance.

    Composition and Structure

    • Molecules are formed when atoms share electrons through covalent bonds.
    • The atoms in a molecule are arranged in a definite geometric structure, governed by the nature of chemical bonding (bond angles, bond lengths).
    • The overall electrical neutrality of a molecule distinguishes it from ions.

    Compound

    A compound is a substance formed when two or more elements chemically combine in fixed proportions. Examples include water (H₂O) and carbon dioxide (CO₂).

    • Binary Compound
    • Ternary Compound
    • Quaternary Compound

    Types of Molecules

    1. Based on Atomic Composition:
      • Elemental Molecules: Atoms of the same element
        → e.g., O₂, N₂, Cl₂
        These represent pure elements in molecular form.
      • Compound Molecules: Atoms of different elements
        → e.g., H₂O, CO₂, CH₄
        These are chemical compounds.
    2. Based on Number of Atoms:
      • Diatomic Molecules: 2 atoms
        → e.g., O₂, HCl
      • Triatomic Molecules: 3 atoms
        → e.g., H₂O, CO₂
      • Polyatomic Molecules: More than 3 atoms
        → e.g., C₆H₁₂O₆ (glucose), HNO₃
    3. Based on Size and Complexity:
      • Small molecules: Simple molecules like CO₂, NH₃
      • Macromolecules: Large molecules like proteins, DNA, polymers

    Properties of Molecules

    • Exhibit specific physical and chemical properties based on the arrangement of atoms and bonds (e.g., polarity, solubility).
    • Can exist in all states of matter (solid, liquid, gas).
    • Do not carry a net charge, unlike ions.

    Molar Mass

    Molar mass is the mass of one mole, represent also the sum of atomic masses.

    Chemical Bond

    Electrons are involved in chemical reactions, as atoms often interact by transferring or sharing electrons. This defines ionic and covalent bonding.

    Oxide

    An oxide is a binary compound that consists of oxygen combined with another element.

    Examples

    Molecule Composition Type Notes
    O₂ 2 oxygen atoms Elemental Nonpolar, diatomic
    H₂O 2 H + 1 O Compound Polar, bent structure
    CH₄ 1 C + 4 H Compound Nonpolar, tetrahedral
    C₆H₁₂O₆ 6 C, 12 H, 6 O Compound Sugar molecule (glucose)
    NH₃ 1 N + 3 H Compound Polar, trigonal pyramidal
