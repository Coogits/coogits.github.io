    Atmospheric Pressure

    Atmospheric Pressure is the force per unit area exerted by the weight of the air in Earth's atmosphere. It is caused by the gravitational attraction of the planet on the air molecules.

    Formulas

    • At sea level: At sea level, the standard atmospheric pressure is approximately: $$p_0\simeq 101325\;Pa=1\;Atm$$
    • Barometric formula (derived from the Boltzmann distribution): $$\Huge p(h)=p_0e^{\frac{-mgh}{kT}}$$
    • Approximate barometric formula (assume constant density $\rho_0$): $$\Huge p(h)=p_0e^{\frac{-\rho_0g}{p_0}h}$$
      • Limitations: This approximation is only valid for small height variations where the density $\rho$ can be assumed constant. It fails at high altitudes, where air density decreases significantly.

    Symbols

    • $p(h)\quad$ Pressure at height $h$ (in Pascals)
    • $h\quad$ is the height above sea level.
    • $m\quad$ is the molecular mass of the gas,
    • $g\quad$ is the acceleration due to gravity,
    • $T\quad$ is the absolute temperature of the gas (in Kelvin).
    • $k\quad$ Boltzmann constant
    • $p_0\quad$ is the Atmospheric pressure at sea level
    • $\rho_0\quad$ is the air density at sea level,
