    Gravitational Constant

    The gravitational constant G is a fundamental physical constant that quantifies the strength of gravitational attraction between two masses. It appears in Newtonâ€™s law of universal gravitation and determines the magnitude of the gravitational force.

    Symbols

    • $G\quad$ Gravitational Constant (unit: $\cfrac{m^3}{kg\cdot s^2}$)
    • $F\quad$ Gravitational Force (unit: Newton ($N = kg \cdot m/s^2$))
    • $m_1, m_2\quad$ Masses of the two objects (unit: kilogram ($kg$))
    • $r\quad$ Distance between the centers of mass (unit: meter ($m$))

    Formulas

    • Value:
      Its approximate value in SI units is: $$G\simeq (6,674\,30\pm0,000\,15)\times10^{-11}\;\cfrac{m^3}{kg\cdot s^2}$$
    • Definition:
      Mathematically, it is defined by the equation: $$F=G\cfrac{m_1m_2}{r^2}$$
