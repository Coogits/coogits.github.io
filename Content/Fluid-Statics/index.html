    Fluid Statics

    Fluid statics is the branch of fluid mechanics that deals with fluids at rest or in equilibrium under the influence of forces. It focuses on understanding the pressure distribution within a fluid and how fluids behave when there is no motion, typically in the presence of gravity or other external forces.

    Key Concepts

    • Hydrostatic Pressure: The pressure in a fluid increases with depth due to the weight of the fluid above it.
    • Pascal's Principle: The pressure exerted on an enclosed fluid is transmitted equally in all directions.
    • Archimedes' Principle: A body submerged in a fluid experiences an upward buoyant force equal to the weight of the displaced fluid.
    • Communicating Vessels: When fluids are connected in different containers, the height of the fluid will adjust to equalize the pressure at the same level in both vessels.
