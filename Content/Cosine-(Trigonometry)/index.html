    Cosine (Trigonometry)

    The sine function ($\cos$) is a fundamental trigonometric function that relates the angle of a right triangle to the ratio of its sides.

    Symbols

    • $\text{adjacent}\quad$ The side next to the angle $\theta$, excluding the hypotenuse.
    • $\text{hypotenuse}\quad$ The longest side of the right triangle.
    • $x\quad$ horizontal coordinate of the point on the unit circle corresponding to the angle $\theta$.

    Definition

    • Right Triangle Definition:
      For a given angle $\theta$ in a right triangle: $$\cos\theta=\cfrac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$$
    • Unit Circle Definition:
      In the unit circle, where a point $(x, y)$ represents an angle $\theta$ measured from the positive $x$-axis: $$\cos\theta=y$$

    Properties

    • Periodicity: $\quad\cos(\theta+2\pi)=\cos\theta$
    • Odd Function: $\quad\sin(-\theta)=-\sin\theta$
    • Range: $\quad-1\leq\cos(\theta)\leq1$
    • Pythagorean Identity: $$\sin^2\theta-\cos^2\theta=1$$

    Theorems

    • Angle Addition and Subtraction Formulas: $$\cos(\alpha\pm\beta)=\cos\alpha\cos\beta\mp\sin\alpha\sin\beta$$
    • Law of Cosines: $$c^2=a^2+b^2-2ab\cdot\cos\gamma$$
