    Slope (Analytic Geometry)

    The slope of a line is a measure of its steepness, inclination, or direction. It represents the ratio of the vertical change (rise) to the horizontal change (run) between any two points on the line.

    Symbols

    • $y\quad$ vertical value
    • $x\quad$ horizontal value

    Notation and Definitions

    • Ratio Definition:
      The slope is defined as the ratio of the change in $y$ to the change in $x$: $$m=\cfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \cfrac{y_1-y_0}{x_1-x_0}$$
    • Standard form Definition:
      The slope of a line given in Standard Form (Line) $ax+by+c=0$ is: $$m=-\cfrac{a}{b}$$
    • Derivative Definition:
      The slope of a curve at a given point is defined as the Derivative: $$\displaystyle m=\lim_{x\to x_0}\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}=\cfrac{dy}{dx}$$

    Properties

    • Positive Slope:
      The line rises from left to right.
    • Negative Slope:
      The line falls from left to right.
    • Zero Slope:
      The line is horizontal.
    • Undefined Slope:
      The line is vertical (division by zero).

    Interpretation

    • The slope quantifies how much $y$ changes for a unit change in $x$.
    • In real-world contexts, the slope can represent rates of change, such as speed, cost per unit, or gradient of a hill.
